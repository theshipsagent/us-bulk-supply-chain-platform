## I. PROJECT SCOPE & OBJECTIVES

### Primary Objectives
1. **Map Egypt's fertilizer export infrastructure** - Production facilities, port terminals, transportation routes
2. **Analyze Egypt's position in global fertilizer trade** - Market share by commodity and destination region
3. **Profile major Egyptian fertilizer producers** - Fertiglobe, Abu Qir, MOPCO, OCP joint ventures
4. **Visualize global trade flows** - Egypt to key markets (Europe, Africa, Americas, Asia)
5. **Assess competitive position** - Egypt vs other exporters (Russia, Saudi Arabia, Morocco, Trinidad)
6. **Provide strategic outlook** - Production expansions, Suez Canal advantage, regional geopolitics

### Strategic Context
Egypt is positioned as:
- **3rd largest fertilizer supplier to Europe** (after Russia, Algeria)
- **Major nitrogen exporter** - 5.5M MT urea/ammonia capacity, targeting 10M MT by 2030
- **Growing phosphate player** - Leveraging Abu Tartur mega-project (500K MT phosphoric acid by 2028)
- **Strategic location** - Mediterranean + Red Sea ports, Suez Canal corridor
- **Export-driven economy** - USD 2.6B fertilizer exports (2023), targeting USD 10B (2025)

### Geographic Coverage
- **National:** All Egyptian fertilizer production facilities (nitrogen, phosphate)
- **Port Focus:** Alexandria, Damietta, Ain Al-Sokhna, Safaga, El-Quseir
- **Regional Context:** Egypt within Middle East/North Africa fertilizer complex
- **Global Trade Flows:** Egyptian exports to 80+ countries

### Commodity Coverage
- **Nitrogen:**
  - Anhydrous Ammonia (NH3) - 2.4M MT/year capacity
  - Granular Urea (46-0-0) - 4.6M MT/year capacity
  - UAN Solutions (32-0-0) - 325K MT/year
  - Ammonium Nitrate - 1.7M MT/year
- **Phosphate:**
  - Phosphate Rock - 5M MT/year current capacity
  - Phosphoric Acid - 500K MT/year (under development, Abu Tartur project)
  - DAP/MAP - Limited (future expansion potential)

## IX. NOTES & ASSUMPTIONS

**Data Limitations:**
- Egyptian government statistics sometimes lag (2-3 year delay)
- Company data for state-owned firms (MOPCO, KIMA) limited transparency
- Trade flow data relies on importing country statistics (cross-validate Egypt export stats with US/EU/Brazil import stats)

**Comparison to US Fertilizer Report:**
- Egypt report is EXPORT-focused (vs US IMPORT-focused)
- Egypt has fewer but larger facilities (5 nitrogen plants vs 50+ in US)
- Transportation analysis simpler (ocean shipping vs complex inland logistics)
- Geopolitics more prominent (Suez Canal, Red Sea security, sanctions)

**Unique Aspects of Egypt Report:**
- **Suez Canal narrative** - Strategic waterway central to Egypt's advantage
- **Green ammonia focus** - Egypt positioning for low-carbon future
- **Africa hub positioning** - Egypt as fertilizer supplier for sub-Saharan Africa
- **Abu Tartur project** - Transformational investment (rock → phosphoric acid)
