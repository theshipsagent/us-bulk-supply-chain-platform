EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: The US fly ash market faces a structural supply deficit as coal plant closures accelerate (-48% capacity since 2011 peak) while infrastructure demand remains elevated through IIJA spending ($1.2T through 2028). Beneficial use rates reached record 72% in 2024 (10th consecutive year >50%), but total CCP production declined from 80M tons (2022) to 67M tons (2023). Import arbitrage remains attractive: Asian FOB $16-27/MT vs US $118-123/MT. Supply gap projected to widen 2025-2030 as 68.8 GW additional coal capacity retires.

3. SUPPLY-DEMAND GAP ANALYSIS

US Fly Ash Supply-Demand Balance (Conceptual - Million Tons)
Conceptual projection based on coal plant retirements and steady concrete demand from IIJA spending. Gap must be filled by imports or reclaimed ash.

3.1 Supply Gap Drivers

Supply Constraints

  • Coal Retirements: 68.8 GW scheduled 2025-2030
  • Production Decline: 107M tons (2015) → 67M tons (2023)
  • Plant Capacity Factor: Dropped from 67% (2010) to 42% (2023)
  • Regional Concentration: MISO + PJM losing 66% of 2025 retirements

Demand Drivers

  • IIJA Spending: $1.2T through 2028 ($62B/year)
  • Cement Demand: +4.5% annual growth through 2029
  • Low-Carbon Mandates: California CALGreen, NY regulations
  • Record Utilization: 72% beneficial use rate

Reclaimed Ash: PROS

  • Domestic supply as coal plants close
  • Reduces environmental liability
  • Strong market demand at premium prices
  • Supports circular economy goals
  • Long-term contracts available
  • Potential REE recovery in future

Reclaimed Ash: CONS

  • Variable quality - contamination risk
  • High moisture requires drying
  • May not meet ASTM C618 specs
  • Beneficiation costs (LOI reduction)
  • Regulatory uncertainty (EPA CCR)
  • Community opposition/litigation

NET ASSESSMENT: US fly ash market will remain supply-constrained through 2030. Domestic production decline (-33% projected) will outpace demand moderation. Gap to be filled by: (1) increased imports from Asia/Turkey, (2) reclaimed ash from landfills/ponds, (3) alternative SCMs (slag, natural pozzolans). Prices likely to remain elevated ($100-130/MT range) with periodic spikes during supply disruptions.
