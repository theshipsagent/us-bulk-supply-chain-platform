# Florida Cement Market Analysis
## Tampa Bay Region Focus

**Prepared For:** SESCO Cement Corp
**Report Date:** December 16, 2025
**Classification:** Market Intelligence Report

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Florida represents the second-largest cement consuming state in the US and the fourth-largest producing state, making it a critical market for cement distributors. SESCO Cement Corp's new Port Redwing terminal in Gibsonton positions the company to capture significant market share in the Tampa Bay region, which receives approximately 75% of Florida's cement imports.

### Key Findings

**Market Size:**
- Florida consumption: 2nd largest in US (behind Texas)
- Florida production: 4th largest (~10.4 MT/yr from 7 plants)
- Tampa area imports: 6.8 million MT (75% of Florida cement imports)
- US import share: 22% of total consumption (~19.8 MT imports in 2024)

**Supply Landscape:**
- Major producers: Argos USA, CEMEX, Titan America, Ash Grove (CRH)
- Import terminals: Tampa Bay, Port Manatee, Jacksonville, Port Everglades
- Key importers: Martin Marietta (4.1M MT), CEMEX (2.4M MT), Titan (239K MT)

**Market Opportunity:**
- Import prices averaged $79/ton in 2024 (down 6.4% YoY)
- Turkey (10% tariff), Egypt (10% tariff) offer competitive sourcing
- White cement specialty market underserved
- Export opportunity to Caribbean/Central America (68.8% of US cement exports)

**Strategic Recommendations:**
1. Focus on white cement differentiation (higher margins, less competition)
2. Target ready-mix producers (70-75% of market)
3. Build Caribbean re-export capability from Port Redwing
4. Establish hurricane reconstruction supply relationships

### 3.3 Re-Export Strategy

#### Port Redwing Advantages

1. **Location:** Tampa Bay access, I-75/I-4 corridors
2. **Import sources:** Egypt, Turkey (10% tariff)
3. **Export routes:** Caribbean, Central America (Atlantic side)
4. **Transit times:** 5-8 days to Caribbean

#### Operational Model

**Import → Warehouse → Export:**
1. Import bulk/bagged cement from Egypt/Turkey
2. Store at Port Redwing facility
3. Re-package as needed (bulk to bagged)
4. Export to Caribbean destinations

**Target Products:**
- White cement (highest margin)
- Specialty gray cement
- Bagged cement for smaller markets
- Emergency/hurricane reconstruction supply

#### Competitive Position

**vs. Asian Suppliers:**
- Transit: 5-8 days vs. 30-36 days (advantage)
- Quality: US standards vs. variable (advantage)
- Price: Higher FOB but lower landed cost (neutral)
- Service: Flexible volumes, fast response (advantage)

**vs. Regional Producers (DR, Colombia, Panama):**
- Transit: Longer than 1-3 days (disadvantage)
- Capacity: Not constrained (advantage)
- Products: White cement specialty (advantage)
- Markets: Target areas they don't serve (opportunity)

#### Methodology

**Trade Flow Analysis:**
- Analyzed 2,706 US cement import manifests from Panjiva (2023-2025)
- Aggregated by consignee, shipper, port of unlading, and origin country
- Weighted trade flows by MT volume for visualization
- Excluded clinker-only shipments where identifiable

**Market Sizing:**
- Florida production capacity from GEM Cement Tracker plant database
- Import volumes from Panjiva manifest aggregation
- Demand estimated from production + imports - exports
- County-level demand indexed by building permits and population growth

**Trucking Radius Calculation:**
- Origin: Port Redwing terminal, Gibsonton, FL (27.8456°N, 82.3789°W)
- Average trucking speed: 50-55 mph (highway, adjusted for loading/traffic)
- 1-hour zone: ~55 miles radius
- 3-hour zone: ~165 miles radius
- 5-hour zone: ~275 miles radius
- Population data from US Census Bureau estimates

**Freight Rate Analysis:**
- Spot rates from Clarksons daily reports (December 2025)
- Route estimates based on vessel class and distance
- Forward rates from Clarksons FFA data

**Customer Identification:**
- Industry association membership lists (NRMCA, PCI, FCPA)
- Trade publication directories
- Panjiva consignee data for importers
- Public company filings and press releases

#### Data Limitations

| Data Point | Limitation | Mitigation |
|------------|------------|------------|
| White cement volumes | Not separately reported in most sources | Estimated from specialty importer data |
| Terminal capacities | Not publicly disclosed by all operators | Estimated from vessel call patterns |
| Market share by company | Competitive data not publicly available | Estimated from import/production data |
| Customer volumes | Proprietary information | Ranges estimated from industry ratios |
| Trucking zones | Actual drive times vary by route/traffic | Approximate circular zones used |

#### Assumptions

1. **Import price decline:** USGS reported 6.4% YoY decline; assumed to continue into 2025
2. **Tariff rates:** Based on current HTS codes; subject to policy changes
3. **Capacity utilization:** 72% industry average applied to Florida plants
4. **Ready-mix share:** 70-75% of cement consumption goes to ready-mix (industry standard)
5. **White cement premium:** 2-3x gray cement pricing (market observation)
