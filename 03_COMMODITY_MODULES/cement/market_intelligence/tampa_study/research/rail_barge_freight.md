**Compiled:** December 16, 2025
**Sources:** STB, USACE, AAR, BTS

## RAIL FREIGHT - NONMETALLIC MINERALS

### Data Sources
- **Surface Transportation Board (STB):** Freight Commodity Statistics (STCC codes)
- **Association of American Railroads (AAR):** Weekly Rail Traffic Reports
- **BTS Freight Analysis Framework (FAF):** SCTG commodity codes 31/32

### STCC Code Classification
- **STCC 32:** Clay, Concrete, Glass, or Stone Products (includes cement)
- Rail freight by 7-digit STCC available in STB Excel downloads (2006-2025)

**Trend:** Nonmetallic minerals rail traffic was generally DOWN in 2024 vs 2023, though recovered toward year-end.

### 2024 Cumulative Rail Traffic
- **Total carloads (52 weeks):** 11,336,412 (down 2.9% YoY)
- **Intermodal units:** 13,842,174 (up 9.3% YoY)
- **Nonmetallic minerals** is one of 20 commodity categories tracked

### Key Rail-Served Cement Distribution
- **GCC of America:** 23 rail-served cement distribution terminals, 2,800 leased railcars
- Most cement plants located close to limestone deposits with rail access
- Rail efficient for long-haul bulk cement distribution

## INLAND BARGE - WATERBORNE COMMERCE

### Data Sources
- **USACE WCSC:** Waterborne Commerce Statistics Center (New Orleans)
- **Navigation Data Center (NDC):** Lock performance data
- **BTS:** Inland Waterborne Traffic statistics

### Mississippi River System

**Total Barge Traffic:**
- **2023:** 449 million short tons (down 3.3% or 15M tons from 2022)
- **2022:** ~464 million tons estimated
- **2021:** ~500 million tons

**Commodity Breakdown (Upper Mississippi):**
- Grain (corn, soybeans, wheat): ~60% of tonnage
- Other dry bulk: fertilizer, cement, sand/gravel, salt, coal, steel, scrap metals

**Direction of Flow:**
- **Downbound:** Grain, coal dominant
- **Upbound:** Fertilizer and cement (for spring planting/construction)

### Barge Fleet Statistics (2023)
- **Total barges:** 22,356 (up 1% or 224 barges)
- **Open barges:** 5,088 (up 27)
- **Covered barges:** 13,321 (historic high, up 98)
- **Fleet age:** 22% of fleet 25+ years old (barge-building slowed since 2015)

### Cement Barge Characteristics
- **Typical load:** 1,500-ton containers per barge
- **Coverage:** Covered barges needed for cement (moisture protection)
- **Cost advantage:** Lowest shipment cost vs truck and rail for river routes
- **Loading:** Wharf differential pressure loading systems common

### Gulf Coast Cement by Barge
- Major Gulf Coast states (LA, TX, MS, AL, GA, FL) import cement from:
  - Ohio River production
  - Upper Mississippi River production
  - Ocean imports from Mexico and worldwide
- Cement shortages in Gulf Coast driven by:
  - Hurricane repair/remediation demand
  - Rapid construction growth in Southeast US

### Data Access
- **WCUS Reports:** https://publibrary.planusace.us/#/series/Waterborne%20Commerce%20of%20the%20United%20States
- **WCSC Data Portal:** https://ndc.ops.usace.army.mil/wcsc/webpub/
- Tonnage in short tons (2,000 lbs)
- 2023 data published as of late 2024

## BTS FREIGHT ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK (FAF)

### Overview
- 42 commodity types by origin, destination, mode
- SCTG 31: Nonmetallic minerals (includes cement)
- SCTG 32: Base metals

### FAF5 Data Available
- **FAF 5.7.1:** Current version (2018-2023 estimates)
- **FAF6:** Benchmark year 2022 (coming)
- County-level experimental data released Jan 2025

### Data Dimensions
- Weight: thousands of tons
- Value: millions of 2017 constant dollars
- Activity: millions of ton-miles

### Access
- Download .csv and Microsoft Access files from https://www.bts.gov/faf
- Contact: faf@dot.gov

## FLORIDA-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Rail Access
- **CSX:** Major Class I railroad serving Florida
- Tampa has direct CSX rail yard connection
- Lines to Jacksonville, Orlando, Fort Lauderdale, Tallahassee

### Waterway Access
- **No navigable inland waterways** connecting Tampa to Mississippi/Ohio River systems
- Florida cement primarily moves by:
  - Ocean vessel (imports)
  - Truck (local distribution)
  - Rail (longer-haul domestic)

### Port Tampa Bay
- Cement terminals receive ocean imports
- No barge transshipment from inland waterways
- Rail connection for onward distribution
