## GRAY CEMENT PROSPECTS

### Tier 1: Independent Ready-Mix Producers

These companies are NOT vertically integrated with cement producers, making them ideal targets for alternative supply.

**Target Profile:**
- Not owned by Argos, CEMEX, or Titan
- Annual cement consumption: 10,000+ tons
- Multiple plants or growing operations
- Price-sensitive but value reliability

#### Prospect List

| Company | Locations | Est. Volume | Priority |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Independent Concrete Companies | Tampa Bay | Varies | Research |
| Regional Ready-Mix Operators | Central FL | Varies | Research |
| Growing Concrete Producers | Expansion mode | Varies | High |

**Approach:**
1. Identify through industry directories (NRMCA members)
2. Contact purchasing managers
3. Offer competitive pricing + supply reliability
4. Start with spot orders, build to supply agreements

## MARKET ENTRY STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Immediate (0-6 Months)

**Gray Cement:**
1. Identify 10-15 independent ready-mix producers
2. Develop pricing model (competitive with current suppliers)
3. Secure 3-5 initial customers
4. Target: 25,000-50,000 tons Year 1

**White Cement:**
1. Contact 3-5 stucco/EIFS manufacturers
2. Sample program for quality approval
3. Secure 1-2 initial customers
4. Target: 5,000-10,000 tons Year 1

### Phase 2: Growth (6-18 Months)

**Gray Cement:**
1. Expand to concrete products manufacturers
2. Qualify for contractor bid lists
3. Target: 75,000-100,000 tons Year 2

**White Cement:**
1. Expand to tile/mortar producers
2. Develop architectural precast relationships
3. Target: 15,000-25,000 tons Year 2

### Phase 3: Maturity (18-36 Months)

**Gray Cement:**
1. Market share target: 5-10% of Tampa Bay
2. Long-term supply agreements
3. Target: 150,000+ tons Year 3

**White Cement:**
1. Market share target: 15-20% of Florida white cement
2. Specialty product leadership
3. Target: 40,000-50,000 tons Year 3

## COMPETITIVE RESPONSE

### Expected Competitor Reactions

**Argos USA (Tampa plant owner):**
- May offer price reductions to retain customers
- Emphasize local production advantage
- Highlight supply reliability

**Response:** Focus on customers dissatisfied with integrated supplier dynamics, offer genuine supply diversification

**Martin Marietta (largest importer):**
- Price competition on gray cement
- Bundled aggregates + cement offers

**Response:** Compete on service, flexibility; don't try to match aggregates bundling

**CEMEX (Brooksville plant):**
- Strong distribution network
- Integrated ready-mix pressure

**Response:** Target non-integrated customers, emphasize independence

### Differentiation Factors

1. **White cement specialty** - competitors have limited focus
2. **Egyptian/Turkish sourcing** - favorable tariff position
3. **Port Redwing location** - new, modern facility
4. **Caribbean export capability** - unique positioning
5. **Customer service focus** - not integrated competitor
