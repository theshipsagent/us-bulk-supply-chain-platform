Market Fundamentals: Why Tampa Bay?

Structural Supply Gap: Florida consumes 14M MT annually but produces only 7.5M MT domestically. The 6.5M MT gap (46% import dependency vs. 22% US average) must be filled through imports—a durable, growing opportunity.
Tampa Bay = Import Gateway: 75% of Florida's cement imports flow through Tampa Bay ports (6.8M MT annually). Superior infrastructure, I-75/I-4 corridor access, and central positioning make Tampa the state's cement distribution hub.
Market Growth Drivers: Florida population growing 1.5-2.0% annually (vs 0.7% US average) • Hurricane building codes drive cement-intensive construction • No new domestic capacity planned (environmental constraints) • Import dependency projected to widen to 7.5-8.5M MT by 2030

SESCO's Competitive Advantages

✓ Favorable Tariff Position
Egyptian/Turkish sources at 10% tariff vs. 25-46% for competitors (Canada, Mexico, Vietnam) = $12-27/MT cost advantage
✓ White Cement Specialty
2-3x price premium ($225/MT vs $90/MT gray) • 45-65% gross margins • Only 1 US producer • Florida demand 42-55K MT/year driven by stucco/EIFS building codes
✓ Port Redwing Infrastructure Scale
150,000 MT storage capacity (vs. 30-60K typical) • I-75/I-4 direct access • 1-hour delivery to 3.4M population • 3-hour reach to 12.8M (Orlando, SW Florida)

Top Risks & Mitigation

  • Port Redwing Operations: Equipment redundancy, experienced team, rigorous commissioning
  • Competitive Price War: Target independents, white cement focus, value over price positioning
  • Supply Chain Disruption: Multiple suppliers (Egypt + Turkey), 60-90 day inventory buffers
  • Demand Downturn: Geographic/product diversification, adequate capitalization ($12-15M total)
  • Tariff Changes: Diversified sourcing, pass-through clauses, competitive even at 20% tariffs
