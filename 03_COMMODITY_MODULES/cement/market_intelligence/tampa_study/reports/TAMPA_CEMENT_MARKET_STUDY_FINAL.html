PART I: THE OPPORTUNITY

Why Florida? Why Tampa Bay? Why Now?

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Market Opportunity Overview

The Tampa Bay cement market represents a strategic entry point into the second-largest cement-consuming state in the United States. With Florida's robust population growth, stringent hurricane-resistant construction codes, and position as a major import gateway, the Tampa region offers SESCO Cement Corp a unique opportunity to establish significant market presence in both domestic distribution and Caribbean re-export markets.

1.1 Market Size & Position

Metric Value Significance
Florida Consumption Rank 2nd largest (after Texas) ~14 million MT/year
Florida Production Rank 4th largest producer ~10.4 million MT/year capacity
Tampa Bay Import Share 75% of Florida imports 6.8 million MT annually
US Import Dependence 22% of total consumption ~19.8 million MT in 2024

Strategic Position: SESCO's Port Redwing terminal in Gibsonton, Florida, positions the company at the epicenter of Florida's cement import infrastructure with immediate access to 3.4 million people within 1-hour trucking radius and same-day delivery capability to 12.8 million people.

1.2 Competitive Advantages

Favorable Tariff Position

Source SESCO Position Competitor Sources Cost Advantage
Egypt/Turkey 10% tariff Canada (25%), Mexico (25%), Vietnam (46%) $12-27/MT advantage

Product Differentiation

Product Price Range Margin Competition Level
Gray Cement $79-95/MT 15-25% High
White Cement $180-280/MT 45-65% Low (single US producer)

White Cement Opportunity: 2-3x price premium with limited competition. Florida building codes mandating stucco/EIFS for hurricane resistance create sustained demand.

1.3 Financial Opportunity

Year 1-3 Volume Targets

Product Year 1 Year 2 Year 3
Gray Cement 25-50K MT 75-100K MT 150K MT
White Cement 5-10K MT 15-25K MT 40-50K MT
Total Volume 30-60K MT 90-125K MT 190-200K MT

Market Share Objectives

  • Tampa Bay gray cement: 2-3% Year 1 → 5-10% Year 3
  • Florida white cement: 5-8% Year 1 → 15-20% Year 3
  • Caribbean export: Establish presence → Build volume

Year 3 Revenue Potential: $24 million annually (Gray: $13.5M + White: $10.5M)

1.4 Key Success Factors

Critical Dependencies

  1. Reliable sourcing from Egypt and Turkey suppliers
  2. Port Redwing operational readiness and logistics efficiency
  3. Customer acquisition in independent ready-mix and stucco/EIFS segments
  4. Quality consistency maintaining competitive product specifications
  5. Market timing capitalizing on current import price softness (down 6.4% YoY)

Risk Mitigation

Risk Area Mitigation Strategy
Supplier diversification Multiple sources (Royal, Cleopatra, Nuh Cement)
Customer diversification No single customer >25% revenue
Market diversification Domestic + export operations
Product diversification Gray + white cement portfolio

1.5 Strategic Recommendations

Immediate Priorities:

  1. Lead with white cement - Highest margins, least competitive resistance
  2. Target stucco/EIFS manufacturers - High volume, consistent demand, Florida-specific need
  3. Build independent ready-mix relationships - Volume builder for gray cement
  4. Establish Caribbean export capability - Leverage proximity and white cement premium
  5. Develop technical support differentiation - Move beyond commodity positioning

Market Entry Sequence

Phase Timeline Focus Target Volume
Phase 1 Months 1-6 Tampa Bay 1-hour radius, secure initial customers 30-60K MT
Phase 2 Months 6-18 I-4 corridor expansion, volume scaling 90-125K MT
Phase 3 Months 18-36 Statewide presence, Caribbean export program 190-200K MT
