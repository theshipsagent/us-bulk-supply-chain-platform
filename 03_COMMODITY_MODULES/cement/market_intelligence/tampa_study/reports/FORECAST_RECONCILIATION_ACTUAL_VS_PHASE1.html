II. Critical Strategic Implications

⚠️ CRITICAL CHANGE #1: SRM/Slag is the Core Business, Not Gray Cement

Phase 1 Missed: Entire Phase 1 study analyzed Tampa Bay as gray cement import opportunity serving ready-mix market. SRM (Supplementary Raw Materials, likely slag cement/fly ash) was not mentioned once in 50+ page report.

Actual Reality:

  • 300,000 MT SRM in 2026 (81% of total volume, 38% of revenue)
  • 400,000 MT SRM full year (69% of volume, 25% of revenue)
  • At $25/MT, this is high-volume, low-margin commodity business
  • Dedicated slag silos (2 silos) with 100K MT customer in discussions
  • "Put more weight to Slag" - this is strategic priority

What This Means:

  • Port Redwing must handle SRM/slag products - infrastructure requirements different than pure cement terminal
  • Customer base is concrete producers needing SCMs (Supplementary Cementitious Materials), not just ready-mix buying portland cement
  • Competitive dynamics different - slag suppliers, fly ash distributors, not just cement importers
  • Margin profile lower - $25/MT SRM vs. $90-230/MT cement requires volume scale
  • Phase 1 financial model invalid - cost structure, margin assumptions, profitability timeline all based on wrong product mix
⚠️ CRITICAL CHANGE #2: Gray Cement Severely Restricted - Cannot Sell to Florida Ready-Mix

Phase 1 Strategy: Primary gray cement target was independent ready-mix producers in Tampa Bay. Year 1 goal: 15-20 ready-mix accounts. Entire customer segmentation built around this.

Actual Restriction: "We have a restriction per the SRM concrete which doesn't allow us to sell gray cement to the ready-mix industry in Florida, so gray sales will be conservative."

Impact:

  • Phase 1 Tier 1-B customer segment (Independent Ready-Mix) is UNAVAILABLE
  • Gray cement limited to: concrete products manufacturers (block, precast, pipe), contractors direct, bagged sales, out-of-state markets
  • 50,000 MT Year 1 vs. Phase 1's 33,000 MT - but growing only to 150K MT full year vs. Phase 1's 150K MT Year 3
  • Higher pricing ($130-145/MT vs. $90/MT) suggests non-ready-mix markets command premium (block plants, specialty applications)
  • Entire Phase 1 go-to-market strategy for gray cement requires rewrite

Question Requiring Clarification: What is the "SRM concrete restriction"? Is this:

  • Contractual restriction with SRM supplier? (Non-compete in ready-mix market?)
  • Product specification issue? (SRM concrete incompatible with certain applications?)
  • Regulatory/certification restriction?
  • Understanding this restriction is critical for refining strategy
