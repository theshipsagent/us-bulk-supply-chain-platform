Phase 1 market study completed comprehensive analysis of Tampa Bay cement opportunity. Recommend Phase 2 validation study focused on actual market consumption patterns, CMU block opportunity quantification, and distributor channel analysis.

I. Stakeholder Feedback Summary

Key Observations from Market Review

🔴 CRITICAL: Traditional Stucco Market Deterioration

Stakeholder Input: "A long time ago FL was a huge Stucco market, houses were constructed with CMU Block and parged (coated) with white cement (a blend of White Masonry & Portland bags). My understanding is this market has deteriorated significantly; we will need to quantify."

Impact on Study: Phase 1 study assumed stucco/EIFS represents 60-65% of Florida white cement demand (28-35K MT/year) and positioned this as primary white cement opportunity. If traditional white cement stucco has largely disappeared, our white cement volume projections (46K MT Year 3) are likely overstated by 40-60%.

🔴 CRITICAL: EIFS Does Not Drive Bulk Cement Demand

Stakeholder Input: "EIFS stucco helps us very little on the bag side."

Impact on Study: Phase 1 grouped EIFS with traditional stucco as white cement driver. If EIFS uses pre-mixed products rather than bulk cement, this eliminates a major assumed customer segment (Dryvit, Sto, BASF Wall Systems) from our targeting strategy.

⚠️ Federal White Cement Market Withdrawal

Stakeholder Input: "If Federal continues to reduce their presence in the US it will definitely have a big impact."

Opportunity: Federal White is a major US white cement supplier. Their market withdrawal could create immediate capture opportunity for SESCO. Phase 1 did not identify this competitive development. Requires investigation of Federal's customer base and volume being released to market.

✓ VALIDATED: Canadian Tariff Impact

Stakeholder Input: "Obviously the Canadian Tariffs will play a big part."

Phase 1 Alignment: Study correctly identified 25% Canadian tariff vs. 10% Egyptian/Turkish rates as $12-15/MT cost advantage. This feedback confirms materiality to competitive dynamics.

⚠️ CMU Block Market Underanalyzed

Stakeholder Input: "There appears to be an abundant amount of Brick and block being used in C.FL. A broad swipe looks like 15 million bricks in C.FL. and another 20 mil. in Jacksonville. CMU block approx. 30 mil. in C.FL."

Impact on Study: Phase 1 mentioned CMU construction but did not quantify cement demand from block manufacturing. 30 million CMU blocks ≈ 270,000 MT cement annually in Central Florida alone—exceeding our entire Year 3 volume target. This represents potentially larger opportunity than white cement.

⚠️ Distributor Channel Not Analyzed

Stakeholder Input: "We will need some further understanding about the distributor Market in FL."

Gap in Study: Phase 1 focused on bulk cement terminal-to-customer model. Did not analyze bagged cement distributor channel, which appears significant for white cement and masonry applications. Port Redwing bagging capability and distributor economics not assessed.

Financial Model Sensitivity to White Cement Assumption

White cement represents 43% of Year 3 revenue but only 23% of volume in Phase 1 projections. If actual white cement opportunity is 50% lower than modeled:

  • Revenue shortfall: $5.1M in Year 3 (21% below target)
  • Profitability delay: EBITDA breakeven pushed from Year 3 to Year 4-5
  • Capital requirement increase: Additional $4-5M to fund extended investment phase
  • Strategic implications: Must compensate with higher gray cement volumes (requires more customer acquisitions, potentially lower margins if competitive pricing required)

Upside Scenarios: CMU Block Opportunity

Potential Offsetting Opportunity: CMU Block Manufacturing

Quick Calculation: 30 million CMU blocks in Central Florida annually

  • Standard 8"×8"×16" CMU block: ~40 lbs, approximately 50% cement content
  • Cement per block: ~20 lbs (0.009 MT)
  • 30M blocks × 0.009 MT = 270,000 MT cement annually
  • At 10-15% market share: 27-40K MT potential volume
  • At $90/MT gray cement pricing: $2.4-3.6M annual revenue

Strategic Implication: CMU block plant supply could partially or fully offset white cement shortfall if validated. Requires identifying block manufacturers, assessing current suppliers, and understanding competitive dynamics in this segment.

IV. Phase 2 Validation Requirements

Critical Validation Studies (Pre-Investment Decision)

RECOMMENDATION: Phase 2 Market Validation Required Before Go/No-Go Decision

Phase 1 study provides strong strategic framework but identified assumptions require field validation before committing capital. Do not proceed to Board approval or capital commitment until completing Phase 2 validation studies addressing white cement market reality, CMU block opportunity quantification, and distributor channel economics.

Phase 2 Study 1: White Cement Actual Market Validation (Timeline: 4-6 weeks)

Objective: Establish actual Florida white cement consumption, identify current consumers, and assess SESCO's realistic capture opportunity.

Research Activities:

  1. Customer Interviews (10-15 interviews):
    • Traditional stucco contractors: Is white cement stucco still used? Volume estimates?
    • EIFS manufacturers (Dryvit, Sto, BASF): Do they purchase bulk cement or use pre-mixed products?
    • Pool plaster manufacturers: Annual white cement consumption?
    • Tile adhesive/grout producers (Mapei, Laticrete): White cement volumes purchased?
    • Architectural precast: White cement usage patterns?
  2. Competitive Intelligence:
    • Federal White Cement: Confirm market withdrawal timeline, identify customer list if possible
    • Lehigh White Cement: Florida sales volumes (via distributor interviews)
    • Import data analysis: Actual white cement import volumes to Florida ports (Panjiva deep dive)
  3. Building Code & Construction Method Analysis:
    • Florida Building Commission: Current code requirements for exterior finishing
    • Builders/contractors: What replaced traditional white cement stucco?
    • Construction volume analysis: Housing starts × % using various exterior systems

Deliverable: Revised Florida white cement consumption estimate with +/- 20% confidence interval. Customer target list with validated volume potential. Updated Year 1-3 projections.

Phase 2 Study 2: CMU Block & Masonry Market Quantification (Timeline: 3-4 weeks)

Objective: Quantify cement demand from CMU block manufacturing and assess SESCO's market entry opportunity in this segment.

Research Activities:

  1. Block Plant Census:
    • Identify all CMU block manufacturers in Central Florida, Tampa Bay, Jacksonville
    • Production capacity and actual output estimates
    • Block types produced (standard, architectural, specialty)
  2. Cement Consumption Analysis:
    • Cement content by block type (standard vs. lightweight vs. architectural)
    • Total annual cement consumption by plant
    • Aggregate Central Florida market size
  3. Competitive Supply Analysis:
    • Current cement suppliers to block plants (integrated producers vs. importers)
    • Supply agreements and pricing structures
    • Switching potential and barriers
  4. Brick Market Assessment:
    • 15M bricks Central Florida, 20M Jacksonville: Validate volume estimates
    • Cement content in brick production vs. CMU
    • Market opportunity sizing

Deliverable: CMU block cement demand quantification (total market + SESCO addressable). Target customer list with volume estimates. Competitive positioning assessment. Revenue/volume impact on financial model.

Phase 2 Study 3: Distributor Channel Analysis (Timeline: 3-4 weeks)

Objective: Understand bagged cement distribution economics, map distributor network, and assess Port Redwing's capability to serve this channel.

Research Activities:

  1. Distributor Network Mapping:
    • Identify major Florida cement distributors (building materials distributors, masonry suppliers)
    • Geographic coverage and market positioning
    • Product mix (bags vs. bulk, gray vs. white, brands carried)
  2. Bag vs. Bulk Market Split:
    • Florida cement consumption: What % moves as bags vs. bulk?
    • White cement specifically: Bag dominance in this segment?
    • Customer preference drivers (contractors, small projects, specialty applications)
  3. Economics & Margin Structure:
    • Distributor margin expectations (% markup on bulk cement)
    • Bagging costs and facility requirements
    • Pricing comparison: bulk terminal vs. bagged distributor delivered
  4. Port Redwing Bagging Assessment:
    • Current bagging capability at Port Redwing (equipment, capacity)
    • Capital requirement if bagging not currently available
    • Throughput economics (MT/hour, cost per bag)

Deliverable: Distributor channel opportunity assessment. Bag vs. bulk strategy recommendation. Port Redwing bagging business case (if applicable). Updated go-to-market strategy including distributor channel.

Phase 2 Study 4: Housing Starts & Construction Trend Correlation (Timeline: 2 weeks)

Objective: Validate demand projections through actual construction data and trend analysis.

Research Activities:

  1. Building Permit Analysis:
    • Central Florida, Tampa Bay, Jacksonville housing starts (residential + commercial)
    • Breakdown by building type (single-family, multifamily, commercial)
    • 5-year trend analysis and forward projections
  2. Cement Consumption Coefficients:
    • Cement consumption per housing unit by construction type
    • CMU construction prevalence vs. wood frame
    • Commercial project cement intensity
  3. Construction Method Evolution:
    • Trend in CMU vs. wood frame residential construction
    • Stucco vs. alternative exterior systems adoption
    • Impact on cement demand per unit

Deliverable: Bottom-up cement demand forecast based on actual construction activity. Validation of Phase 1 2-3% annual growth assumption. Sensitivity scenarios for construction cycle variations.
