📊 SUMMARY: Critical Business Model Clarifications

Phase 1 market study provides valid strategic framework (Tampa Bay gateway, tariff advantage, Port Redwing positioning) but requires clarification on actual business model. Core business is SRM partner throughput (not SESCO product), white cement opportunity validated and exceeds Phase 1, and gray cement restricted from Florida ready-mix per SRM partnership agreement. Key clarifications below align Phase 1 analysis with actual go-to-market strategy.

I. SRM Partnership Clarification

⚠️ CLARIFICATION #1: SRM is Partner/Customer, Not SESCO Product Line
(RVD): "SRM is a partner that has throughput agreement, so is a customer of the terminal but there market share agreement, so to clarify, this means by contract, can't market to the ready-mix"

What This Means:

  • SRM is a customer of Port Redwing terminal, not a SESCO product line or revenue source
  • 300-400K MT represents SRM's throughput volume through the facility (partner's business, not SESCO's)
  • Market share agreement restricts SESCO from selling gray cement to Florida ready-mix industry
  • SRM relationship provides terminal throughput/utilization but revenue comes from handling fees, not cement sales

Impact on Phase 1 Analysis:

  • Phase 1's primary gray cement customer target (independent ready-mix producers, Tier 1-B in study) is contractually unavailable
  • Gray cement strategy must focus on: CMU block plants, precast manufacturers, bagged distribution, contractors, out-of-state ready-mix
  • Financial model needs revision: Terminal revenue model (throughput fees) vs. commodity sales model

II. Slag Business Opportunity

⚠️ CLARIFICATION #2: Slag as Strategic Product Line (Not Analyzed in Phase 1)
(AEE): "We are planning to dedicate two silos to slag – there are preliminary discussions with one customer which would purchase ~100k per year. Pricing still unknown. I would put more weight to Slag"

What This Means:

  • 2 dedicated silos at Port Redwing = significant infrastructure commitment to slag product line
  • 100K MT/year customer in discussions = single anchor customer opportunity
  • "Put more weight to Slag" = strategic priority for business development
  • Pricing still unknown = need to establish market rates and margin profile

Phase 1 Gap:

  • Slag not mentioned once in Phase 1 study (zero competitive analysis, customer targeting, or market sizing)
  • Need to add: Slag supplier landscape, customer requirements, competitive positioning, margin structure
  • Port operations assessment required: Slag handling capabilities, grinding requirements (if any), quality control

III. White Cement Strategy - Validated and Enhanced

✓ VALIDATED: White Cement Opportunity Confirmed - Exceeds Phase 1 Estimates
(AEE): "Also, the plan is to push more white cement sales. We know that one of the Mapei plants alone consumes 30k tpa, which we plan to supply. I would push white cement in 2027 to at least 60-75k tons."

What This Validates:

  • Mapei anchor customer confirmed: 30K MT/year consumption (single customer validates Phase 1 market sizing)
  • 2027 target 60-75K MT exceeds Phase 1 Year 3 estimate of 46K MT
  • Strategic emphasis on white cement ("plan to push more white cement sales") confirms Phase 1 specialty product focus

Addressing Phase 1 Concerns:

(BH): "A long time ago FL was a huge Stucco market, houses were constructed with CMU Block and parged (coated) with white cement (a blend of White Masonry & Portland bags). My understanding is this market has deteriorated significantly; we will need to quantify. EIFS stucco helps us very little on the bag side."

Clarification on Customer Targeting:

  • Phase 1 incorrectly emphasized stucco/EIFS manufacturers as primary white cement target
  • Actual driver: Tile adhesive/grout manufacturers (Mapei confirms this)
  • Traditional white cement stucco market has deteriorated (not viable target)
  • EIFS uses pre-mixed products (doesn't drive bulk cement demand)
  • Revised white cement targets: Tile adhesive producers (Mapei anchor), pool plaster, architectural precast, specialty applications
✓ Additional White Cement Opportunity: Federal White Market Withdrawal
(BH): "If Federal continues to reduce there presence in the US it will definitely have a big impact."
(MS): "I'm hearing that Federal White is reducing their presence in USA market"

Strategic Opportunity:

  • Federal White Cement is major US supplier - market withdrawal creates capture opportunity
  • Phase 1 did not identify this competitive development
  • Action: Identify Federal White customer list, assess volume being released, develop capture strategy

IV. Gray Cement Market - Restricted But Viable

⚠️ CLARIFICATION #3: Gray Cement Restriction Scope and Alternative Markets
(AEE): "We have a restriction per the SRM concrete which doesn't allow us to sell gray cement to the ready-mix industry in Florida, so gray sales will be conservative."
(MS): "Our contract with SRM needs to be reviewed to know what restrictions we have pertaining to selling gray cement."

What This Means:

  • Contractual restriction from SRM partnership prohibits marketing to Florida ready-mix industry
  • Gray cement volumes will be "conservative" (50-150K MT) vs. Phase 1's ready-mix focus (150K MT Year 3)
  • Need to clarify exact scope: All Florida? Just ready-mix? Geographic boundaries?

Alternative Gray Cement Markets (Not Restricted):

(BH): "There appears to be an abundant amount of Brick and block being used in C.FL. A broad swipe looks like 15 million bricks in C.FL. and another 20 mil. In Jacksonville. CMU block approx. 30mil. In C.FL."

CMU Block Opportunity:

  • 30 million CMU blocks in Central Florida ≈ 270,000 MT cement demand annually
  • Phase 1 mentioned CMU construction but did not quantify cement consumption
  • At 10-15% market share: 27-40K MT potential (meaningful volume within restrictions)
  • Target customers: Block manufacturers, not restricted by SRM ready-mix agreement

Other Non-Ready-Mix Targets:

  • Precast concrete manufacturers
  • Bagged cement distributors
  • Direct contractor sales
  • Out-of-state ready-mix (Georgia, Alabama - if geographically outside restriction)
