Gulf Coast Import Infrastructure

Houston Ship Channel

Houston processes approximately 5.2 million tons annually (20% of total US cement imports), making it the largest cement import gateway in North America.

  • SESCO: World's largest privately-owned single-user cement terminal (150,000 ton capacity)
  • CEMEX: Multi-modal terminal serving TX and OK markets
  • Buzzi Unicem: Integrated terminal supporting Houston grinding facility
  • Holcim US: Regional distribution hub for bagged and bulk cement

Louisiana Ports

Lake Charles: Handles ~3% of US imports (780,000 tons), primarily serving Louisiana and Eastern Texas markets.

Lower Mississippi River System: Collectively handles ~18% of US imports (4.7 million tons) through New Orleans, Baton Rouge, and upstream terminals.

Import Pattern Analysis: Total US cement imports have stabilized around 26-28 million tons annually, with Houston's share growing from 17% in 2020 to 20% in 2025, reflecting supply chain concentration in the Gulf Coast.

Baltic Dry Index & Freight Market Analysis

Current Shipping Conditions

  • Overall BDI: 2,038 points (Aug 11, 2025) - down 21% from March highs
  • Supramax Segment: Most relevant for cement shipping, 30% of BDI weighting
  • Key Routes: Mediterranean to US Gulf significantly impacted

Freight Rate Impact

Supramax freight rates have declined to 12-month lows, reducing shipping costs by approximately $8-12 per ton for Mediterranean-Gulf routes.

Supplementary Cementitious Materials (SCM) Market

Market Overview

The SCM market represents a $28.1 billion global opportunity in 2024, projected to reach $40 billion by 2032. The US Gulf Coast faces unique supply dynamics:

Domestic SCM Sources

Fly Ash Supply Constraints:

  • Coal plant closures in Washington and Oregon by 2025
  • Texas maintains capacity at W.A. Parish, Limestone, Martin Lake
  • Louisiana sources from Big Cajun II and Rodemacher
  • Transport costs increasing due to longer distances

Slag Cement Production:

  • Texas: No major blast furnace operations
  • Louisiana: Nucor's Convent facility (limited volumes)
  • Import dependency from Great Lakes region
  • Foreign sources: Turkey, Brazil, Japan

Industry Consolidation & M&A Activity

Eagle Materials - Kosmos Cement ($665M, 2024)

Eagle Materials acquired Louisville-based Kosmos Cement, adding strategic import terminal capabilities and expanding geographic reach into Ohio River valley markets.

Buzzi Unicem Consolidation

Buzzi continues strategic consolidation with recent acquisitions in Brazil and Italy, while strengthening US Gulf operations through technology investments.

Industry Trend: Consolidation continues as companies seek vertical integration, terminal control, and sustainable material portfolios. Gulf Coast terminals represent strategic assets in this environment.

  • Develop alternative supply relationships

For Cement Consumers

  • Secure long-term supply agreements given market volatility
  • Evaluate SCM usage to optimize concrete performance
  • Monitor Baltic freight rates for procurement timing
  • Develop contingency plans for supply disruptions

Conclusion

The US Gulf Coast cement market operates in an increasingly complex environment shaped by trade policy, supply chain restructuring, and evolving material specifications. While current tariff policies create advantages for certain suppliers, the market faces ongoing volatility from multiple factors including freight costs, environmental regulations, and weather events.

The transition in SCM markets from traditional fly ash and slag to alternative materials represents both challenge and opportunity. Companies that successfully navigate this transition while maintaining supply chain flexibility will be best positioned for long-term success.

Houston's position as the dominant import gateway appears secure, supported by infrastructure investments and strategic location. However, continued monitoring of trade policies, freight markets, and competitive dynamics remains essential for market participants.
