## Facility Type Taxonomy

### Country Elevators
- **Function:** Receive grain by truck directly from farms during harvest
- **Scale:** Smaller than terminal elevators
- **Operations:** May clean or dry grain before transporting to terminal elevators or processors
- **Era:** Traditional design (enclosed headhouse/gallery) vs. modern open-structure design
- **NAICS:** 115114 (Postharvest Crop Activities), 424510 (Grain Merchant Wholesalers)

### Terminal Elevators (Inland)
- **Function:** Dry, clean, blend, and store grain before shipment to other terminals or processors
- **Scale:** Larger grain handling and storage than country elevators
- **Inputs:** Truck, rail, or barge
- **Outputs:** Ship to other terminals, processors, or export elevators
- **NAICS:** 493130 (Farm Product Warehousing and Storage)

### Export Elevators
- **Function:** Load grain primarily onto ships for export
- **Location:** Seaports (Gulf, PNW, Great Lakes, Atlantic)
- **Inputs:** Rail or barge (aggregated from inland terminal elevators)
- **Outputs:** Ocean bulk vessels (Panamax, Supramax, Handysize)
- **NAICS:** 488320 (Marine Cargo Handling), 493130

## Grain Elevator Process Flow (Figure 9.9.1-1)

```
Truck/Rail Receiving → Conveyor → Boot → Receiving Leg → Headhouse
                                                              ↓
Barge/Ship Receiving → Conveyor → Boot              Distributor
                                                              ↓
                                          ┌──────────────────────────────┐
                                          │ Grain Cleaner  Gallery Belt  │
                                          │ Interstice Bin Garner Scale  │
                                          │       Tripper                 │
                                          └──────────────────────────────┘
                                                              ↓
                                                       Storage Bins
                                                              ↓
                                            Truck/Rail Shipping  OR  Barge/Ship Shipping
```

**Optional components:** Intermediate Storage Bin, Grain Dryer (VOC emission source)

**PM/PM-10 emission sources (•):** Truck/rail receiving, barge/ship receiving, conveyor, receiving leg, grain cleaner, storage bin vents, shipping operations

**VOC emission sources (▲):** Grain dryer

## Grain Processing Operations Covered

### 9.9.1.1.1 Flour Milling (Wheat)
5-step process:
1. Grain reception, preliminary cleaning, and storage
2. Grain cleaning (screens, aspirator, magnetic separator, disc separator, stoner, scourer)
3. Tempering/conditioning (moisture raising to 15-16%)
4. Milling (break rolls → sifter → purifier → reducing rolls)
5. Storage and bagging of finished flour

**Products:** Flour, bran, middlings, germ
**NAICS:** 311211

### 9.9.1.1.2 Oat Milling
7-step process: reception → cleaning → drying/cooling → grading/hulling → cutting → steaming → flaking
**Products:** Rolled oats, oat flour, groats
**NAICS:** 311230

### 9.9.1.1.3 Rice Milling
3 stages: (1) rough rice receiving/cleaning/drying/storage; (2) milling (disk huller → whitening cones → polishing cone); (3) packing/shipping
**Products:** White rice (polished), brown rice, broken rice, bran
**NAICS:** 311212

### 9.9.1.1.4 Corn Dry Milling (with Degerming — Tempering-Degerminating System)
8-step process: dry cleaning → wet cleaning → tempering → degerming → drying/cooling → roller milling → sifting → packaging
**Products:** Flaking grits, meal, flour, crude corn oil (from germ)
**NAICS:** 311221 (partial — dry milling vs. wet milling)

### 9.9.1.1.5 Animal Feed Mills
- More than 200 ingredients possible (grains, byproducts, meat meal, bone meal, vitamins)
- Grain received by hopper truck, rail car, or barge
- Process: receiving → cleaning → storage → grinding (hammermill) → mixing → pelleting or meal
- **NAICS:** 311119 (Other Animal Food Manufacturing)
