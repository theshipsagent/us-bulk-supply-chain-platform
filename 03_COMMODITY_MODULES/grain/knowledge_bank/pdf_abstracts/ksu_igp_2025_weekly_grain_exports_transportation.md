## Overview

This is a recurring weekly market intelligence report produced by the KSU International Grains Program (IGP) covering export activity, logistics, freight rates, and supply/demand for the U.S. grain and oilseed export complex. The January 31, 2025 issue is the reference copy in this knowledge bank — the series provides current market data useful for cross-referencing against USDA data sources.

**Report sources integrate:**
- USDA FAS export sales data (weekly export inspections + outstanding sales)
- USDA AMS Grain Transportation Report (GTR) — barge and rail data
- Baltic Exchange (BDI, weekly dry bulk roundup)
- IGC Grains and Oilseeds Freight Index (GOFI)
- Freightos Baltic Index (container freight)
- O'Neil Commodity Consulting (vessel rates)
- Maritime Research Inc. (ocean freight rate tables)

## Key Value for Platform

**Primary use:** Weekly market intelligence template; freight rate benchmarking; port-region grain inspection tracking

**Relevance to grain module:**
- **Freight rates:** Gulf→Japan and PNW→Japan vessel rates provide current ocean cost benchmarks for `supply_chain_models/`
- **FGIS weekly inspection data:** By-region volumes (Table 18) are the primary source for tracking export activity by port; these are also accessible via the FGIS API
- **Barge/rail weekly data:** GTR-sourced data provides real-time modal flow indicators
- **Export sales data:** FAS weekly outstanding sales and inspections (Table 14) cross-validate against `fetch_fas_esr()` ingest pipeline
- **Top importer list (Table 17):** Destination country market intelligence for trade flows analysis

**Data source URLs provided in report:**
- USDA FAS ESR: `https://apps.fas.usda.gov/export-sales/wkHistData.htm`
- USDA AMS GTR: `https://www.ams.usda.gov/services/transportation-analysis/gtr`
- KSU Ag Manager: `https://www.agmanager.info/grain-marketing/publications/us-grain-exports-and-trade`

## Notes

- This report is produced **weekly** — the January 31, 2025 issue is a point-in-time snapshot
- The report integrates multiple commercial freight indices (IGC GOFI, Freightos FBX, Drewry WCI) alongside government data — provides context for correlating ocean freight with grain export volumes
- BNSF shuttle incentive change is market-relevant for rail_cost_model cross-reference
- Trump tariff uncertainty noted as a current market factor (tariff threats on Canada/Mexico/China as of report date)
- BDI at 726 reflects a weak dry bulk market — below 1,000 generally indicates excess vessel supply
