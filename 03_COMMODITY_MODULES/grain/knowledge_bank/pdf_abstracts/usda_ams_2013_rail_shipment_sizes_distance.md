# USDA AMS: Grain and Oilseed Shipment Sizes and Distance Hauled by Rail (2013)

**Source:** USDA Agricultural Marketing Service, Transportation Services Division
**Authors:** Marvin Prater, Adam Sparger
**Date:** December 2013
**DOI:** http://dx.doi.org/10.9752/TS059.12-2013
**File:** `Grain and Oilseed Shipment Sizes and Distance Hauled by Rail.pdf`
**Relevance:** HIGH — definitive source on shuttle train trends; rail shipment economics by commodity

---

## Summary

Analyzes changes in rail shipment size and length of haul for grain and oilseeds (corn, soybean,
wheat, sorghum, barley) from 1994 to 2009 using STB Confidential Waybill Sample. Documents
the shift from carload to shuttle-size shipments and from short-haul to long-haul rail movements.

---

## Key Statistics

### Grain's Role in Rail System
- Grain = **7.9%** of all rail tons (2009)
- Grain = **8.4%** of total rail revenue (2009) — disproportionate revenue importance
- Railroads haul **33%** of all U.S. grain transported (2007)
- Grain = 94% of all farm commodities moved by rail

### Shipment Size Categories Used
| Category | Car Count |
|---|---|
| Small carload | 1 to 5 railcars |
| Carload | 6 to 49 railcars |
| Unit train | 50 to 74 railcars |
| Shuttle train | 75 or more railcars |

### Distance Categories
| Category | Miles |
|---|---|
| Short | 20 to 500 miles |
| Medium | 501 to 1,000 miles |
| Long | 1,001 to 1,500 miles |
| Very long | More than 1,500 miles |

## Key Findings (1994-2009)

### Shipment Size Shift — All Grains
- **1994:** Dominant size = 6-49 railcars for ALL 5 grains
- **2009:**
  - Corn: Shifted to 75+ cars (shuttle-size dominant)
  - Soybeans: Shifted to 75+ cars (shuttle-size dominant)
  - Wheat: Remained 6-49 cars (carload dominant)
  - Sorghum: Remained 6-49 cars (carload dominant)
  - Barley: Remained 6-49 cars (carload dominant)

**Interpretation:** Corn and soy shifted because domestic destinations (ethanol plants, crush
facilities) can handle shuttle volumes; wheat/sorghum/barley export markets require more
point-to-point flexibility.

### Distance Haul Shift
- **1994 dominant category:**
  - Corn, Soybeans, Barley: 20-500 miles (short)
  - Wheat, Sorghum: 501-1,000 miles (medium)
- **2009 dominant category:**
  - Corn: 1,500+ miles (very long)
  - Soybeans: 1,500+ miles (very long)
  - Wheat: 501-1,000 miles (unchanged)
  - Sorghum: 501-1,000 miles (unchanged)
  - Barley: 501-1,000 miles (unchanged)

**Interpretation:** Corn/soy length increases reflect:
1. Growth of corn exports to PNW (long haul from Midwest)
2. Ethanol plants clustering in Corn Belt creating longer average hauls to port
3. BNSF/UP ability to dominate long-distance corn/soy moves

## Rail Shuttle Economy (Key Concept)

**Why shuttle trains lowered costs:**
- Faster terminal turnaround (high-speed loading at sub-terminal elevators)
- Larger grain elevators can fill a mile-long train in 15 hours or less
- Single-origin/single-destination simplifies operations; reduces switching costs
- New jumbo covered hoppers: 286,000-315,000 lbs vs. 263,000 lbs traditional
- Shuttle trains (100-110 cars) vs. unit trains (50-54 cars): even lower cost per bushel

**Enabling infrastructure:**
- High-speed load-out facilities at sub-terminal elevators
- Class I rationalization away from carload/multi-stop service
- Longer, heavier trains require upgraded track at originating short lines ($7B estimated)

## Data Source

**STB Confidential Waybill Sample:** Stratified sample of carload waybills for all U.S. rail
traffic carriers with 4,500+ revenue carloads/year. Contains: origin state, destination state,
commodity (STCC), tons, revenue, car type. Does NOT include facility-level identifiers for
competitive reasons.

**STCC Codes Used:**
- 01112 = Corn
- 01131 = Wheat
- 01141 = Soybeans
- 01155 = Grain Sorghum
- 01130 = Barley
