# Jaller (2022): Grain Transportation Study — Snake River Dam Removal Impact

**Source:** Water Foundation (commissioned study)
**Author:** Miguel Jaller, University of California Davis
**Date:** July 2022
**File:** `Jaller-Grain-Transportation-Study-7-6-2275.pdf`
**Relevance:** HIGH — PNW grain transport multimodal model; wheat export corridor analysis;
Snake River and Columbia River system data; transport cost benchmarks by mode

---

## Summary

Final report analyzing changes in grain movement (primarily wheat) before and after hypothetical
removal of the four lower Snake River (SNR) dams. Develops a multimodal transport model for
Idaho/Washington/Oregon wheat export flows to PNW ocean ports. Provides the most current (2022)
PNW-specific grain transport data available.

---

## Study Region

**Geography:** Idaho, Washington, Oregon
**Commodity focus:** Wheat (primary); secondary grain movements
**Destination:** PNW ocean ports (Kalama WA, Portland OR, Vancouver WA, Longview WA)

4. Farm → Truck → Elevator → Truck → Elevator with rail → Rail → Ocean port

## Transport Cost Benchmarks

**Source:** USACE et al. (2020) rates per bushel over distance

**Key insight (Figure 3 in report):**
- Barge outperforms rail at distances beyond **~250 miles** for total cost
- For multimodal routes, initial truck leg (25-50 miles) significantly affects
  total cost comparison — barge advantage can be negated by long truck origins
- Handling cost: **$0.05/bushel** assumed at each transshipment point
  (elevator, shuttle facility, river port)

**National wheat mode shares (USDA 2021):**
- Rail: ~60% (national)
- Barge: ~28%
- Truck: remaining

**PNW mode variation by sub-region:**
- Northern Idaho (near Lewiston-Clarkston elevators): Truck+Barge ~99% of volume
- Southwest Washington: Truck-Barge 66%
- Southcentral/Southeast Oregon: 0% barge (no waterway access)

## Emissions Analysis

**Transport mode emission factors (Table 5, grams/ton-mile):**
| Mode | CO2 | NOx | PM2.5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Truck | ~161 g/ton-mi | High | High |
| Rail | ~22 g/ton-mi | Medium | Medium |
| Barge | ~31 g/ton-mi | Medium | Medium |

**Key finding:** Rail and barge both substantially more fuel-efficient than truck.
Dam removal scenario would shift volumes to truck/rail, increasing emissions.

---

## Modeling Approach

- ZIP-code level production data (1,308 TAZs/ZIP codes)
- Shortest-route assignment for each TAZ to ocean port
- 10 multimodal route options considered
- All-or-nothing assignment to cheapest route
- Validated against STB Waybill Sample and USACE WCSC barge data
