**Last Updated:** 2026-03-01

**File:** `Port Profiles Entire Pub.pdf`
**Type:** USDA AMS reference publication; port-by-port agricultural trade profiles

---

## Overview

This publication profiles the top 20 U.S. ocean ports handling waterborne agricultural trade. In 2011, **80% of U.S. agricultural exports** (146.5 million metric tons) and **78% of agricultural imports** (40.7 million metric tons) moved by water. The publication provides commodity breakdowns, destination/origin countries, and shipping line data for each port.

**Data sources used:** Census Bureau, U.S. Department of Commerce; PIERS (Port Import Export Reporting Service)

## New Orleans Port Region — Detailed Profile

**Position:** #1 U.S. agricultural port; 33% of total U.S. waterborne agricultural trade

**Geographic coverage:** Mississippi River from Baton Rouge (Mile 228) to Myrtle Grove (Mile 61)

**Key facts:**
- Ocean-going vessels access ports 228 miles upriver from the Gulf of Mexico
- Port of South Louisiana: 54-mile stretch; handled over 274 million tons of cargo in 2011
- Port of South Louisiana moves more tonnage than any other North American port
- Grain = 74% of the port's total export cargo in 2011

**Grain elevator locations in the New Orleans Port Region:**
- Baton Rouge, Darrow, Gramercy
- South Louisiana (Convent, St. Elmo, Reserve, Destrehan, Ama, Westwego)
- New Orleans, Myrtle Grove

## Los Angeles — Detailed Profile

**Position:** #2 U.S. agricultural port; 6% share

**Characteristics:** Containerized; 8 million TEU handled; ~11% agriculture-related

**Top exports (2011):** Animal feed 31%, Soybeans 14%, Cotton 10%, Grain products 9%

**Primary destinations:** China 23%, Taiwan 18%, Japan 13%, Korea 9%

**All exports were containerized** (629,103 TEU total ag exports)

## Notes

- Data is 2011 vintage — port rankings and volumes have shifted since then, particularly New Orleans vs. PNW balance
- Publication does not break out bulk grain vs. grain products vs. oilseeds at every port — commodity categories vary
- PIERS was the commercial data source for shipping line and destination country data; replaced in this platform by Census Bureau AES/FTD data
- Vancouver WA and Kalama WA are often grouped together as "Lower Columbia River" ports; both serve the same corridor
