Sources are organized by category: federal government agencies, industry organizations, academic/research institutions, and data platforms. All URLs were verified and accessible as of Q1 2026. Where data is drawn from subscription or restricted-access services, the source is noted accordingly.

## Citation Notes

1. **Data vintage conventions:** Where this report cites annual statistics (e.g., waterway tonnage, railroad carloads), the most recent complete reporting year available at time of publication is used. USACE waterborne commerce data typically lags 12--18 months; STB railroad data lags 6--12 months. The specific data year is noted in each table or figure where it differs from the report publication date.

2. **Subscription data sources:** Several data sources referenced in this report (Panjiva, Clarkson Research, Drewry, IHS Markit, Baltic Exchange, Argus Media) require paid subscriptions. Data from these sources is used in aggregate or derivative form consistent with licensing terms. Readers seeking to replicate specific analyses should secure appropriate subscriptions.

3. **Federal Register notices:** Section 301 maritime shipping fee rules are published in the Federal Register and are subject to ongoing amendment. The fee structures cited in this report reflect the rules as published through Q1 2026. Readers should consult the Federal Register for the most current fee schedules and phase-in timelines.

4. **Projected data:** Forward-looking projections (demand forecasts, volume projections, completion timelines) represent the report authors' analysis based on the sources listed above. Projections are not forecasts and should be used for planning and scenario analysis purposes only.

5. **URL persistence:** All URLs were verified as accessible at the access dates listed. Federal government URLs may change due to website reorganizations. The Wayback Machine (https://web.archive.org/) may provide archived versions of pages that become unavailable.
