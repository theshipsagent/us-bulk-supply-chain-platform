## KEY STATISTICS HIGHLIGHTED IN REPORT

### Historical Growth
- Total waterborne commerce: 0.7B tons (1947) → 2.1B tons (1979)
- Annual growth rate: 3.1% (total), 4.3% (inland waterways)
- Grain traffic: 12-fold increase (1953-1979)

### Freight Rate Elasticities
- 10% rate increase: 1.4 elasticity (mode/OD choice)
- 100% rate increase: 0.6 elasticity (mode/OD choice)
- Annual volume elasticity: 0.3 (10% rate increase)

### Modal Cost Comparison
- Barge: 1.19 cents/ton-mile
- Rail: 3.16 cents/ton-mile (2.7x barge)
- Truck: 12.90 cents/ton-mile (10.8x barge)

### Export Corridors
- Mississippi River to Gulf: 46% of U.S. grain exports
- Total Gulf Coast ports: 70% of grain exports
- Energy commodities: 61% of all waterborne commerce

### Shipper Behavior
- 26% captive (would shut down if mode unavailable)
- 38% would not switch even with 100% rate increase
- 76% of new facilities choose locations based on transport costs
