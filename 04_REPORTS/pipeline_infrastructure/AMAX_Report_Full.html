Project Overview

Purpose

Analyze Southeast Louisiana oil and gas pipeline infrastructure to support industrial development planning for the AMAX Port Nickel Terminal (387 acres, Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish). Evaluate two strategic business cases:

  1. Case A: Pipeline Network Integration - Natural gas storage facility development with interconnection to regional transmission systems
  2. Case B: Construction Materials Terminal - Cement/aggregates import and distribution serving Gulf Coast LNG, data center, and pipeline construction markets

Geographic Scope

  • Primary Focus: Southeast Louisiana (Plaquemines Parish to St. Charles Parish)
  • Secondary Context: Cameron Parish LNG hub, Richland Parish data center, Texas Gulf Coast, Haynesville Shale

Key Findings Summary

Regional Infrastructure: - 10+ major natural gas transmission systems (30+ Bcf/d capacity) - 15.3 Bcf/d LNG export capacity operating (5 facilities) - 12.3 Bcf/d additional LNG under construction (2025-2028) - Recent pipeline additions: LEG (1.8 Bcf/d), NG3 (2.2 Bcf/d), LEAP Phase 4 (0.3 Bcf/d)

Site Proximity: - Harvest CAM crude oil pipeline: <5 miles (West Bank) - Gulf South natural gas: 10-25 miles estimated - Cayenne NGL river crossing: 10-20 miles estimated - Plaquemines LNG: 50-75 miles south (same parish)

Market Demand (2024-2030): - LNG Construction: 1.5-2.5 million CY concrete, 300-500k tons cement - Data Centers: 0.5-1.0 million CY concrete (Meta Richland Parish $10B project) - Gas Pipelines: 0.3-0.5 million CY concrete, 50+ compressor stations - Total Market: 3-5 million CY concrete, 5-10 million tons aggregates, 500k-1M tons cement

Business Case Assessment: - Case A (Gas Storage): Moderate potential, $200-600M capital, requires Phase 1 feasibility study ($250-500k) - Case B (Materials Terminal): High potential, $25-100M phased capital, 2026 operational target aligns with 2025-2028 peak LNG construction demand

Strategic Recommendation: Prioritize Case B development (near-term market entry) while maintaining Case A optionality via feasibility study

  • FERC: Federal Energy Regulatory Commission
  • EIA: Energy Information Administration
  • AHP: Above Head of Passes (Mississippi River mile marker reference)

  • Gathering: Local collection system from wellhead to processing
  • Intrastate: Pipeline operating within single state
  • Interstate: Pipeline crossing state boundaries (FERC regulated)
