## Implications for AMAX Site Development

### Case A: Pipeline Network Integration
- Plaquemines LNG creates gas demand in same parish as AMAX site
- Gator Express Pipeline likely passes within regional vicinity
- Potential for interconnection or support infrastructure opportunities
- Gas storage demand driven by LNG load balancing requirements

### Case B: Construction Materials Terminal
- Plaquemines LNG: Recently completed (2024-2025) - limited future construction materials demand
- Future Louisiana projects (Louisiana LNG, Commonwealth, CP2): Significant construction materials demand if FID occurs
- Texas facilities under construction (Golden Pass, Port Arthur, Rio Grande): Major demand 2025-2028
- Each LNG train requires:
  - ~50,000-100,000 cubic yards concrete (foundations, containment)
  - Extensive precast concrete (utility vaults, trenches, cable trays)
  - Aggregates for site preparation and access roads
  - Marine terminal construction (jetties, berthing structures)

### Market Outlook
- LNG remains primary driver of Gulf Coast gas pipeline infrastructure investment
- Cameron Parish concentration creates logistics challenges (congestion, workforce, materials supply)
- AMAX site positioning: 75-100 miles from Cameron Parish LNG hub; optimal barge route via Mississippi River/GIWW
