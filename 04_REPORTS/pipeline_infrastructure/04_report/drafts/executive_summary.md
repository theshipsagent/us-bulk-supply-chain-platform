## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Purpose and Scope

This report analyzes the Southeast Louisiana oil and gas pipeline infrastructure corridor to support industrial development planning for the former AMAX Port Nickel Terminal site in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish. The 387-acre waterfront industrial site features deep-draft Mississippi River access, Class I rail service (Norfolk Southern), and substantial laydown and warehousing capacity.

The analysis evaluates pipeline infrastructure through two strategic business cases:

**Case A: Infrastructure Integration Opportunity** - Assess potential for the terminal to participate in regional pipeline networks as a connection point, natural gas storage facility, or distribution hub within the Gulf Coast energy corridor.

**Case B: Construction Materials Terminal Development** - Evaluate how existing and planned pipeline infrastructure (driving LNG exports, data center development, and industrial expansion) creates demand for cement, aggregates, precast concrete, and construction materials, positioning the terminal as a supply hub for regional infrastructure buildout.

### Key Findings

#### Regional Pipeline Infrastructure
- **Natural Gas Transmission:** Southeast Louisiana hosts 10+ major interstate gas transmission systems with combined capacity exceeding 30 Bcf/d, serving LNG export facilities, industrial demand, and power generation. Recent capacity additions (2024-2026) include LEG (1.8 Bcf/d), NG3 (2.2 Bcf/d), and LEAP Phase 4 (0.3 Bcf/d).

- **LNG Export Growth:** Gulf Coast LNG capacity totals 15.3 Bcf/d operating, with 12.9 Bcf/d under construction (expected 2025-2028) and 10.3+ Bcf/d proposed (post-2028). Total potential capacity approaches 40 Bcf/d by early 2030s, requiring proportional natural gas pipeline infrastructure.

- **Site Proximity:** The AMAX site is located within 10 miles of the Harvest CAM crude oil gathering system (West Bank) and estimated 10-25 miles from Gulf South natural gas transmission network. The Cayenne NGL pipeline crosses the Mississippi River within 10-20 miles of the site. Plaquemines LNG (Venture Global, 2.9 Bcf/d) is located 50-75 miles downstream in the same parish.

- **Crude Oil and Products Context:** Major crude oil systems (Harvest CAM, Genesis offshore systems, LOOP, Capline) and refined products pipelines (Colonial Lines 1 & 2, Collins, Plantation) establish Southeast Louisiana as a critical petroleum logistics corridor. Three refineries (combined 1+ million BPD capacity) are located within 20-50 miles of the AMAX site.

#### Market Demand Drivers
- **LNG Export Facilities:** Operating Gulf Coast LNG facilities consume ~15 Bcf/d of natural gas. Facilities under construction (Golden Pass, Port Arthur, Rio Grande, Corpus Christi Stage III) will add 12.9 Bcf/d by 2028. Each 1.0 Bcf/d LNG facility requires 50,000-100,000 cubic yards of concrete for construction.

- **Data Center Development:** Meta's $10 billion Richland Parish data center requires 2,250 MW initial power (up to 5 GW full buildout) supplied by three Entergy combined-cycle gas plants (ISD 2028-2029). Gas demand: 0.4-0.5 Bcf/d initial, up to 1.0 Bcf/d at full buildout. Construction materials demand: 350,000-500,000 cubic yards concrete over 4-6 years.

- **Pipeline Construction Wave:** Major gas pipeline projects under construction or recently completed (2024-2027) include 10+ Bcf/d of new capacity. Each 100 miles of large-diameter (42") pipeline requires extensive concrete for compressor stations, valve vaults, and river crossing infrastructure.

- **Gas Storage Buildout:** Multiple new underground storage facilities proposed or under construction in Louisiana Gulf Coast to provide load balancing for LNG facilities and industrial demand variability.

#### Construction Materials Market Quantification (Case B)
**2024-2030 Gulf Coast Construction Activity:**
- **LNG Facilities:** 1.5-2.0 million CY concrete (Texas + Louisiana facilities under construction)
- **Data Centers:** 0.5-1.0 million CY concrete (Meta Richland + other projects)
- **Gas Pipelines:** 500-1,000 miles new construction; 50+ compressor stations
- **Power Generation:** 10-15 GW new gas-fired capacity
- **Total Concrete Demand:** 3-5 million cubic yards (2024-2030)
- **Aggregates Demand:** 5-10 million tons
- **Cement Demand:** 500,000-1,000,000 tons
- **Precast Products Market:** $500 million - $1 billion

### Strategic Implications for Site Development

#### Case A: Pipeline Network Integration (Moderate Potential)
**Opportunities:**
- Natural gas storage facility development to serve Plaquemines LNG load balancing (50-75 miles south)
- Interconnection with Gulf South Pipeline (estimated 10-25 miles) or lateral to Transco system
- Mississippi River access advantageous for storage facility construction and ongoing operations
- 387-acre site sufficient for above-ground gas storage or salt cavern surface infrastructure

**Challenges:**
- Capital requirements for 20-50 mile lateral pipeline construction to interstate systems
- FERC approval process for interstate connections (12-24 month timeline)
- Market justification (storage capacity or industrial demand anchor tenant)
- Competition from existing Louisiana gas storage infrastructure

**Recommendation:** Requires detailed market study of Gulf Coast gas storage needs and operator discussions with Boardwalk (Gulf South), Williams (Transco), or Venture Global (Plaquemines LNG operator) to assess commercial viability.

#### Case B: Construction Materials Terminal (High Potential)
**Favorable Factors:**
- **Massive demand wave:** 3-5 million CY concrete demand over 6 years driven by LNG, data centers, pipelines, power generation
- **Strategic logistics position:** Mississippi River barge access + Norfolk Southern Class I rail + 386,000 SF laydown yard
- **Import terminal potential:** Cement/clinker import via ship, distribution to regional batch plants via barge/rail
- **Precast products:** Rail delivery of precast concrete to LNG/data center construction sites
- **Aggregates supply:** Ship-to-barge transloading for upriver and Gulf Coast distribution
- **Regional market access:** 75-100 miles from Cameron Parish LNG hub (via GIWW/Mississippi River), direct river access to Plaquemines LNG (downstream), upriver access to refineries and industrial corridor
- **Existing infrastructure:** Dock capacity, rail spurs, laydown yard minimize capital investment vs. greenfield development

**Market Positioning:**
- **Primary Role:** Import terminal and distribution hub (cement, aggregates, precast products)
- **Secondary Role:** Construction materials staging and logistics coordination for major projects
- **Competitive Advantages:** Multimodal access (barge/rail/truck), deep-draft vessel capability, large laydown capacity, reciprocal rail switching to all Class I carriers

**Economic Opportunity:** Materials supply contract participation for one or more major LNG facilities or Meta data center project could generate substantial throughput volumes. Example: 500,000 CY concrete for single LNG facility = 100,000-150,000 tons cement demand over 2-3 year construction period.

### Timeline Considerations

#### Near-Term (2025-2027)
- **LNG Construction Peak:** Golden Pass, Port Arthur, Rio Grande under construction
- **Pipeline Projects:** LEG operating, Trident under construction, multiple FERC approvals
- **Materials Demand:** Immediate and growing through 2027

#### Mid-Term (2028-2030)
- **Data Center Construction:** Meta Richland Parish peak construction activity
- **LNG Commissioning:** Golden Pass, Port Arthur, Rio Grande commissioning
- **Proposed LNG FIDs:** Louisiana LNG (Woodside FID 2024), Commonwealth, CP2 (if approved)
- **Materials Demand:** Sustained high demand

#### Long-Term (Post-2030)
- **LNG Buildout:** Potential 40 Bcf/d total Gulf Coast capacity if all proposed facilities built
- **Storage Infrastructure:** Multiple new storage facilities to support LNG load balancing
- **Maintenance Cycle:** Ongoing pipeline and facility maintenance, turnarounds, expansions

### Risk Factors

#### Market Risks
- **LNG Export Policy:** Federal LNG export approvals subject to policy changes (CP2 project paused January 2024 pending DOE review)
- **Construction Timing:** Project delays or cancellations reduce materials demand forecast
- **Competition:** Existing cement plants, aggregates suppliers, and materials terminals compete for market share
- **Commodity Pricing:** Cement and aggregates prices subject to supply/demand fluctuations

#### Operational Risks
- **Capital Investment:** Terminal upgrades required for cement/aggregates import and handling infrastructure
- **Permitting:** Louisiana DENR, USACE permits for dock modifications and materials handling
- **Environmental:** Dust control, stormwater management for bulk materials operations
- **Logistics Coordination:** Managing multimodal logistics complexity (ship/barge/rail/truck)

#### Regulatory/Political Risks
- **Pipeline Approvals:** FERC process unpredictable for future pipeline projects
- **Environmental Review:** Increasing scrutiny of fossil fuel infrastructure (NEPA, Clean Water Act)
- **Louisiana Coastal Protection:** Wetland mitigation requirements for Mississippi River construction

### Summary Assessment

**Case A (Pipeline Integration):** Moderate potential requiring significant market validation and capital investment. Natural gas storage facility development is plausible if market studies support Gulf Coast storage demand growth and operator partnerships can be established.

**Case B (Construction Materials Terminal):** High potential aligned with near-term (2025-2030) demand drivers. The AMAX site's multimodal logistics capabilities, existing infrastructure, and strategic position in the Gulf Coast energy corridor create compelling opportunity for construction materials import, distribution, and staging operations. Market entry timing (2025-2026) positions terminal to capture peak LNG construction demand (2026-2028) and Meta data center buildout (2028-2030).

**Recommended Path Forward:** Prioritize Case B development with phased capital investment. Pursue materials supply contracts with major project developers (LNG operators, Meta/Entergy). Maintain flexibility for Case A opportunities if gas storage market opportunity emerges.

## 1. INTRODUCTION

### 1.1 Project Context

The former AMAX Port Nickel Terminal in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana, represents a strategic industrial redevelopment opportunity in the heart of the Gulf Coast energy corridor. The 387-acre waterfront site features rare multimodal logistics capabilities: deep-draft Mississippi River access, active Norfolk Southern Class I rail service with reciprocal switching to all major carriers, and substantial on-site laydown and warehousing infrastructure.

This analysis examines how the site can participate in the Gulf Coast's ongoing energy infrastructure expansion, driven by liquefied natural gas (LNG) export growth, data center development, and supporting pipeline construction. Two business cases frame the assessment:

**Case A** evaluates the site's potential to integrate directly into natural gas pipeline networks through storage facilities, interconnections, or distribution infrastructure.

**Case B** assesses the opportunity to position the site as a construction materials terminal supplying cement, aggregates, and precast products to LNG facilities, data centers, pipeline projects, and industrial expansion across the region.

The Louisiana Gulf Coast is experiencing unprecedented natural gas infrastructure investment, with $20-30 billion in pipeline, LNG, storage, and power generation projects under construction or planned through 2030. This buildout creates derivative demand for construction materials, logistics services, and supporting infrastructure—opportunities well-aligned with the AMAX site's existing capabilities.

## 2. REGIONAL PIPELINE INFRASTRUCTURE OVERVIEW

### 2.1 Natural Gas Transmission Systems

Southeast Louisiana serves as a critical nexus for natural gas transmission, connecting Haynesville Shale production (northern Louisiana/East Texas) with Gulf Coast LNG export facilities, industrial demand centers, and interstate transmission to Northeast and Southeast markets. The region hosts 10+ major interstate and intrastate gas transmission systems with combined capacity exceeding 30 Bcf/d.

#### 2.1.1 Interstate Pipelines - Major Systems

**Transco (Transcontinental Gas Pipe Line) - Williams Companies**
The largest natural gas transmission system in the United States, Transco's Zone 3 (Gulf Coast region) serves as the primary artery for Gulf Coast gas supply and demand balancing. System capacity: ~15 Bcf/d system-wide; Louisiana segment capacity variable by zone. Key Louisiana interconnects: Gillis hub (major convergence point for Haynesville takeaway), Station 65, multiple LNG feed points. Transco delivers gas to Cameron LNG, proposed CP2, and other Gulf Coast facilities. The system's extensive reach from Gulf Coast to New York metro area positions it as critical infrastructure for both LNG exports and domestic market supply.

**Texas Eastern Transmission (TETCO) - Enbridge**
Historic interstate system (~10 Bcf/d system-wide) crossing northern Louisiana with growing interest in southern Louisiana expansion. The Venice Extension project demonstrates TETCO's commitment to expanding Gulf Coast market access. Routes through northern Louisiana connect Haynesville production to Northeast markets, while potential southern extensions could serve LNG and industrial demand.

**Tennessee Gas Pipeline - Kinder Morgan**
Large-diameter (up to 36") interstate system (~11 Bcf/d system-wide) with 500 Line through north/central Louisiana. Connects Gulf Coast production to Northeast and Midwest markets. Louisiana segment serves as critical takeaway capacity for Haynesville production and supply route for regional industrial demand.

**ANR Pipeline - TC Energy**
Interstate system with interconnects in northern Louisiana, providing Gulf Coast to Midwest connectivity. Limited direct Southeast Louisiana presence but contributes to regional gas market dynamics.

**Columbia Gulf Transmission - TC Energy**
Louisiana Gulf Coast to Tennessee/Ohio interstate system serving as takeaway capacity for Gulf Coast production and supply route for Midwest/Southeast markets.

#### 2.1.2 Intrastate/Gathering Systems

**Gulf South Pipeline - Boardwalk Pipelines**
Extensive Louisiana intrastate network (~3 Bcf/d capacity) providing gathering, transmission, and local distribution throughout the state. Dense pipeline coverage in Southeast Louisiana makes Gulf South a likely interconnection option for AMAX site (estimated 10-25 miles). Recent Kosci Junction reconfigurations optimize flow patterns for evolving Louisiana gas market dynamics. As Louisiana-focused intrastate system, Gulf South offers operational flexibility not available with interstate systems subject to FERC interstate tariffs.

**Gulf Run Pipeline - Energy Transfer**
Haynesville to Gulf Coast transportation serving as critical Haynesville production takeaway capacity.

**Acadian Haynesville Extension - Enterprise Products Partners**
Haynesville region gathering and mainline transportation supporting Enterprise's integrated midstream operations.

**Bridgeline - Enterprise Products Partners**
Louisiana intrastate transmission system connecting Enterprise's gathering and processing infrastructure.

**High Point Gas Transmission**
Regional transmission system serving Louisiana gas market.

**Sea Robin Pipeline**
Offshore Louisiana to onshore delivery system gathering offshore Gulf of Mexico production for processing and market delivery.

#### 2.1.3 LNG Feeder Infrastructure - Recently Constructed Systems

**Louisiana Energy Gateway (LEG) - Williams Companies**
**Capacity:** 1.8 Bcf/d | **Route:** Haynesville to Gillis interconnect | **ISD:** 2025 (operating)
135-mile, 42-inch diameter pipeline designed to transport Haynesville production to Williams' Gillis hub for delivery to Gulf Coast LNG facilities. FERC Docket CP21-18 approved after extensive environmental review. LEG represents Williams' major commitment to Gulf Coast LNG market growth, with interconnections to Transco, Texas Eastern, Gulf South, and other systems at Gillis. Project capital investment exceeded $1 billion, requiring substantial concrete for compressor stations, valve sites, and construction access.

**NG3 Pipeline (New Generation Gas Gathering) - Momentum Midstream**
**Capacity:** 2.2 Bcf/d | **Route:** Haynesville to Gulf Coast LNG markets | **ISD:** 2025 (operating)
Large-diameter system specifically designed to serve growing LNG export demand. Momentum Midstream's major investment in Gulf Coast gas infrastructure demonstrates strong market confidence in LNG export growth trajectory.

**LEAP Phase 4 (Louisiana Energy Access Project) - DT Midstream**
**Capacity:** +0.3 Bcf/d (expansion) | **Route:** Haynesville to Gillis | **ISD:** 2026 (operating)
Expansion of DT Midstream's existing LEAP system providing additional Haynesville takeaway to Gillis hub convergence. Demonstrates continued investment in Haynesville production egress capacity despite recent major additions (LEG, NG3).

**Gator Express Pipeline - Venture Global LNG**
**Capacity:** Sufficient for 2.9 Bcf/d LNG facility | **Route:** To Plaquemines LNG | **Status:** Operating
Dedicated feed pipeline for Venture Global's Plaquemines LNG facility (2.9 Bcf/d export capacity) located ~50-75 miles south of AMAX site in Plaquemines Parish. **Direct relevance to AMAX site:** Gator Express routing likely passes through or near Braithwaite area en route to Plaquemines LNG, though exact ROW requires GIS verification.

**TransCameron Pipeline - Venture Global LNG**
Dedicated feed for Calcasieu Pass LNG (1.6 Bcf/d) in Cameron Parish.

**Delta Express Pipeline (Proposed) - Venture Global LNG**
Proposed feed for CP2 LNG (2.8 Bcf/d) if project receives DOE approval and proceeds to construction.

#### 2.1.4 Market Context and Capacity Utilization

**Haynesville Production:**
Current production: ~14-15 Bcf/d (2024-2025)
Projected growth: 20-25 Bcf/d by 2030 (driven by LNG demand)
Key operators: Chesapeake Energy, Comstock Resources, Southwestern Energy, EQT, Aethon Energy

**Gulf Coast LNG Demand:**
Operating facilities: ~15 Bcf/d gas consumption
Under construction additions: +12.9 Bcf/d by 2028
Total potential LNG demand: ~28-30 Bcf/d by 2028-2030

**Pipeline Capacity Constraint Analysis:**
Recent capacity additions (LEG 1.8 + NG3 2.2 + LEAP 0.3 + others = 5-7 Bcf/d, 2024-2026) address near-term takeaway needs but leave limited excess capacity for post-2028 LNG growth. Additional pipeline projects likely required if proposed LNG facilities (Louisiana LNG, Commonwealth, CP2) receive FID and proceed to construction.

**Storage Infrastructure Need:**
LNG facilities require gas supply reliability despite production and demand variability. Storage facilities provide: (1) daily load balancing for LNG plant operations, (2) seasonal supply management, (3) market price optimization, (4) emergency supply backup. Growing LNG capacity justifies additional Gulf Coast gas storage investment, creating potential opportunity for AMAX site (Case A scenario).

### 2.2 Crude Oil Pipelines (Reference Context)

While natural gas infrastructure is the primary focus for AMAX site analysis, crude oil pipeline systems provide important context for Southeast Louisiana's role in the Gulf Coast petroleum corridor and industrial development patterns along the Mississippi River.

#### Major Systems Near AMAX Site

**Harvest CAM Pipeline System - Harvest Midstream Company**
**Estimated distance from AMAX:** <5 miles (high confidence) | **Bank:** West Bank (same as AMAX)
Crude oil gathering system serving West Bank New Orleans area refineries. The CAM (crude oil gathering) and BAO (Belle Chasse to Area Oil) systems collect offshore/nearshore crude production and deliver to three New Orleans area refineries: PBF Chalmette (190,000 BPD, East Bank, ~15-20 miles from AMAX), Phillips 66 Alliance (250,000 BPD, West Bank, ~20-25 miles from AMAX), and Marathon Garyville complex (578,000 BPD, West Bank, ~45-50 miles from AMAX).

**Significance for AMAX site:** Harvest CAM system proximity (<5 miles) demonstrates existing pipeline right-of-way infrastructure in immediate AMAX vicinity. Pipeline may cross or run adjacent to AMAX property boundary. The system connects to Belle Chasse Terminal (3,200 acres, 1+ million barrels storage), a competing large-scale industrial terminal facility on the West Bank upriver from AMAX.

**Genesis Energy Offshore Systems**
Multiple offshore crude oil gathering systems (Poseidon, Eugene Island Pipeline System, Odyssey, CHOPS) deliver Gulf of Mexico offshore production to Louisiana coast for refinery supply. Combined capacity: 400,000+ BPD. Systems connect to Louisiana refinery network, contributing to Southeast Louisiana's position as Gulf Coast crude oil logistics hub.

**LOOP (Louisiana Offshore Oil Port)**
Deepwater oil terminal 18 miles offshore with 1.2 million BPD throughput capacity and 62 million barrels storage (Clovelly salt dome). Historic function: supertanker crude oil imports (VLCC-capable). Current transition: declining imports, increasing export activity. 48-inch diameter, 130-mile pipeline to shore delivers to St. James Parish refinery corridor. LOOP's massive storage capacity influences Gulf Coast crude pricing and logistics patterns.

**Capline Pipeline - Plains All American**
630-mile, 40-inch diameter pipeline originally built (1960s) to move Louisiana Gulf Coast crude (LOOP imports) to Midwest refineries. **Reversed 2021** to flow SOUTH (Illinois to Louisiana), reflecting U.S. shale oil production surge and reduced import dependence. Now delivers Midwest crude (Cushing-sourced, including Permian and Bakken) to Louisiana Gulf Coast refineries. Louisiana terminus: St. James Parish (~60-70 miles upriver from AMAX, East Bank).

#### Implications for AMAX Site

**Direct crude oil pipeline integration opportunity:** Limited. AMAX site not suited for refinery operations or crude oil storage/distribution (no existing tankage, insufficient infrastructure investment justification).

**Indirect relevance:** Harvest CAM proximity demonstrates pipeline corridor infrastructure viability in AMAX vicinity. Refinery proximity (three facilities within 20-50 miles, combined 1+ million BPD) creates derivative opportunities: refinery turnaround materials supply, construction logistics, specialty products delivery via AMAX multimodal capabilities.

### 2.3 Refined Products Pipelines (Reference Context)

Southeast Louisiana serves as both origin and transit corridor for major refined products pipeline systems delivering gasoline, diesel, and jet fuel to Southeast and Atlantic Coast markets.

#### Colonial Pipeline System

**Operator:** Colonial Pipeline Company
**Lines:** Line 1 (gasoline), Line 2 (distillates/jet fuel)
**Combined Capacity:** ~2.5 million BPD (~100 million gallons/day)
**Diameter:** 40" (Line 1), 36" (Line 2)
**Route:** Houston, TX → Greensboro, NC (5,500+ miles total system length)

**Louisiana Segment:** Crosses southern Louisiana along East Bank corridor (estimated route through Jefferson/St. Charles Parish area). Estimated distance from AMAX: 15-30 miles (East Bank, across Mississippi River).

**Significance:** Largest refined products pipeline in United States, critical gasoline/diesel supply artery for Southeast US. Originates at Houston/Gulf Coast refineries including Louisiana refiners. Colonial's Louisiana routing demonstrates East Bank refined products pipeline corridor parallel to Mississippi River.

#### Collins Pipeline

**Operator:** Collins Pipeline Company (JV: PBF Energy 80%, Valero 20%)
**Diameter:** 16 inches | **Length:** ~125 miles | **Capacity:** ~65,000 BPD
**Route:** **PBF Chalmette Refinery (St. Bernard Parish) → Collins, MS**
**Products:** Gasoline, jet fuel, diesel, heating oil
**Louisiana Terminus:** Meraux/Chalmette, St. Bernard Parish (~15-20 miles upriver from AMAX, East Bank)

**Significance:** Louisiana-origin refined products pipeline connecting Chalmette Refinery output to Mississippi markets. Interconnects with Plantation and Colonial systems for broader distribution. **Direct relevance to AMAX:** Collins pipeline terminus ~15-20 miles from AMAX site demonstrates refined products infrastructure in immediate regional corridor.

#### Plantation Pipeline

**Operator:** Kinder Morgan
**Capacity:** ~700,000 BPD | **Products:** Gasoline, diesel, jet fuel
**Route:** Baton Rouge, LA → Washington, DC area (3,100+ miles)
**Louisiana Segment:** Originates at Baton Rouge refinery complex (~60-80 miles upriver from AMAX)

**Context:** Major refined products system connecting Louisiana Gulf Coast refineries to Southeast US markets. Demonstrates Louisiana's role as refined petroleum products export state.

#### Implications for AMAX Site

**Refined products pipeline integration:** Limited direct opportunity. AMAX West Bank location separated from primary refined products corridor (East Bank Colonial and Collins systems). No refined products storage or distribution infrastructure currently on site.

**Indirect relevance:** Pipeline maintenance, turnarounds, and expansions create specialty construction materials demand serviceable from AMAX multimodal logistics capabilities.

### 2.4 NGL and Petrochemical Pipelines (Reference Context)

Natural gas liquids (NGLs) and petrochemical pipelines connect Gulf Coast gas processing facilities with fractionation plants and petrochemical consumers.

#### Cayenne Pipeline - AMID/Targa Joint Venture

**Products:** Y-grade NGLs (unfractionated natural gas liquids)
**Capacity:** 40,000-50,000+ BPD
**Route:** **Venice Gas Processing Plant (Plaquemines Parish) → Toca Gas Processing Plant (Ascension Parish)**
**Distance:** ~125 miles
**Status:** Operating

**Route Details:**
- **Origin:** Venice Gas Processing Complex, Plaquemines Parish (~75 miles south of AMAX, East Bank)
- **Destination:** Toca Gas Processing/Fractionation Facility, Ascension Parish (~50 miles northwest of AMAX, West Bank)
- **Key Feature:** **Crosses Mississippi River via horizontal directional drill (HDD)** likely within 10-20 miles of AMAX site

**Function:** Gather raw NGLs from Venice gas processing plant (processing offshore gas production) and transport to Toca for fractionation into purity products (ethane, propane, butane, natural gasoline). Critical link between offshore production and petrochemical markets.

**Significance for AMAX:** Cayenne Pipeline's Mississippi River crossing in AMAX regional vicinity (estimated 10-20 miles) demonstrates: (1) NGL pipeline infrastructure in immediate corridor, (2) technical feasibility of river crossing via HDD, (3) Venice-to-Toca NGL logistics corridor that AMAX site could potentially support (storage, transloading, or related services).

#### Other Major NGL Systems

**ONEOK NGL Systems** (consolidated from EnLink and Magellan acquisitions, 2023-2024): Extensive Louisiana gathering, processing, fractionation, and storage network. ONEOK's 2023-2024 acquisitions created integrated NGL giant with dominant Louisiana market position.

**Enterprise Products Partners:** Gulf Coast NGL infrastructure including Mont Belvieu connections, gathering systems, and Louisiana processing assets.

**Targa Resources:** Louisiana processing plants and gathering systems, including Toca Fractionation Complex (Cayenne Pipeline destination).

#### Implications for AMAX Site

**NGL infrastructure opportunity (Case A potential):** Cayenne Pipeline's proximity and river crossing demonstrates NGL market activity in AMAX corridor. Potential opportunities: NGL storage for load balancing (Venice processing to Toca fractionation), barge-to-pipeline NGL transfer infrastructure, NGL import terminal if Toca fractionation capacity expands. NGL market dynamics less significant than natural gas storage opportunity but merit consideration in comprehensive site development planning.

## 3. SITE-PROXIMATE INFRASTRUCTURE

### 3.1 AMAX Port Nickel Location Context

The former AMAX Port Nickel Terminal occupies 387 acres of West Bank Mississippi River frontage in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, approximately 80 miles above Head of Passes (AHP). The site's geographic position places it downstream from New Orleans' industrial concentration (25 miles south of CBD) but upstream from the deepwater Port of Plaquemines and offshore petroleum activity. This intermediate location balances proximity to metropolitan demand centers with access to Gulf-oriented maritime logistics.

**Site Coordinates:** 29.88°N, 89.93°W (approximate, requires parcel data verification)
**River Mile:** ~80 AHP
**Bank:** West Bank (Louisiana Highway 39 corridor)

**Existing Infrastructure Assets:**
- **Marine:** 510' x 52' dock with 774' mooring area; second dock (former liquids) requires refurbishment
- **Rail:** Active Norfolk Southern spur with on-site car storage and multiple loading docks; reciprocal switching access to KCS, CN, UP, BNSF, CSX

## 6. IMPLICATIONS FOR SITE DEVELOPMENT

### 6.1 Case A: Pipeline Network Integration Opportunities

#### 6.1.1 Natural Gas Storage Facility Development

**Concept:** Develop underground natural gas storage facility (salt cavern or depleted reservoir) at or near AMAX site with interconnection to Gulf South Pipeline (estimated 10-25 miles) or other interstate transmission systems.

**Value Proposition:**
- **Load Balancing for Plaquemines LNG:** Located ~50-75 miles from AMAX, Plaquemines LNG (2.9 Bcf/d) requires reliable gas supply despite production and demand variability; storage provides daily and seasonal buffering
- **Haynesville Production Management:** Storage mitigates Haynesville production volatility and provides market arbitrage opportunity (buy low/inject summer, withdraw/sell high winter)
- **Industrial Demand Buffer:** Southeast Louisiana petrochemical facilities benefit from storage-backed supply reliability
- **Market Arbitrage:** Gulf Coast gas price volatility (driven by LNG export demand) creates profitable buy/store/sell opportunities

**Site Advantages:**
- **387-Acre Land Area:** Adequate for compressor station, dehydration, metering, and surface infrastructure
- **Mississippi River Access:** Construction materials and equipment delivery via barge reduces capital costs
- **Rail Access:** Norfolk Southern service enables equipment and specialty materials delivery
- **341 Acres Upland:** Minimal wetland mitigation vs. greenfield coastal sites

**Technical Feasibility Requirements:**
1. **Geological Assessment:** Determine salt dome presence/suitability in Plaquemines Parish; alternatively assess depleted offshore field conversion feasibility
2. **Capacity Sizing:** Market study to determine optimal storage capacity (5-20 Bcf typical range for new salt cavern facilities)
3. **Interconnection Design:** Engineering study for 10-25 mile lateral to Gulf South or 20-50 mile lateral to Transco/TETCO
4. **FERC Process:** If interstate interconnection, FERC certificate application (12-24 months); intrastate (Gulf South) avoids FERC but requires Louisiana regulatory approvals

**Capital Requirements Estimate:**
- **Salt Cavern Development:** $100-300 million (depending on number/size of caverns)
- **Surface Infrastructure:** $50-100 million (compressor, dehydration, metering)
- **Pipeline Interconnection:** $30-100 million (10-25 miles to Gulf South); $100-300 million (20-50 miles to interstate system)
- **Total Project:** $200-600 million depending on configuration

**Revenue Potential:**
- **Storage Service Fees:** $0.50-2.00/MMBtu/month typical commercial rates
- **10 Bcf Working Gas Capacity Example:** $5-20 million annual revenue (depending on utilization and rates)
- **Market Arbitrage:** Additional upside from seasonal/daily price spreads (volatile Gulf Coast market)

**Challenges and Risks:**
- **Geological Uncertainty:** Salt dome suitability unknown without detailed subsurface study
- **Capital Intensity:** $200-600 million requires strong commercial foundation (storage contracts)
- **Market Competition:** Existing Louisiana storage infrastructure; must demonstrate incremental value
- **Regulatory Timeline:** FERC approval (if interstate) adds 12-24 months; Louisiana approvals typically faster
- **Operator Partnership:** AMAX likely needs experienced midstream operator partner (Boardwalk, Williams, Venture Global, or other)

**Recommendation:** Commission Phase 1 feasibility study ($250,000-500,000) including: (1) geological assessment (salt dome presence/characteristics), (2) market demand study (storage capacity needs, competitive analysis, revenue projections), (3) interconnection engineering (route analysis, permitting assessment, capital cost estimate), (4) operator discussions (Boardwalk for Gulf South interconnection; Venture Global for Plaquemines LNG load balancing; others). Proceed to full development only if Phase 1 confirms geological feasibility, market demand, and operator partnership interest.

#### 6.2.2 AMAX Strategic Positioning

**Primary Business Model: Import Terminal and Distribution Hub**

**Value Proposition:**

2. **Barge Distribution:** Load cement onto river barges for upriver delivery (Baton Rouge, Natchez, Vicksburg) or GIWW transit (Cameron Parish LNG hub via Intracoastal Waterway)
3. **Rail Distribution:** Load cement onto rail cars (Norfolk Southern) for delivery to regional batch plants and project sites
4. **Aggregates Import and Distribution:** Similar ship/barge/rail model for construction aggregates (crushed stone, sand, gravel)
5. **Precast Concrete Products:** Rail delivery of precast products manufactured at AMAX (if precast plant developed) or imported via ship/barge

**Competitive Advantages:**
- **Multimodal Access:** Unique combination of deep-draft dock + Class I rail + barge loading + truck access
- **Reciprocal Rail Switching:** Norfolk Southern with switching rights to all Class I carriers (KCS, CN, UP, BNSF, CSX) maximizes rail market reach
- **Laydown Capacity:** 386,000+ SF yard provides materials storage and staging area (cement silos, aggregate stockpiles, precast product inventory)

## 7. CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

### 7.1 Summary Findings

Southeast Louisiana's natural gas infrastructure is undergoing historic expansion driven by LNG export growth (15.3 Bcf/d operating, 12.3 Bcf/d under construction), data center development (Meta's $10 billion Richland Parish investment), and supporting pipeline construction (10+ Bcf/d capacity additions 2024-2027). This buildout creates derivative demand for construction materials (3-5 million cubic yards concrete, 500,000-1,000,000 tons cement over 2024-2030) and potential opportunities for natural gas storage infrastructure to support LNG load balancing and market operations.

The AMAX Port Nickel Terminal site in Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, occupies a strategic position in this infrastructure corridor: West Bank Mississippi River frontage with deep-draft dock access, Class I rail service (Norfolk Southern with reciprocal switching), 386,000+ SF laydown yard, and proximity to both operating pipeline infrastructure (Harvest CAM crude oil system <5 miles) and major LNG facilities (Plaquemines LNG 50-75 miles downstream).

Two distinct business cases emerge for AMAX site redevelopment:

**Case A (Pipeline Network Integration)** presents **moderate potential** contingent on geological feasibility (salt dome presence for gas storage) and commercial validation (operator partnerships, market demand confirmation). Natural gas storage facility development could serve Plaquemines LNG load balancing, Haynesville production management, and Gulf Coast market arbitrage needs. Capital requirements ($200-600 million) and development timeline (3-5 years from feasibility study to operations) are substantial. Recommended approach: Commission $250,000-500,000 Phase 1 feasibility study (geological assessment, market analysis, operator discussions) before committing to full development.

**Case B (Construction Materials Terminal)** presents **high potential** aligned with near-term market demand (2025-2030 peak LNG/data center construction) and AMAX site's existing multimodal logistics infrastructure. Terminal development for cement/aggregates import, storage, and distribution (ship/barge/rail) requires $25-50 million Phase 1 investment with 12-18 month implementation timeline, positioning for 2026 operational launch to capture 2025-2028 peak LNG construction materials demand. Revenue potential: $5-15 million/year depending on throughput and value-added services. Risk profile lower than Case A (phased capital investment, diversified customer base, proven business model).

### 7.5 Final Assessment

The AMAX Port Nickel Terminal site presents a **compelling opportunity for Case B construction materials terminal development** with near-term market entry (2026 operational target) to capture 2025-2030 Gulf Coast LNG, data center, and pipeline infrastructure construction materials demand. The site's multimodal logistics capabilities (ship/barge/rail/truck), existing infrastructure (dock, rail, laydown), and strategic position in the Gulf Coast energy corridor align with construction materials import, storage, and distribution terminal operations. Phased capital investment ($25-50M Phase 1, $50-100M full buildout) and diversified customer base (LNG, data centers, industrial, pipeline construction) create favorable risk-adjusted return profile.

Storage facility development timeline (3-5 years) and capital intensity warrant staged approach: feasibility study first, full development decision contingent on positive results.

**Recommended path forward:** Prioritize Case B development with 2026 operational target while maintaining Case A optionality through parallel feasibility study. Market entry timing critical: 2025-2026 decision and deployment positions AMAX to capture peak demand window (2025-2030); delay beyond 2026 risks missing opportunity.
