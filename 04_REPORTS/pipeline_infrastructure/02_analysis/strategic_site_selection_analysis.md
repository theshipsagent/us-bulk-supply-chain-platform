# Strategic Site Selection Analysis
## Lower Mississippi River: Optimal Zones for Deep-Water + Pipeline Access

**Geographic Scope:** Below Baton Rouge (~Mile 230 AHP) to Head of Passes (Mile 0 AHP)
**Focus:** Sites combining deep-water vessel access with proximity to major pipeline systems
**Purpose:** Industrial terminal or pipeline interconnection site development

---

## Site Selection Criteria

### Critical Factors:
1. **Deep-water access** - Draft capability for mid-sized to large vessels (25+ ft draft)
2. **Pipeline proximity** - Within economical lateral construction distance (<15 miles ideal, <30 miles acceptable)
3. **Pipeline system significance** - Proximity to major transmission systems or convergence points
4. **Industrial zoning** - Existing or achievable industrial land use designation
5. **Multimodal access** - Rail, truck, barge capability
6. **Labor availability** - Workforce proximity
7. **Existing infrastructure** - Utilities, roads, developed parcels
8. **Environmental factors** - Wetland avoidance, hurricane risk, floodplain considerations

## TIER 1 ZONES - Optimal Combined Access

### 🏆 Zone 1A: St. James Parish (East Bank) - Convent/Gramercy/Wallace Area
**River Mile:** 160-170 AHP

**Why This is THE Premier Location:**
- **MAJOR PIPELINE CONVERGENCE POINT** - unmatched in lower Mississippi corridor
- **Deep-water access:** Excellent (upriver of New Orleans congestion, downriver of Baton Rouge)
- **Industrial establishment:** Mature industrial corridor

**Pipeline Infrastructure Density:**

**Crude Oil (HIGHEST DENSITY):**
- ✅ **Capline Pipeline Terminus** - 1.2 million BPD, 40-inch diameter, from Patoka IL
  - Plains All American operated
  - Delivers to St. James terminal complex
  - Serves Marathon Garyville, Shell Norco, Phillips 66 Alliance refineries

- ✅ **Bayou Bridge Pipeline Terminus** - 480,000 BPD, 24-inch diameter
  - Energy Transfer operated
  - Nederland TX to St. James
  - Connects to St. James terminal infrastructure

**Natural Gas (Strong Likelihood):**
- Gulf South Pipeline (Boardwalk) - Extensive Louisiana intrastate network likely passes through/near
- Transco Zone 3 (Williams) - Major interstate system, possible lateral access
- Tennessee Gas 500 Line (Kinder Morgan) - Louisiana routing, potential proximity

**Refined Products:**
- Plantation Pipeline origin (Baton Rouge ~60 miles upriver, but St. James in distribution zone)
- Multiple refinery product loading terminals

**Connected Facilities:**
- Marathon Garyville Refinery (578,000 BPD) - ~15 miles upriver (West Bank)
- Shell Norco Refinery (~50,000 BPD processing) - ~10 miles downriver (West Bank)
- Phillips 66 Alliance Refinery (250,000 BPD) - ~20 miles downriver (West Bank)
- Multiple crude oil storage terminals (St. James is THE Louisiana crude hub)

**Site Selection Advantages:**
- **Existing terminal infrastructure:** Multiple crude terminals, marine docks, tank farms
- **Pipeline convergence:** Two major crude systems terminate here (rare combination)
- **Rail access:** Class I rail service (likely CN, UP access)
- **Industrial zoning:** Fully established industrial corridor
- **Labor pool:** St. James/Ascension/Baton Rouge workforce accessible
- **Property availability:** Industrial parcels likely available (check St. James Parish GIS)

**Optimal Use Cases:**
- **Crude oil storage/blending terminal** (primary opportunity - leverages Capline + Bayou Bridge)
- **Natural gas storage interconnection** (if pipeline lateral to Gulf South/Transco feasible)
- **Construction materials terminal** (less optimal than closer to New Orleans, but serviceable)
- **Refined products distribution** (refinery cluster proximity)

**Challenges:**
- High competition (established industrial area, may have limited available parcels)
- Petroleum focus may limit diversification options

**Search Strategy:**
- **Target parcels:** 100-500 acres with river frontage in St. James Parish industrial zone
- **GIS resources:** St. James Parish assessor, Louisiana State GIS
- **Proximity target:** <5 miles from Capline/Bayou Bridge terminus

**Verdict:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ **If crude oil infrastructure is the priority, this is THE location**

## CONCLUSION

**The AMAX Port Nickel site (Braithwaite, Plaquemines Parish, West Bank, Mile 80 AHP) is ALREADY in an optimal location** for deep-water + pipeline access. It ranks among the top 3 zones in the entire Baton Rouge-to-Head of Passes corridor.

**If seeking alternative sites:**
- **For crude oil focus:** St. James Parish (East Bank) is THE premier location - Capline + Bayou Bridge convergence is unmatched
- **For gas storage/multimodal:** AMAX current location or St. Charles/Norco area (West Bank) are optimal
- **For construction materials (Case B):** Jefferson Parish/Harvey-Westwego has New Orleans market proximity advantage, but AMAX multimodal capability (especially rail) is competitive

**Strategic insight:** Rather than seeking alternative locations, AMAX site's optimal positioning suggests **development at the current location** is the highest-value strategy. The combination of deep-water access, Harvest CAM crude proximity, Gulf South gas potential, Norfolk Southern rail, and 387-acre footprint is difficult to replicate elsewhere in the corridor.
