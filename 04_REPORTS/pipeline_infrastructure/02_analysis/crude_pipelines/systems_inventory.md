### LOOP - Louisiana Offshore Oil Port

#### Facility Overview
| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **Name** | Louisiana Offshore Oil Port (LOOP) |
| **Location** | 18 miles offshore, Plaquemines/Lafourche Parish waters |
| **Type** | Deepwater oil import terminal (historically); now export-capable |
| **Capacity** | 1.2 million BPD throughput capacity |
| **Storage** | 62 million barrels (Clovelly underground salt dome storage) |
| **Pipeline to Shore** | 48" diameter, 130 miles to Houma area |

#### Current Operations
- **Historic Function:** Supertanker crude oil import (VLCC capable)
- **Transition:** Declining imports; increasing export activity
- **Strategic Assets:** Massive storage capacity, deep-draft vessel capability
- **Connected Systems:** Capline reversal feed, Gulf Coast refinery supply

#### Significance for Regional Context
- Major crude oil infrastructure anchor for Louisiana Gulf Coast
- Storage capacity influences Gulf Coast crude pricing and logistics
- Export capability growing with US production increases

### Capline Pipeline

#### System Overview
| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **Name** | Capline Pipeline (formerly Marathon-operated) |
| **Operator** | Plains All American (acquired 2021) |
| **Historical Route** | St. James, LA (Mississippi River) to Patoka, IL (original direction) |
| **Status** | **Reversed 2021** - Now flows SOUTH (Illinois to Louisiana) |
| **Capacity** | ~1.2 million BPD (original design) |
| **Current Function** | Deliver crude from Midwest (Cushing-sourced) to Louisiana Gulf Coast refineries |
| **Diameter** | 40 inches |
| **Length** | ~630 miles |

#### Reversal Context
- **Original Purpose (1960s-2010s):** Move crude from Louisiana Gulf Coast (LOOP imports) to Midwest refineries
- **Reversal Driver:** US shale oil production surge, reduced imports, Gulf Coast refinery demand shift
- **Post-Reversal Function:** Supply Louisiana refineries with domestic crude (Permian, Bakken via Cushing hub)

#### Louisiana Terminus
- **Delivery Point:** St. James Parish (near Marathon/Phillips 66/Shell refineries)
- **Distance from AMAX:** ~60-70 miles upriver
- **Bank:** East Bank

## NGL and Petrochemical Pipelines

### Cayenne Pipeline (HIGHEST RELEVANCE TO AMAX SITE)

#### System Overview
| Field | Data |
|-------|------|
| **Operator** | AMID/Targa Joint Venture |
| **Products** | Y-grade natural gas liquids (unfractionated) |
| **Capacity** | 40,000-50,000+ BPD |
| **Route** | **Venice Gas Processing Plant → Toca Gas Processing Plant** |
| **Distance** | ~125 miles |
| **Status** | Operating |

#### Route Details
- **Origin:** Venice Gas Processing Complex, Plaquemines Parish (~75 miles south of AMAX)
- **Destination:** Toca Gas Processing/Fractionation Facility, Ascension Parish (~50 miles northwest of AMAX)
- **Key Feature:** **Crosses under Mississippi River** (likely near or south of AMAX site vicinity)
- **Bank Routing:** Originates East Bank (Venice), crosses to West Bank, delivers West Bank (Toca)

#### Proximity to AMAX Site
- **Estimated Distance:** <10 miles (likely crosses river within 10-15 miles of AMAX)
- **Crossing Method:** Horizontal directional drill (HDD) under Mississippi River
- **Significance:** Major NGL pipeline infrastructure in immediate AMAX regional corridor

#### Function
- Gather raw NGLs from Venice gas processing plant (offshore gas production processing)
- Transport to Toca for fractionation into purity products (ethane, propane, butane, natural gasoline)
- Critical link between offshore production and petrochemical markets

## Implications for AMAX Site Development

### Case A: Pipeline Network Integration

**Crude Oil Infrastructure Context:**
- Harvest CAM system proximity (<10 miles) demonstrates existing pipeline corridor infrastructure
- No direct crude oil pipeline integration opportunity (AMAX not suited for refinery operations)
- Belle Chasse Terminal (Harvest) competitor/comparison for Louisiana river terminal operations

**Refined Products Context:**
- Colonial and Collins pipelines demonstrate East Bank refined products corridor
- AMAX West Bank location separated from primary refined products infrastructure
- Limited refined products pipeline integration opportunity

**NGL Infrastructure - HIGHER POTENTIAL:**
- Cayenne Pipeline crosses Mississippi River in AMAX regional vicinity
- River crossing infrastructure demonstrates technical feasibility
- NGL market (Venice to Toca corridor) creates potential for:
  - Storage facilities for NGL load balancing
  - Barge-to-pipeline transfer infrastructure
  - NGL import terminal (if Toca fractionation capacity expands)

### Case B: Construction Materials Terminal

**Pipeline ROW Construction Activity:**
- Recent NGL pipeline construction (Cayenne) required river crossing construction
- Ongoing maintenance and potential expansions require construction access
- Future pipeline projects (gas, NGLs, or products) may utilize Mississippi River corridor

**Refinery Maintenance and Turnarounds:**
- Three major refineries within 20-50 miles (PBF Chalmette, Phillips 66 Alliance, Marathon complex)
- Combined capacity: ~1+ million BPD
- Turnaround cycles: Major maintenance every 3-5 years
- Materials demand: Refractory materials, precast concrete, aggregates, specialty products
- AMAX positioning: River/rail access for heavy/bulk materials supply
