## Implications for AMAX Site Development

### Case A: Pipeline Network Integration
- Data center gas demand in northern Louisiana creates market pressure on Haynesville takeaway capacity
- Reinforces need for Louisiana gas transmission infrastructure
- Potential for AMAX site to participate in gas storage (load balancing for data center/LNG combo demand)

### Case B: Construction Materials Terminal
**High Relevance for Materials Demand:**

#### Richland Parish Distance from AMAX
- **Direct Distance:** ~275 miles north of Braithwaite
- **Logistics Route:** Mississippi River barge to Vicksburg or Natchez, then truck ~100-150 miles east
- **Alternative:** Norfolk Southern rail to Monroe/Richland Parish area
- **Competition:** Closer cement/concrete plants in north Louisiana and Arkansas

#### Market Opportunity Assessment
**Favorable Factors:**
- Massive volume demand (300,000-500,000 CY concrete over 4-6 years)
- AMAX rail access (Norfolk Southern) connects to Monroe area
- Barge-to-truck transloading could reduce costs vs. all-truck delivery
- Potential to supply precast concrete products via rail

**Challenges:**
- Distance to site (275+ miles) increases transportation costs
- Regional suppliers closer to Richland Parish (Monroe, Shreveport, Arkansas)
- Economics favor local concrete batch plants over long-distance delivery

**Realistic Positioning:**
- AMAX unlikely to be primary concrete supplier for Richland Parish project
- Better positioned for **cement/clinker import and distribution** to regional batch plants
- Rail delivery of **precast products** manufactured at AMAX or imported via ship/barge
- **Aggregates supply** for regional distribution if imported via ship

### Combined Market Effect (Data Center + LNG)

#### Total Gulf Coast Gas Demand Growth (2024-2030)
- **LNG Additions:** +10-12 Bcf/d (facilities under construction)
