Regional Infrastructure Investment

Southeast Louisiana is experiencing significant natural gas infrastructure investment, with $20–30 billion in pipeline, LNG, storage, and power generation projects under construction or planned through 2030. The region's pipeline network connects Haynesville production to Gulf Coast export markets and serves growing industrial and power generation demand across Louisiana and the Gulf Coast.

10+ Natural Gas Systems

Transco, TETCO, Tennessee, Gulf South, LEG, NG3, LEAP, and others combine for 30+ Bcf/d capacity. Recent additions (LEG 1.8, NG3 2.2, LEAP 0.3) serve LNG growth. Gulf South intrastate system estimated 10–25 miles from AMAX site.

15.3 Bcf/d LNG Capacity

5 operating facilities (Cameron, Corpus Christi, Sabine, Calcasieu Pass, Plaquemines) consume massive natural gas volumes. Plaquemines LNG (2.9 Bcf/d) located 50–75 miles south of AMAX in same parish, with Gator Express feed pipeline.

Massive Materials Demand

LNG construction (1.5–2.5M CY concrete), data center buildout (Meta Richland Parish: 350K–500K CY), pipeline projects, and power generation drive 3–5 million CY concrete demand through 2030. Import gap creates terminal opportunity.

Key Takeaway: Gulf South Proximity

Gulf South Pipeline's Louisiana-wide intrastate network provides estimated 10–25 mile distance to AMAX site and offers operational flexibility for interconnection. Recent systems (LEG, NG3, LEAP) add 5+ Bcf/d new capacity to serve LNG growth, demonstrating sustained infrastructure investment in Southeast Louisiana corridor.

LNG Construction Demand Driver

LNG facilities under construction require 50,000–100,000 cubic yards concrete per 1.0 Bcf/d nameplate capacity. 12.9 Bcf/d under construction = 645,000–1,290,000 CY concrete demand. Combined with operating facility maintenance and data center/pipeline projects, total 2024–2030 demand reaches 3–5 million CY concrete, 5–10 million tons aggregates, 500K–1M tons cement.
