SUBJECT: Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Request for Historical Maritime Agency Fee Records

Dear FOIA Officer:

Pursuant to the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA), 5 U.S.C. § 552, I respectfully request access to records related to port agency fees and vessel disbursement costs during the period 1915-1995.

BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE

I am conducting historical economic research on the maritime port agency industry, specifically analyzing how agency fees and port service costs have changed relative to inflation over the past 110 years. This research aims to document cost trends in an industry that has operated with minimal technological change since the early 20th century.

SPECIFIC RECORDS REQUESTED

I am seeking any records that contain information about port agency fees, ship agent compensation, vessel disbursement accounts, or port service costs from the following federal record groups:

Record Group 032: Records of the United States Shipping Board (1917-1936)

Specifically:
1. Vessel disbursement files or accounts showing port agency fees paid for vessel port calls
2. Rate investigation files examining agency fees or port costs
3. Tariff filings that include agency service charges
4. General correspondence referencing "agency fees," "ship agent compensation," "port disbursements," or "vessel service costs"
5. Orders and decisions relating to agency fee disputes or rate regulation
6. Cost studies or surveys of maritime service providers

Record Group 178: Records of the United States Maritime Commission (1936-1950)

Specifically:
1. Conference agreement files (1920-1947) mentioning agency fees or service charges
2. Industry cost studies examining port expenses or vessel operating costs
3. Rate case files involving agency compensation
4. Annual reports or statistical compilations showing average port costs or agency fees
5. General subject files (1936-1950) containing materials on "port agents," "ship agents," "disbursements," or "port service costs"

Record Group 357: Records of the Federal Maritime Board/Commission (1950-1995)

Specifically:
1. Tariff and rate filings that include agency fee schedules
2. Industry cost surveys from the 1950s-1990s
3. Rate proceeding files examining port costs or agency compensation
4. Annual reports containing maritime service cost data

TIME PERIOD

Primary focus: 1915-1995

Specific benchmark years of high interest:
- 1915-1920 (WWI period, Shipping Board establishment)
- 1935-1940 (Depression era, Maritime Commission establishment)
- 1950-1960 (Post-WWII, Federal Maritime Board)
- 1970-1980 (Modern containerization)
- 1990-1995 (Pre-internet era)

FORMAT PREFERENCE

I request records in the following priority order:
1. Digital copies (PDF) if available
2. Photocopies of original documents
3. Access to view originals at National Archives II (College Park, MD)

FEE WAIVER REQUEST

I respectfully request a waiver of all fees associated with this request pursuant to 5 U.S.C. § 552(a)(4)(A)(iii) on the grounds that:

1. Public Interest: This research serves the public interest by documenting historical economic trends in a critical but under-studied sector of U.S. maritime commerce.

2. Educational Purpose: The findings will be used to analyze structural changes in maritime service industries and inform understanding of technological disruption in long-established business models.

3. Non-Commercial: I am not seeking these records for commercial purposes, but rather for historical and economic research.

4. Limited Income: As an independent researcher, duplication fees would impose a financial hardship.

If my fee waiver request is denied, I am willing to pay up to $100 in search and duplication fees. Please notify me before incurring costs exceeding this amount.
