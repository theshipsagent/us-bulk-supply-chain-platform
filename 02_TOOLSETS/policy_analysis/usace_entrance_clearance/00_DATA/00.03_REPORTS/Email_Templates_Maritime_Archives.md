### Email Body:

I am seeking access to historical records that might contain information about **port agency fees**, **ship agent compensation**, or **vessel disbursement costs** from approximately 1915-1995.

**Specifically, I am looking for:**

1. **Trade publications** such as:
   - Fairplay magazine (especially 1920, 1935, 1955, 1975, 1995)
   - Lloyd's List archives
   - Journal of Commerce shipping sections
   - Maritime Reporter
   - American Shipper

2. **Shipping company records** that might include:
   - Vessel disbursement accounts showing port agency fees paid
   - Rate schedules or tariff books for port services
   - Agent invoices or billing statements
   - Correspondence discussing agency compensation

3. **Port authority publications** containing:
   - Tariff schedules listing agency service charges
   - Annual reports with cost data
   - Rate investigation files

4. **Industry directories** such as:
   - Ship brokers and agents directories
   - Maritime service provider listings with fee schedules

**Time periods of particular interest:**
- 1915-1920 (WWI era, Shipping Board establishment)
- 1935-1940 (Depression era, Maritime Commission establishment)
- 1950-1960 (Post-WWII, Federal Maritime Board)
- 1970-1980 (Container revolution)
- 1990-1995 (Pre-internet era)

**Research Purpose:**
This is non-commercial academic research examining structural changes in maritime service industries and understanding technological disruption in long-established business models. The findings will contribute to understanding how port agency fees have evolved relative to inflation and whether the current AI disruption represents a fundamental break from historical patterns.

**My Question:**
Does your collection contain any materials that might include this type of information? If so, I would be grateful for:
- Guidance on which collections or record series to examine
- Information about access procedures (on-site visit, digital scans, etc.)
- Estimated research fees or reproduction costs
- Availability for remote research assistance

I am based in [Your Location] and am willing to visit in person if necessary, though remote access would be preferred if available.

Thank you for your time and expertise. I understand that specific fee data may be scarce in public collections, so any guidance toward potential sources would be greatly appreciated.

## RESEARCH VISIT PLANNING (If In-Person Required)

### Before Visiting:

1. **Make appointment** at least 2-4 weeks in advance
2. **Submit research request** in advance so materials can be pulled
3. **Check access policies:**
   - Photo ID requirements
   - Registration procedures
   - Materials handling rules (no pens, bags, etc.)
4. **Confirm hours** and any closures
5. **Ask about duplication services:**
   - Can you photograph documents with phone/camera?
   - Is scanning service available?
   - What are costs?

### During Visit:

1. **Bring:**
   - Laptop for notes
   - Pencils only (no pens)
   - Clear bag for personal items
   - Research outline/checklist
   - Citation format for tracking sources

2. **Document everything:**
   - Take photos of relevant pages (if allowed)
   - Record full citation information
   - Note folder/box numbers for future reference

3. **Be efficient:**
   - Focus on most promising materials first
   - Make quick assessments of relevance
   - Flag items for deep review later

### After Visit:

1. Send thank-you email to archivist
2. Organize and catalog materials collected
3. Follow up on any leads or suggestions provided
4. Consider return visit if needed
