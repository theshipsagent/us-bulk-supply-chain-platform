Executive Summary

Fleet Composition: 78 US-flag vessels analyzed - 20 MSC (25.6%), 58 commercial (74.4%), 3 RRF (3.8%)

Norfolk Concentration: 18 vessels based (23.1% of fleet) - 14 MSC, 4 commercial

Key Operators: Maersk Line Ltd (MSC prime), Matson (Hawaii), Crowley (Alaska/ESB), OSG (product tankers)

Business Opportunity: Norfolk MSC operations dominated by Maersk Line Ltd (9 vessels) and Crowley (4 ESBs)

MSC civilian contractors operate 20 vessels across fleet replenishment, expeditionary sea bases, sealift, and hospital ships. Commercial fleet concentrated in Jones Act trades: Hawaii (Matson, Pasha), Alaska (Crowley, TOTE), Puerto Rico (TOTE), and coastwise petroleum (OSG, American Shipping, Liberty Maritime).

5.3 Hawaii Trade Opportunities

Matson Navigation: 7 vessels operating LA/Long Beach/Oakland to Honolulu. Weekly service requires full terminal support, stevedoring, provisioning at both ends.

Pasha Hawaii: 2 geared bulkers (San Diego/Honolulu). Handles automobiles, project cargo, break-bulk alongside containers.

5.4 Alaska Trade Opportunities

Crowley Maritime: 2 Orca-class containers (Seattle/Tacoma to Anchorage). Weekly Alaska Direct service.

TOTE Maritime: 2 RoRo vessels (Tacoma-Anchorage). Competes with Crowley in Alaska market.

5.5 Petroleum/Chemical Opportunities

OSG Ship Management: 6 product tankers (largest Jones Act product tanker fleet). Coastwise refined products - requires bunkering, chandlery, routine maintenance at all major ports.

American Shipping: 4 Aframax crude tankers. Crude oil movements Houston/Louisiana to California refineries.

Liberty Maritime: 2 product tankers (Gulf Coast). Smaller operator, may be more responsive to local service providers.

5.6 RRF/NDRF Activation Support

3 RRF RoRo vessels identified (2 Norfolk, 1 Baltimore). MARAD contracts for RRF maintenance (ROS-4 status requires 5-day activation). Opportunities in activation drills, preservation work, emergency response support.

6. Data Sources & Methodology

6.1 Data Sources

  • MARAD US-Flag Fleet Dashboard - Primary source for commercial vessel registry
  • MSC Ship Inventory (www.msc.usff.navy.mil) - Official Military Sealift Command fleet list
  • MARAD NDRF/RRF Inventory - Ready Reserve Force monthly reports
  • Industry databases - Lloyd's Register, MarineTraffic, vessel operator websites

6.2 Vessel Selection Criteria

  • US flag registry (excludes foreign-flag, even if US-owned)
  • Self-propelled vessels ≥1,000 GT (excludes barges, small craft)
  • Operational or Ready Reserve status (excludes laid-up/scrapping except where noted)
  • Excludes Navy commissioned vessels (USS prefix), Coast Guard cutters
  • Includes all MSC vessels regardless of tonnage (EPFs at 1,615 GT included)
