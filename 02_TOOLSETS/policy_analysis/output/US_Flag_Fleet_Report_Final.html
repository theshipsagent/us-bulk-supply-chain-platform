Executive Summary

Fleet Composition: 78 US-flag vessels analyzed - 20 MSC (25.6%), 58 commercial (74.4%), 3 RRF (3.8%)

Norfolk Concentration: 18 vessels based (23.1% of fleet) - 14 MSC, 4 commercial

Key Operators: Maersk Line Ltd (MSC prime), Matson (Hawaii), Crowley (Alaska/ESB), OSG (product tankers)

Business Opportunity: Norfolk MSC operations dominated by Maersk Line Ltd (9 vessels) and Crowley (4 ESBs)

MSC civilian contractors operate 20 vessels across fleet replenishment, expeditionary sea bases, sealift, and hospital ships. Commercial fleet concentrated in Jones Act trades: Hawaii (Matson, Pasha), Alaska (Crowley, TOTE), Puerto Rico (TOTE), and coastwise petroleum (OSG, American Shipping, Liberty Maritime).

6. NDRF Ghost Fleets & Ready Reserve Force

Three active NDRF anchorages maintain approximately 40 vessels in reserve status for national defense mobilization. Ready Reserve Force vessels can be activated in 5-10 days.

6.1 James River Reserve Fleet (Fort Eustis, Virginia)

Location: 37°07′13″N 76°38′47″W - James River at Fort Eustis

Current Status: 4 vessels remaining (down from 800+ at Cold War peak)

Established: 1925 - oldest NDRF site

Environmental Concerns: Vessels hold estimated 13 million gallons oil/fuel, systematic reduction underway

6.2 Beaumont Reserve Fleet (Sabine-Neches River, Texas)

Location: 30°1′30″N 94°0′39″W - Neches River, McFadden Bend Cutoff

Current Status: 27 vessels (as of July 2025)

Notable: Only Gulf Coast NDRF anchorage, 200+ vessel capacity

Composition: Primarily RoRo ships in ROS-5 status (10-man maintenance crews)

Known Vessels: CAPE VINCENT (T-AKR-9666), CAPE TAYLOR, CAPE TEXAS, CAPE TRINITY, USNS ALGOL, USNS ANTARES

6.3 Suisun Bay Reserve Fleet (Benicia, California)

Location: Suisun Bay, Benicia CA - only West Coast NDRF site

Current Status: 9 vessels remaining (as of 2025)

Historical Peak: 340 vessels (1952)

Environmental Cleanup: Significant reduction due to Bay pollution concerns, ongoing MARAD disposal program

6.4 Ready Reserve Force - National Overview

Total RRF Fleet: 51 vessels nationwide

Activation Capability: 5-10 days (ROS-4/ROS-5 status)

Recent Investment: $6.2 billion MARAD contracts over 10 years (July 2025)

Strategic Positioning: Vessels outported at major US ports for rapid deployment

7.3 Why Brownsville?

  • Deep Draft Access: Brownsville Ship Channel accommodates largest naval vessels
  • Gulf Location: Economical towing from both coasts, avoids Panama Canal transit
  • Specialized Infrastructure: Largest ship dismantling facilities in US (established 1995)
  • Environmental Compliance: EU accreditation, advanced hazmat processing
  • Economic Impact: $3 billion Texas economy, 51,500 jobs statewide

7.4 MARAD Ship Disposal Program

MARAD serves as US Government disposal agent for merchant vessels ≥1,500 GT no longer useful for defense missions. Disposal priorities focus on vessels presenting highest environmental risk. Between 2000-2025, over 150 obsolete vessels removed from NDRF ghost fleets.

Business Opportunity: Towing contracts from NDRF anchorages to Brownsville range $500K-$3M depending on vessel size. Maritime service providers in Norfolk/Hampton Roads positioned for James River fleet towing contracts as ghost fleet reduction accelerates.

8. Data Sources & Methodology

8.1 Data Sources

  • MARAD US-Flag Fleet Dashboard - Primary source for commercial vessel registry
  • MSC Ship Inventory (www.msc.usff.navy.mil) - Official Military Sealift Command fleet list
  • MARAD NDRF/RRF Inventory - Ready Reserve Force monthly reports
  • Naval Vessel Register - Official US Navy vessel status database
  • Industry databases - MarineTraffic, VesselFinder, vessel operator websites

8.2 Vessel Selection Criteria

  • US flag registry (excludes foreign-flag, even if US-owned)
  • Self-propelled vessels ≥1,000 GT (excludes barges, small craft)
  • Operational, Ready Reserve, or recently decommissioned status
  • Excludes Navy commissioned vessels (USS prefix), Coast Guard cutters
  • Includes all MSC vessels regardless of tonnage (EPFs at 1,615 GT included)
