## Root Cause Analysis

### **What Happened:**

**Brazil steel exports to Lower Mississippi near-total collapse (99% decline) + Japan major decline (85%)**, spread across hundreds of consignees and multiple steel mills.

### **Why It Happened:**

#### **Hypothesis 1: Section 232 Steel Tariffs** ⭐ (Most Likely)

**Background:**
- March 2018: Section 232 steel tariffs imposed (25% on steel imports)
- 2020-2022: Some exemptions granted for select countries
- 2023-2025: Enforcement continues, exemptions expire

**Evidence:**
- **Brazil 99% decline:** Section 232 likely made Brazilian steel uncompetitive
- **Japan 85% decline:** Similar tariff impact
- **Vietnam +436% growth:** Potential tariff circumvention or exemptions
- **Taiwan +41% growth:** May have favorable tariff treatment
- **China minimal (1.3%):** Already heavily tariffed pre-Section 232

**Tariff Impact by Country:**
| Country | Likely Tariff Status | 2023-2025 Trend |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Brazil | 25% tariff | -99% (uncompetitive) |
| Japan | 25% tariff | -85% (severely impacted) |
| Turkey | 25% tariff | -82% (severely impacted) |
| Vietnam | Lower/exempt? | +436% (gaining share) |
| Mexico | USMCA exempt? | +11% (stable/slight growth) |
| South Korea | Quota arrangement? | -22% (moderate decline) |

**Conclusion:** Tariffs making Brazilian/Japanese steel uncompetitive vs domestic US steel or exempted countries (Vietnam, Mexico, Taiwan).

#### **Hypothesis 2: Domestic US Steel Production Increases**

**Rationale:**
- Section 232 designed to boost US steel production
- Domestic mills expanded capacity 2018-2025
- Lower Mississippi importers may have switched to domestic suppliers

**Evidence:**
- Steel imports declined but **shipments increased** (3,064 → 3,565 → 2,365)
  - Suggests demand still exists, but smaller average shipment sizes
  - Consistent with domestic suppliers (smaller, more frequent deliveries)
- Tubular Goods GREW +55% (oil/gas sector demand strong)
  - Some steel products still imported (not total substitution)

**Validation Needed:** Check US domestic steel production 2023-2025 (expected growth).

#### **Hypothesis 3: Brazilian Steel Industry Restructuring**

**Brazil near-total collapse (99%) is extreme:**
- Suggests Brazilian steel mills stopped targeting US market
- Possible explanations:
  - **Domestic Brazil demand increased** (infrastructure investment)
  - **Shifted to other export markets** (Latin America, Europe, Asia)
  - **Section 232 tariffs made US exports unprofitable**
  - **Brazilian steel production decreased** (operational issues)

**Validation Needed:** Check Brazilian steel export data (where did 1.6M tons go?).

#### **Hypothesis 4: Japanese Steel Export Strategy**

**Japan 85% decline (1.8M → 285K tons):**
- Japanese steel mills historically major US exporters
- Section 232 likely made US market less attractive
- Possible explanations:
  - **Shifted to other Asian markets** (China, Southeast Asia)
  - **US-Japan trade agreements** expired or modified
  - **Domestic Japan steel demand increased**
  - **Japanese mills diversified away from US reliance**

**Validation Needed:** Check Japanese steel export data by destination.
