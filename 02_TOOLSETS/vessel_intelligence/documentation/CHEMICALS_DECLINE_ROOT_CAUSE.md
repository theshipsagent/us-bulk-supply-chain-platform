# Chemicals Decline Investigation: Root Cause Identified

**Date:** February 22, 2026
**Investigation:** 91% Chemicals Import Decline (2023-2025)
**Status:** ROOT CAUSE CONFIRMED

---

## Executive Summary

**The 91% chemicals decline is NOT a broad market trend - it's a single-company supply chain decision.**

**ROOT CAUSE:** Celanese Europe GmbH stopped importing MIBK/MIBC chemical intermediates from Mexico through New Orleans.

- **Celanese Impact:** 3.6M tons (2023) → 5.7K tons (2025) = **99.8% decline**
- **Represents:** 65% of total 2023 chemical imports to Lower Mississippi
- **Products:** MIBK (1.7M tons) + MIBC (1.5M tons) = 3.2M tons
- **Origin:** Mexico (4.4M → 0.02M tons) - Celanese Mexico facilities
- **Carrier:** Ace Tankers (4.5M → 0.02M tons) - dedicated chemical tanker fleet

**This is a business intelligence finding, not a data quality issue or broad market collapse.**

## Root Cause Analysis

### **What Happened:**
Celanese Europe stopped importing MIBK/MIBC chemical intermediates from their Mexican facilities through New Orleans starting in 2024.

### **Why It Happened (Hypotheses):**

#### Hypothesis 1: **Supply Chain Rerouting** (Most Likely)
- **Celanese shifted imports to Houston** (closer to Texas chemical hub)
- **Rationale:** Houston has larger chemical infrastructure, shorter ocean transit from Mexico
- **Validation Needed:** Check Houston import manifest for Celanese MIBK/MIBC increases in 2024-2025

#### Hypothesis 2: **Domestic Production Substitution**
- **Celanese expanded US production capacity** (eliminated need for Mexican imports)
- **Rationale:** Trade tensions, nearshoring, domestic chemical production incentives
- **Validation Needed:** Check Celanese corporate reports for US facility expansions

#### Hypothesis 3: **Direct-to-Customer Distribution**
- **Celanese restructured supply chain** (Mexico direct to end customers, bypassing New Orleans)
- **Rationale:** Reduced logistics costs, faster delivery
- **Validation Needed:** Check if MIBK/MIBC imports declined at ALL US ports or just Lower Mississippi

#### Hypothesis 4: **Mexican Facility Operational Changes**
- **Celanese reduced/shut down Mexican MIBK/MIBC production**
- **Rationale:** Plant closures, operational efficiency, product line changes
- **Validation Needed:** Check Celanese Mexico facility operational status

### **Why It's NOT Data Quality Issues:**

✅ **Consistent Across All Dimensions:**
- Consignee (Celanese 99.8% decline)
- Product (MIBK/MIBC 99.9% decline)
- Origin (Mexico 99.6% decline)
