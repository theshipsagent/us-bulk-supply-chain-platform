## Notes
The Quebec Gatineau Railway is a shortline railway owned by Genesee & Wyoming Inc., operating the 358-mile former Canadian Pacific Railway line between Quebec City, Trois-Rivières, Laval, Lachute, and Gatineau. The railway primarily utilizes former CPR infrastructure to provide freight services along the north shore of the St. Lawrence and Ottawa rivers. A significant portion (26%) of QGRY's carloads are related to either the Port of Trois-Rivières or Quebec City, highlighting the railway's importance in port operations. Junction points enable the exchange of commodities such as forest products and intermodal containers, supporting the railway's role in Quebec's regional supply chains. The QGRY is part of Genesee & Wyoming Canada (GWCI), headquartered in Montreal.
