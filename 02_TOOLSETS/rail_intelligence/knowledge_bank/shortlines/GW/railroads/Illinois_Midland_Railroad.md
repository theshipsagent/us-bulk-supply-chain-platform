## Notes
The IMRR is the successor to the historic Chicago & Illinois Midland Railway, which was once a Class I railroad specializing in coal hauling. Genesee & Wyoming acquired the railroad in February 1996 and renamed it the Illinois and Midland Railroad. Despite no longer being a Class I, the IMRR retains substantial infrastructure and capabilities from its Class I heritage. The railroad continues to move primarily coal, though it has diversified into agricultural products, building materials, and waste products. The IMRR's strategic location in central Illinois and multiple Class I connections make it an important regional carrier.
