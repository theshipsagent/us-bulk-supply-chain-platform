## Notes
The PNWR is a Class II railroad (not Class III) and one of Genesee & Wyoming's larger operations. The railroad is a wholly owned subsidiary of G&W, acquired through the RailAmerica purchase. PNWR's tracks lie entirely within Oregon (with one interchange in Washington), extending from Astoria to Portland along the Columbia River and from Portland to Eugene through the fertile Willamette Valley. The railroad handles significant log traffic, moving logs from a major import-export firm in Rainier across the entire system to an interchange at Eugene. In 1997, PNWR acquired the "Astoria Line" from Burlington Northern, bringing numerous paper, lumber, and chemical customers onto the system. PNWR celebrated 30 years of operations in recent years and continues to be a major Oregon freight carrier.
