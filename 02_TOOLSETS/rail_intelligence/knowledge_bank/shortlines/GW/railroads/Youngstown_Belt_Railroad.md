## Notes
The Youngstown Belt Railroad began operations in 1997, mainly on ex-Erie Railroad trackage, known as the "Lordstown Cluster," which was acquired from Conrail in 1996. The railroad is part of the Ohio Central Railroad System acquired by Genesee & Wyoming Inc. in 2008. In the Youngstown area, OCR operations include the Youngstown Belt Railroad, Youngstown & Austintown Railroad, Warren & Trumbull Railroad, and Mahoning Valley Railway Company, collectively operating over 20 miles primarily serving the steel industry. These railroads interchange with CSX and Norfolk Southern, providing critical first and last mile service to Youngstown's historic steel industry. The ex-Erie trackage includes branches serving major steel operations including the U.S. Steel Ohio Works.
