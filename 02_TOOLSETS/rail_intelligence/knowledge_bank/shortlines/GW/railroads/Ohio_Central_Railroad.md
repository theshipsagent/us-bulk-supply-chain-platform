## Notes
The Ohio Central Railroad System is a network of ten short line railroads operating 500 miles of track across Ohio and western Pennsylvania, headquartered in Coshocton, Ohio. Genesee & Wyoming acquired the entire Ohio Central System from founder Jerry Jacobson for $219 million, effective October 1, 2008. The system primarily serves the steel industry, along with coal and solid waste transportation. The Columbus and Ohio River Railroad is a major component, running approximately 160 miles from Columbus to Mingo Junction near the Pennsylvania border, plus an 85-mile line between Newark and Cambridge. The Youngstown area operations (Youngstown Belt, Youngstown & Austintown, Warren & Trumbull, and Mahoning Valley) collectively serve the historic Youngstown steel district. The OCRS interchanges with CSX, Norfolk Southern, and Wheeling and Lake Erie Railway, providing customers with multiple routing options and access to the broader rail network.
