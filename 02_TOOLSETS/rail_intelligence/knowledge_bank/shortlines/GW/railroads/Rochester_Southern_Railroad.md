## Notes
The Rochester and Southern Railroad started in 1986 when the B&O sold off its Buffalo and Rochester branches. The trackage was purchased by Genesee & Wyoming Inc. and split into two railroads: the Buffalo and Pittsburgh Railroad and the Rochester and Southern Railroad. The RSR is a Class III shortline operating 101 track miles in Monroe County and surrounding areas of New York. The railroad is particularly notable for its heavy salt traffic, operating long trains of salt cars that are a signature feature of RSR operations. The railroad serves a variety of customers with mixed freight while maintaining its focus on bulk salt movements. RSR's multiple Class I connections provide flexibility for customers to reach broader markets.
