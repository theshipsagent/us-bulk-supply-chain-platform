## Notes
The Providence & Worcester Railroad is a Class II regional railroad (not Class III) operating 612 miles of track across New England and into New York. Genesee & Wyoming purchased P&W in 2016 for $126 million, adding this substantial regional operation to its Northeast portfolio. The acquisition was completed without significant changes to operations. P&W provides crucial connections between New England industries and four Class I railroads (CN, CPKC, CSX, and NS), making it a strategic link in regional freight movements. The railroad's diverse commodity base and extensive network make it one of G&W's most important New England properties.
