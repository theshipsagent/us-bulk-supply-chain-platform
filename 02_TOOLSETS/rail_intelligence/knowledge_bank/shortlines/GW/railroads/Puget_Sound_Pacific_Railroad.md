## Notes
The Puget Sound and Pacific Railroad is a Class III shortline serving the Kitsap Peninsula, Grays Harbor County, and Centralia, Washington. Genesee & Wyoming acquired PSAP through the 2012 RailAmerica purchase, with STB approval in December 2012. The railroad has undergone significant growth, with the line from Centralia to Grays Harbor now handling unit grain, soybean, soda ash, auto, garbage, and military trains in addition to regular local traffic. The railroad's main commodities historically were timber products, garbage, and chemicals, but the traffic base has diversified substantially. PSAP continues to invest in infrastructure, including planned railroad crossing improvements in Grays Harbor to enhance safety and operations.
