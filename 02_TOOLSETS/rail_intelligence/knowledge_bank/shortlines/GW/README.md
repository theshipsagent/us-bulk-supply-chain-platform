# Genesee & Wyoming Short Line Railroad Knowledge Bank

## Overview

This knowledge bank contains comprehensive profiles of Genesee & Wyoming Inc.'s short line and regional railroad operations across North America. Genesee & Wyoming is the largest short line railroad holding company in North America, operating over 100 railroads across 42 U.S. states and four Canadian provinces with more than 13,000 track-miles.

## About Genesee & Wyoming Inc.

**Company Background:**
- Founded: 1899 (original Genesee & Wyoming Railroad)
- Current Ownership: Brookfield Infrastructure Partners, L.P. and GIC (privatized in 2019)
- Headquarters: Darien, Connecticut
- Scale: 122 railroads globally (113 in North America)
- Employees: Approximately 7,300
- Customers: Approximately 3,000
- Track Miles: Over 13,000 in North America

**Business Model:**
Genesee & Wyoming railroads provide critical "first and last mile" connections between Class I railroads and local industries. The company specializes in:
- Short line and regional freight railroad operations
- Industrial switching services (via Rail Link, Inc. subsidiary)
- Port terminal operations (30+ ports)
- Transload facilities
- Rail-ferry service (U.S. Southeast to Mexico via CG Railway joint venture)
- Railcar switching and repair

**Regional Structure:**
G&W organizes its North American operations into regional groups:
- **Northeast Region:** New England, New York, Pennsylvania
- **Mid-Atlantic/Southeast Region:** Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Carolinas, Tennessee
- **Midwest Region:** Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Michigan
- **West Region:** Texas, Arkansas, Kansas, Colorado, Oregon, Washington, Arizona, California
- **Canada Region:** Quebec, Ontario, Maritime provinces, Alberta

## Known Limitations

1. **Incomplete Coverage:** While this knowledge bank profiles 30+ major G&W railroads with detailed information, Genesee & Wyoming operates 113 railroads in North America. Many smaller properties and switching operations may not have individual profiles due to limited publicly available information.

2. **Data Currency:** Railroad operations, ownership, and routes can change. This snapshot represents data as of February 2026. Users should verify current operations with G&W directly for time-sensitive decisions.

3. **Proprietary Information:** Specific customer names, traffic volumes, and financial data are often proprietary and not included in public sources.

4. **Geographic Detail:** Exact junction points and facility locations may not be fully documented for all railroads.

5. **International Operations:** This knowledge bank focuses on North American (U.S. and Canadian) operations. G&W's UK and European operations (Freightliner, etc.) are not covered.

## Comparison with Other Short Line Operators

This G&W knowledge bank is designed to complement similar profiles of other major short line operators:
- **Watco Companies:** ~40 short lines, strong Midwest/Plains presence
- **Patriot Rail:** ~20 short lines, diverse geographic coverage
- **R.J. Corman:** ~10+ railroads, Midwest/Southeast focus
- **Anacostia Rail Holdings:** ~10 railroads, regional clusters
- **OmniTRAX:** ~10 railroads, focus on bulk commodities

G&W is significantly larger than all other North American short line holding companies, with the most extensive geographic reach and the greatest diversity of railroad operations.
