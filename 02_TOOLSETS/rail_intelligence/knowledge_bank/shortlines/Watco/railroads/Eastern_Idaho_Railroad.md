## Notes
The Eastern Idaho Railroad (reporting marks EIRR) began operations on November 21, 1993, as a collection of two disconnected clusters of former Union Pacific branches. A subsidiary of Watco, EIRR operates two separate segments that move more than 35,000 carloads per year to the Union Pacific. Base of operations is in Twin Falls. The EIRR is a critical transportation link for Idaho's potato industry, one of the state's most important agricultural sectors, along with grain transportation. This is one of four Watco railroads operating in Idaho and was among Watco's earliest acquisitions in 1993.
