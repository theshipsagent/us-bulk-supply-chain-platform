## Notes
Mission Mountain Railroad is a subsidiary of Watco, operating 26 miles of track between Stryker, Trego, Fortine, and Eureka, Montana. Watco moves any commodity on this railroad, but primarily focuses on forest products and grain. In 2004, Watco started operations of the Mission Mountain Railroad in Montana. The railroad serves northwestern Montana's forest products industry and agricultural operations, providing connections to BNSF Railway for movement to larger markets. The MMT is one of two Watco railroads in Montana, along with the Yellowstone Valley Railroad (YSVR).
