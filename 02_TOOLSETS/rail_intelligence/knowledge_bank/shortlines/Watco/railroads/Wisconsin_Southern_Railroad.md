## Notes
The Wisconsin and Southern Railroad (reporting mark WSOR) is a Class II regional railroad in Southern Wisconsin and Northeastern Illinois currently operated by Watco. On November 29, 2011, it was announced that WSOR would be acquired by Watco, with the deal closing on January 1, 2012. The railroad now owns and operates 837 miles, jointly owned by Wisconsin and the counties in which the railroad serves. Within Wisconsin, WSOR connects with four western Class I railroads: BNSF Railway, Canadian National Railway, Canadian Pacific Kansas City, and Union Pacific Railroad. Through trackage rights over Metra, WSOR accesses Chicago to connect with the two eastern Class I railroads, CSX Transportation and Norfolk Southern Railway. This unique connectivity to all six Class I carriers makes WSOR strategically important. It operates former Chicago, Milwaukee, St. Paul and Pacific Railroad (Milwaukee Road) and Chicago and North Western Railway (C&NW) trackage, mostly acquired by the state of Wisconsin in the 1980s. The WSOR is headquartered in Madison, Wisconsin and operates both Northern and Southern divisions serving agricultural, industrial, and manufacturing sectors throughout the region. This is Watco's largest operation by route miles.
