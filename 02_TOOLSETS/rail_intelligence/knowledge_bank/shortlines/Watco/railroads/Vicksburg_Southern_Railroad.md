## Notes
The Vicksburg Southern Railroad (reporting mark VSOR) is a short line railroad in and near Vicksburg, Mississippi, United States, owned by Watco. It connects with the Kansas City Southern Railway's Meridian Speedway in Vicksburg, and stretches north to Redwood and south to Cedars. The railroad operates 36.58 track miles with a track capacity of 286 cars, and Watco began operations in 2006. On this railroad, Watco primarily moves products in the energy and paper industries. Historically, the line was once part of a main line between Memphis and New Orleans, completed by the Louisville, New Orleans and Texas Railway, a predecessor of the Illinois Central Railroad, in about 1884. The newly created Vicksburg Southern leased the lines from Kansas City Southern Railway (KCS) in January 2006. The railroad's location in Vicksburg provides strategic access to the Mississippi River corridor.
