## Notes
Watco welcomed the Verdigris Southern Railroad (VESO) as its 46th short line railroad in 2024. The 3-mile line serves the Port of Inola in Oklahoma. The line became operational in April 2024 with Sofidel, a tissue paper company, being the initial customer, with the primary commodity being raw pulp. The VESO currently provides switching and interchange services for one customer. More than four miles of new track were built to connect the port's customers to Union Pacific. The Port owns the trackage, which is then leased to VESO. The goal is to provide service five days a week from a dedicated VESO team. Although small in route miles, the VESO represents significant investment in new infrastructure and positions the Port of Inola for future growth with rail service capabilities.
