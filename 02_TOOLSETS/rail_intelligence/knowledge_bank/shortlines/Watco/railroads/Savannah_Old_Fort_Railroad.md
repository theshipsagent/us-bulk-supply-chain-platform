## Notes
The Savannah and Old Fort Railway is a 6.5-mile short-line railroad running within Savannah, Georgia. Watco moves any commodity on this railroad, but primarily focuses on sulfuric acid, sulfur, gypsum, pulpboard, wood pellets, and petroleum. The railroad operates in Savannah's industrial corridor with connections to both major eastern Class I carriers (NS and CSX). The SVHO's location in Savannah provides strategic access to one of the East Coast's major ports, making it important for both domestic and international commodity movements. The railroad specializes in hazardous materials handling, particularly sulfuric acid, requiring specialized equipment and safety protocols.
