## Notes
The Great Northwest Railroad (GRNW), a wholly owned subsidiary of Watco Companies, operates a Class III short line in eastern Washington and western Idaho, crossing the state line at Clarkston. The GRNW operates freight service from a connection with UP and BNSF at Ayer Junction, WA to Riparia, WA (15.1 miles via trackage rights over UP), and then 72 miles to Lewiston, ID. The Camas Prairie Railroad Company was formed in 1909 by the Northern Pacific Railroad (now BNSF) and the Oregon Washington Railroad and Navigation Company (now UP). The railroad was operated jointly until April 18, 1998, when BNSF and UP sold their interests to a new short line, Camas Prairie Railnet. Watco Companies purchased the line in 2004, renaming it the GRNW.
