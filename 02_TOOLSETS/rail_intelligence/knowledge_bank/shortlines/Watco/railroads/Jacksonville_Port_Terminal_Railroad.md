## Notes
The Jacksonville Port Terminal Railroad serves the Jacksonville Port Authority with ten miles of track, running west from the Tallyrand Marine Terminal on the St. Johns River to an interchange with CSX and Norfolk Southern northeast of downtown Jacksonville. On March 8, 2017, Watco bought the railroad and renamed it the Jacksonville Port Terminal Railroad. The JXPT provides critical rail service connecting the port's marine facilities with Class I rail networks, handling diverse commodities including automobiles, chemicals, farm and food products, intermodal containers, and pulp and paper products. The railroad is strategically important for Jacksonville's port operations and international trade.
