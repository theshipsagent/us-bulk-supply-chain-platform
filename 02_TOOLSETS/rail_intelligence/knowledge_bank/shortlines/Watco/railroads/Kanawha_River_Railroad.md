## Notes
The Kanawha River Railroad (reporting mark KNWA) is a common-carrier railroad in the United States and a subsidiary of Watco that leases 359.12 miles of track in Ohio and West Virginia from Norfolk Southern. The railroad was formed in 2016 to lease and operate Norfolk Southern's West Virginia Secondary. The system operates along a corridor extending approximately 250 miles, connecting Columbus, Ohio, to Charleston, West Virginia, and further into the Gauley River valley, terminating at Enon, West Virginia. The system has greatly improved service over this former New York Central line that had witnessed decreasing traffic under NS. Primary commodities include coal, chemicals, cement, aggregates, and metals. The railroad uses a fleet of rebuilt EMD locomotives including SD45s, SD60s, GP39-2s, and SD60Ms for various operations including helper service on coal trains.
