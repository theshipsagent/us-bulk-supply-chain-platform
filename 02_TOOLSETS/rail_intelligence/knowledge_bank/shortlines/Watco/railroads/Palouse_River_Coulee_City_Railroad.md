## Notes
The Palouse River & Coulee City Railroad (PCC) is the longest short line freight rail system in Washington State, serving five eastern Washington counties: Grant, Lincoln, Spokane, Adams, and Whitman. In 2007, the State of Washington purchased three short-line railroad tracks consisting of the CW, P&L and PV Hooper rail lines, encompassing approximately 300 miles of track. Watco Companies now operates portions of the PCC system under contract with the state. The railroad moves primarily wheat, lentils, and barley. This short line system provides farmers and growers with the ability to ship their agricultural products via rail from remote locations, connecting with mainline Class I railroads, barges, and container ships for distribution throughout the world. The PCC is critical infrastructure for eastern Washington's agricultural economy.
