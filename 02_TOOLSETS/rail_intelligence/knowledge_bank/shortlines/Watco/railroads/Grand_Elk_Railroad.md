## Notes
The Grand Elk Railroad (reporting mark GDLK) is a Class III railroad operating in Indiana and Michigan, owned by Watco. The company leases a 123-mile line from Norfolk Southern running south from Grand Rapids, Michigan, through Kalamazoo, Michigan, to Elkhart, Indiana. Operations began on March 8, 2009. The line uses former Grand Rapids and Indiana Railroad mainline (Grand Rapids to Kalamazoo) and former Lake Shore & Michigan Southern branch (Kalamazoo to Elkhart). Watco expanded to Michigan's Upper Peninsula in 2021 when Canadian National divested former Wisconsin Central Ltd. lines, including lines from Trout Lake to Munising and to White Pine. The railroad connects with three Class I railroads and three short lines including Marquette Rail, Grand Rapids Eastern Railroad, and Michigan Southern Railroad.
