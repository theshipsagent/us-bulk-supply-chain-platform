## Notes
Kaw River Railroad operates in Kansas as part of Watco's extensive network in the state. The railroad takes its name from the Kaw River (Kansas River), which flows through northeastern Kansas. The KAW is one of several Watco railroads serving Kansas's important agricultural industry, providing connections between local grain elevators and processors to Class I carriers. Watco is headquartered in Pittsburg, Kansas, and has deep roots in the state with multiple railroad operations including KO (Kansas & Oklahoma), SKOL (South Kansas & Oklahoma), and KAW (Kaw River Railroad).
