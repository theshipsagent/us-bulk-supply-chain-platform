**Source:** Web search results

## Tri-Coastal Network

CN is the only rail network in North America connecting three coasts:
- Pacific (Vancouver, Prince Rupert)
- Atlantic (Halifax)
- Gulf of Mexico (New Orleans, Mobile)

## Major East-West Corridor

**Transcontinental Mainline:**
Halifax, NS → Montreal, QC → Toronto, ON → Winnipeg, MB → Saskatoon, SK → Edmonton, AB → Jasper, AB → Prince George, BC → Vancouver, BC

**Key Features:**
- Grain corridor (Prairies to ports)
- Intermodal (trans-Pacific trade)
- Potash export route
- Forest products

## North-South Corridor (Ex-Illinois Central)

**Route:** Chicago, IL → Memphis, TN → Jackson, MS → Baton Rouge, LA → New Orleans, LA

**Branch to Mobile:** Meridian, MS → Mobile, AL

**Key Features:**
- Grain export to Gulf
- Intermodal to/from Gulf ports
- Chemicals (Gulf Coast corridor access)
- Auto assembly plants (Mississippi, Alabama)

## Western Canada Routes

**Edmonton-Vancouver:**
- Via Jasper (Canadian Rockies)
- Via Kamloops
- Double-stack intermodal
- Grain, potash, forest products

**Prince Rupert Route:**
- Edmonton → Prince George → Prince Rupert
- Fastest ocean route to Asia
- Grain export, intermodal

**Calgary-Vancouver:**
- Via Kamloops
- Potash, grain, intermodal

## Central Canada Routes

**Toronto-Montreal Corridor:**
- High-density freight and passenger (VIA Rail)
- Automotive, intermodal, manifest

**Winnipeg Hub:**
- East-west transcontinental junction
- Grain origins
- Intermodal gateway

## Eastern Canada Routes

**Montreal-Halifax:**
- Atlantic gateway
- Grain export, intermodal (Europe trade)
- Forest products

**Montreal-Thunder Bay:**
- Great Lakes access
- Grain to lake vessels

## U.S. Midwest Routes

**Chicago Gateway:**
- Multiple routes into Chicago
- Interchange with all Class I carriers
- Major intermodal terminal

**Wisconsin/Minnesota:**
- Duluth-Superior (Great Lakes grain)
- Iron Range access (historical)
