## Company Overview

**Headquarters:** Montreal, Quebec, Canada
**Route Miles:** Approximately 18,600-20,000 miles
**Service Territory:** All Canadian provinces (except PEI and Newfoundland) + 16 U.S. states
**Stock Symbol:** TSX: CNR, NYSE: CNI

## Tri-Coastal Network

CN is the only transcontinental rail network in North America connecting to three coasts:
- **Pacific Coast:** Vancouver, BC and Prince Rupert, BC
- **Atlantic Coast:** Halifax, Nova Scotia
- **Gulf Coast:** New Orleans, Louisiana and Mobile, Alabama

## Key Network Features

### Ex-Illinois Central (1998 Acquisition)
CN acquired Illinois Central Railroad connecting:
- Vancouver/Halifax network to Chicago
- Chicago to New Orleans corridor
- Mississippi River Valley access
- Gulf Coast port connections

### Core East-West Mainline
- Halifax, NS → Montreal → Toronto → Winnipeg → Edmonton → Vancouver
- Transcontinental grain corridor
- Potash export route
- Forest products to export

### North-South Corridor
- Chicago → Memphis → Jackson, MS → New Orleans
- Ex-IC mainline
- Grain export to Gulf
- Intermodal to/from Gulf ports

## 2026 Outlook

- Capital spending: C$2.8 billion (down C$500M from 2025)
- Flat volume growth expected
- Earnings growth slightly faster than volumes
- Tariff headwinds (35% on non-CUSMA goods from U.S.)
- Strong grain movements continue
