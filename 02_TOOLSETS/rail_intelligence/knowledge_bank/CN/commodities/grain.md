# Canadian National — Grain & Fertilizers

**Source:** Web search results
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

Grain and fertilizers represent 20% of CN's consolidated revenue (2024). In the 2024-2025 crop year (August 2024-July 2025), CN and CPKC moved 58.6 million metric tonnes of Canadian grain and grain products – 22% higher than the previous 3-year average.

## Commodities

**Grain:**
- Wheat (spring wheat, durum, winter wheat)
- Canola
- Barley
- Oats
- Soybeans (limited, southern regions)
- Corn (limited, Ontario)
- Pulse crops (peas, lentils)

**Fertilizers:**
- Potash (potassium chloride) — major commodity
- Nitrogen fertilizers
- Phosphate
- Blended fertilizers

## Source Regions

### Canadian Prairies (Primary Grain Origin)
- Saskatchewan (largest grain producer)
- Alberta
- Manitoba
- Export-oriented production

### Fertilizer Sources
- Saskatchewan (potash mines — world's largest reserves)
- Alberta (nitrogen production)

## Export Destinations

**Pacific Ports:**
- Vancouver, BC (grain terminals)
- Prince Rupert, BC (grain terminal)
- Destinations: Asia (China, Japan, South Korea, Southeast Asia)

**Atlantic Port:**
- Halifax, NS (grain terminal)
- Destinations: Europe, Middle East, North Africa

**Gulf Coast Ports:**
- New Orleans, LA
- Destinations: Latin America, Asia (via Panama Canal)

**Thunder Bay, ON:**
- Great Lakes transshipment to lower lakes/St. Lawrence

## Service Characteristics

- Unit train service (100-120 car trains)
- Shuttle train service to export terminals
- Grain hopper fleet (covered hoppers)
- High-velocity seasonal movements (post-harvest)

## Potash Transportation

- 41.5% of Saskatchewan potash moves by rail to West Coast
- Unit trains from mines to Vancouver/Prince Rupert
- Export to global markets (fertilizer for agriculture worldwide)
