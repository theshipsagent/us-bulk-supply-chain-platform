## Overview

Intermodal is CN's largest revenue segment at 22% of consolidated revenue (2024). CN operates intermodal terminals across Canada and the U.S., connecting three coasts (Pacific, Atlantic, Gulf).

## Major Terminals

### Canada
- **Vancouver, BC:** Pacific gateway (Asia trade)
- **Prince Rupert, BC:** Fast port to Asia
- **Edmonton, AB:** Prairie hub
- **Winnipeg, MB:** Central Canada hub
- **Toronto (Brampton), ON:** GTA distribution
- **Montreal (Taschereau Yard), QC:** Major intermodal hub
- **Halifax, NS:** Atlantic gateway

### United States
- **Chicago, IL:** Major U.S. gateway
- **Memphis, TN:** Mid-South hub
- **New Orleans, LA:** Gulf Coast

## Service Corridors

**Trans-Pacific Trade:**
- Asia → Prince Rupert/Vancouver → North America
- Fastest ocean route to North America via Prince Rupert

**Transcontinental:**
- Vancouver/Prince Rupert → Winnipeg → Toronto → Montreal
- East-west Canadian corridor

**North-South:**
- Chicago → Memphis → New Orleans (ex-IC route)
- Toronto/Montreal → Chicago → Gulf Coast

**Transatlantic:**
- Halifax → Montreal → Toronto → Chicago
- Europe imports via Halifax

## Equipment & Operations

- Double-stack trains
- Domestic containers (53-foot)
- ISO international containers
- Well cars, spine cars
