**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

## CN Port Network

CN serves three coasts with major port access:
- **Pacific:** Vancouver, Prince Rupert
- **Atlantic:** Halifax
- **Gulf of Mexico:** New Orleans, Mobile

## Pacific Ports

### Port of Vancouver, BC
- Major container terminal
- Grain export terminals (multiple)
- Potash export
- Coal export (Ridley Terminals via Prince Rupert)
- Forest products export
- **Destinations:** Asia (China, Japan, South Korea, Southeast Asia)

### Port of Prince Rupert, BC
- Fairview Container Terminal (fastest North America-Asia route)
- Grain terminal
- Coal terminal (Ridley Island)
- **Destinations:** Asia

## Atlantic Port

### Port of Halifax, NS
- Container terminal
- Grain export terminal
- Double-stack service to Montreal, Toronto, Chicago
- **Destinations:** Europe, Middle East, Mediterranean

## Gulf Coast Ports

### Port of New Orleans, LA
- Container terminals
- Grain export
- Steel, chemicals, forest products
- **Destinations:** Latin America, Asia (via Panama Canal), Europe

### Port of Mobile, AL
- Container terminal
- Bulk commodities
- **Destinations:** Various international markets

## Great Lakes Ports

### Thunder Bay, ON
- Grain transshipment to lake vessels
- Lower Great Lakes and St. Lawrence Seaway access

### Duluth-Superior
- Grain export via Great Lakes
- Iron ore (historical)

## Export Commodities

- Grain (wheat, canola, barley)
- Potash
- Coal (metallurgical)
- Forest products (lumber, pulp, paper)
- Containers (manufactured goods)

## Import Commodities

- Containers (consumer goods from Asia)
- Steel
- Automobiles (via Halifax, Vancouver)
