## Class I Railroad Interchanges

### Overview
CSX maintains strategic interchange connections with all major North American Class I railroads, enabling seamless freight movement across the continent.

## Direct Class I Connections

### Norfolk Southern Railway (NS)
**Relationship:** Parallel operations, shared Conrail territory, competitive and cooperative

#### Major Interchange Points
- **Buffalo, NY** - Shared Conrail operations area
- **Cleveland, OH** - Multiple interchange locations
- **Cincinnati, OH** - Major gateway for north-south traffic
- **Louisville, KY** - Strategic interchange
- **Nashville, TN** - Southeast gateway
- **Atlanta, GA** - Major Southeast hub interchange
- **Birmingham, AL** - Southern interchange
- **Washington, DC area** - Multiple connection points
- **Philadelphia, PA** - Conrail shared asset area
- **Detroit, MI** - Shared operations area

#### Traffic Types
- Overhead bridge traffic between eastern and western carriers
- Competitive routing options for shippers
- Coordinated movements in shared territories
- Joint access to customers in Conrail shared areas

### Canadian National Railway (CN)
**Relationship:** International gateway partner, transcontinental connection

#### Major Interchange Points
- **Buffalo, NY** - Primary Canadian gateway via Fort Erie
- **Detroit, MI/Windsor, ON** - Tunnel and bridge connections
- **Sarnia, ON** - Connection point for western Canada traffic
- **Montreal, QC** - Eastern Canadian gateway
- **Port Huron, MI/Sarnia, ON** - St. Clair Tunnel operations

#### Traffic Types
- Import/export traffic to/from Canada
- Transcontinental overhead traffic
- Canadian manufacturing (automotive, forest products)
- Port of Montreal connections
- Western Canada grain and potash movements

### Canadian Pacific Kansas City Railway (CPKC)
**Relationship:** Strategic partnership with new direct connection

#### Myrtlewood, Alabama Gateway (NEW - Effective November 16, 2024)
- **Location:** Myrtlewood, Alabama (near Meridian, MS)
- **CSX Operation:** 93.7 miles from Burkville to Myrtlewood (leased from M&B)
- **CPKC Operation:** 50.4 miles from Myrtlewood to Meridian (purchased)
- **Initial Traffic:** Average of 2 trains per day each direction
- **Agreement Term:** 5-year initial period
- **Strategic Importance:** Direct Class I-to-Class I connection linking Mexico, Texas, and U.S. Southeast

#### Traffic Opportunities
- Mexico-Southeast U.S. traffic lanes
- Texas-Florida movements
- Agricultural products from Mexico
- Automotive traffic (Mexican production)
- Intermodal movements between Mexico and Southeast
- Gulf Coast to Southeast corridors

#### Historical Connections
Prior to Myrtlewood gateway:
- **Chicago, IL** - Via trackage rights and connections
- **Kansas City, MO** - Via other Class I intermediaries
- **Detroit, MI** - Via Canadian operations

### BNSF Railway
**Relationship:** Strategic partnership for transcontinental traffic

#### Interchange Locations
- **Chicago, IL** - Primary gateway (multiple yard connections)
- **Memphis, TN** - Southern gateway via other carriers
- **St. Louis, MO** - Gateway via connecting carriers
- **Kansas City, MO** - Via connecting carriers

#### Collaborative Initiatives
**CSX-BNSF Partnership** for intermodal improvements:
- Focus on transcontinental intermodal movements
- Coordinated service improvements
- Enhanced equipment utilization
- Improved transit times coast-to-coast

#### Traffic Types
- Transcontinental intermodal (Asia imports via West Coast)
- Agricultural products (grain, food products)
- Coal connections
- Merchandise freight
- Automotive traffic

### Union Pacific Railroad (UP)
**Relationship:** Western connection partner

#### Interchange Locations
- **Chicago, IL** - Primary gateway (multiple connections)
- **St. Louis, MO** - Via connecting carriers
- **Memphis, TN** - Southern gateway via connections
- **New Orleans, LA** - Via connections
- **Kansas City, MO** - Via connecting carriers

#### Traffic Coordination
CSX has worked with UP to identify most efficient locations for interchanging traffic, optimizing:
- Transit times
- Equipment cycles
- Service reliability
- Cost efficiency

#### Traffic Types
- West Coast-East Coast intermodal
- Chemical movements (Gulf to East)
- Agricultural products
- Automotive traffic
- Energy products

## Regional and Short Line Connections

### Network Statistics
- **240+ short line and regional railroad connections**
- **160+ line haul carriers**
- **80+ switch carriers**
- Effectively doubles CSX's market reach in route miles

### Strategic Value
- First mile/last mile service to customers
- Access to smaller communities and industrial sites
- Specialized local knowledge
- Flexible switching operations
- Economic impact: $3.7B in CSX revenue with short lines (2024)
- Volume: 1.2 million carloads via short lines (2024)

## International Gateway Operations

### Port of Montreal
- Connection to ocean shipping via St. Lawrence Seaway
- Container and bulk traffic
- Access to European markets
- Partnership with CN for Canadian traffic

### Border Crossings (U.S.-Canada)
- **Buffalo-Fort Erie** - Via International Bridge
- **Detroit-Windsor** - Via tunnel and Ambassador Bridge
- **Port Huron-Sarnia** - Via St. Clair Tunnel
- **Niagara Falls crossings** - Multiple bridge options

## Overhead Bridge Traffic

### Definition
Traffic that originates and terminates off CSX, but moves over CSX lines between interchange points.

### Major Overhead Routes
- **Western carrier to Norfolk Southern** - Via Chicago to eastern interchanges
- **Canadian National to Norfolk Southern** - Via Detroit/Buffalo to eastern points
- **CPKC to Eastern destinations** - Via new Myrtlewood gateway
- **Transcontinental movements** - Connecting western and eastern carriers

### Strategic Importance
- Revenue generation from line-haul movements
- Network utilization
- Competitive routing options for shippers
- Relationship building with connecting carriers

## Competitive Considerations

### Routing Options
Shippers often have routing choices involving:
- Direct single-line service (CSX only)
- Two-line service (CSX + one connection)
- Multi-line service (CSX + multiple connections)
- Competitive routings via Norfolk Southern

### Gateway Competition
Certain gateways offer competitive routing options:
- **Chicago** - Multiple western connections available
- **Memphis** - North-south gateway competition
- **Buffalo** - Canadian gateway options
- **Detroit** - Canadian routing alternatives

## Recent Developments

### CPKC-CSX Myrtlewood Connection (2024)
Most significant recent interchange development:
- STB approval granted October 2024
- Operations commenced November 16, 2024
- Creates new Mexico-Southeast corridor
- Opens new competitive routing options
- Estimated to benefit shippers with improved service and pricing

### Pan Am Railways Integration
CSX's acquisition of Pan Am Railways enhanced:
- New England market access
- Port of Boston connections
- Interchange opportunities in Northeast
- Integration with existing CSX network
