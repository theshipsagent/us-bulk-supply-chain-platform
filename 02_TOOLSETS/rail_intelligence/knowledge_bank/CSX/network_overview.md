# CSX Transportation — Network Overview
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23
**Carrier Code:** CSXT
---

## Corporate Information
- **Headquarters:** Jacksonville, Florida
- **Parent Company:** CSX Corporation
- **Classification:** Class I Freight Railroad
- **Operations:** Eastern United States and Canada

## Network Statistics
- **Route Miles:** Approximately 19,500 to 21,000 route miles
- **States Served:** 23 states + District of Columbia
- **Canadian Provinces:** Ontario and Quebec
- **Population Reach:** Approximately 2/3 of U.S. population

## Geographic Coverage

### States Served (23 + DC)
CSX operates in the eastern United States, covering:
- **Northeast:** Connecticut, Massachusetts, Vermont, New Hampshire, Maine, New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island
- **Mid-Atlantic:** Maryland, Delaware, Virginia, West Virginia, District of Columbia
- **Southeast:** North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Tennessee, Kentucky
- **Midwest:** Ohio, Indiana, Illinois

### Canadian Operations
- **Ontario** - including Greater Toronto Area
- **Quebec** - including Montreal area, connections via St. Lawrence Seaway

### Recent Network Expansion
Addition of **Pan Am Railways** (completed) expanded CSX's reach into:
- Connecticut
- New York (additional coverage)
- Massachusetts
- Vermont (new markets)
- New Hampshire (new markets)
- Maine (new markets)

## Network Characteristics

### Port Access
- **70+ ports** served (ocean, river, and lake terminals)
- **Atlantic Coast** - Major ports from Maine to Florida
- **Gulf Coast** - Ports in Florida, Alabama
- **Great Lakes** - Lake ports in Ohio, New York
- **St. Lawrence Seaway** - Access via Canadian operations
- **Mississippi River** - River port connections

### Intermodal Network
- **50+ intermodal terminals** across network
- All major East Coast ports served
- Inland port facilities in strategic locations
- Double-stack clearances on major corridors

### Short Line Connections
- Connections to **240+ short line and regional railroads**
- Effectively doubles market reach in route miles
- Access to additional 160 line haul carriers
- Connections to 80+ switch carriers

## Class I Railroad Interchanges

### Major Class I Connections
CSX connects with all other major Class I railroads:
- **Norfolk Southern (NS)** - Multiple interchange points throughout eastern U.S.
- **Canadian National (CN)** - Interchange in Buffalo, Detroit, other border crossings
- **Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC)** - New direct connection at Myrtlewood, Alabama (effective November 2024)
- **BNSF Railway** - Strategic partnerships for transcontinental traffic
- **Union Pacific (UP)** - Connections via gateways for western traffic
- **Kansas City Southern (now part of CPKC)** - Historical connections maintained

### Strategic Gateway Development
- **Myrtlewood, Alabama** - New CPKC-CSX Class I-to-Class I interchange
  - 93.7 miles CSX operates (Burkville to Myrtlewood)
  - Links Mexico, Texas, and U.S. Southeast
  - Average of 2 trains per day each direction (initially, for 5 years)
  - Creates new routing options for north-south traffic

## Key Corridors and Routes

### Major Traffic Corridors
- **I-95 Corridor** - Parallel to Interstate 95, serving Northeast to Southeast traffic
- **Appalachian Coal Routes** - Through Kentucky, West Virginia, Virginia
- **Seaboard Route** - Former Seaboard Air Line Railway main line through Southeast
- **Chicago-Florida Route** - Major north-south corridor
- **Gulf Coast Corridor** - Serving ports and industry along Gulf of Mexico

### Strategic Infrastructure
- **CSX Gateway Corridor Strategic Plan** - Ongoing development of key corridors
- **Carolina Connector** - Intermodal terminal in Rocky Mount, NC linking East Coast ports with Midwest
- **Mid-Atlantic Rail Operations (MAROps)** - Partnership addressing rail capacity in I-95 corridor region

## Fleet and Equipment
- **84,000+ rail cars** in freight car fleet
- Modern locomotive fleet
- Specialized equipment for various commodities:
  - Center-beam flatcars (lumber, building materials)
  - Autorack cars (automotive)
  - Tank cars (chemicals, petroleum)
  - Covered hoppers (grain, plastics)
  - Coal hoppers
  - Intermodal well cars (double-stack)

## Service Statistics
- **5+ million vehicles** handled annually (automotive)
- **1/3 of all North American light vehicles** shipped
- Serves major metropolitan areas in eastern U.S.
- Connects every major market east of Mississippi River

## Historical Context
CSX was formed from the merger of:
- **Chessie System** (C&O, B&O, Western Maryland)
- **Seaboard Coast Line Industries** (Seaboard, Atlantic Coast Line, L&N, Clinchfield)
- Later acquisitions include **Conrail** (shared with Norfolk Southern)
- Most recent: **Pan Am Railways** (2022)

## Operational Philosophy
CSX has implemented **Precision Scheduled Railroading (PSR)** principles to improve:
- Train velocity
- Asset utilization
- Operating efficiency
- Customer service consistency
- Network fluidity
