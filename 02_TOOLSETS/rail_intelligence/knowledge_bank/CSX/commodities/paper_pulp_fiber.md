## Overview
CSX is an integral part of the North American forest products industry, moving a wide range of products for the country's largest paper companies, as well as small producers and receivers.

## Network Coverage
CSX provides service to:
- More than **80 pulp and paper mills**
- Box plants (corrugated packaging facilities)
- Numerous fiber sources

## Products Transported

### Paper Products
- Newsprint
- Printing and writing papers
- Packaging papers
- Tissue products
- Specialty papers

### Pulp Products
- Wood pulp
- Chemical pulp
- Mechanical pulp
- Market pulp for export

### Fiber Products
- Wood chips
- Sawdust
- Bark
- Biomass materials
- Recycled fiber

### Packaging Materials
- Containerboard
- Corrugated products
- Boxboard
- Packaging materials

## Equipment Fleet
CSX customers have access to a fleet of more than 84,000 rail cars, including:
- Boxcars for finished paper products
- Center-beam flatcars for pulpwood
- Covered hoppers for wood chips
- Specialized equipment for forest products

## Service Coverage

### Geographic Reach
- Approximately 20,000 miles of track
- 26 states covered
- Washington D.C.
- Ontario and Quebec (Canada)
- Access to over 70 ports

### Key Regions Served
- Southeastern United States (major pulp and paper region)
- Great Lakes region
- Appalachian timber regions
- Atlantic and Gulf Coast ports

## Sustainability Benefits
Shipping by rail can significantly reduce CO2 emissions compared to shipping by highway, supporting sustainability goals of paper and forest products companies.

## Industry Support
CSX works closely with the forest products industry to provide:
- Efficient transportation solutions
- Equipment optimization
- Load planning assistance
- Just-in-time delivery to manufacturing facilities
- Export shipment coordination

## Port Access
CSX provides access to over 70 ocean, river, and lake ports, facilitating:
- Export of pulp and paper products
- Import of raw materials
- International trade connections
- Coastal distribution
