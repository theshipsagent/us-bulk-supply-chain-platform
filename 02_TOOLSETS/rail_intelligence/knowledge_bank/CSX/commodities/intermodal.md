## Overview
Intermodal combines the efficiencies of rail with the flexibility of truck. CSX is a leading supplier of rail-based intermodal transportation in North America.

## Terminal Network
CSX Intermodal Terminals operates more than 50 terminals across the eastern United States, providing comprehensive coverage of major markets.

### Inland Ports
CSX now offers service at inland port locations:
- **Northwest Georgia**
- **Northeastern South Carolina**
- **Syracuse, New York**
- **Charlotte, NC to Port of Wilmington, NC** corridor

## Service Coverage
- **50+ terminals** across the United States
- Serves all major East Coast ports
- Connects to all major Class I carriers
- Coverage extends to 23 states in the East, Midwest and South
- District of Columbia
- 2 Canadian provinces

## Operations

### Container and Trailer Service
- Containers and trailers are loaded and unloaded from trains
- Trucks provide the link between intermodal terminals and customers
- Through a network of over 40 terminals, the intermodal business serves all major markets east of the Mississippi
- Transports goods in containers for domestic and international shipments

### Equipment Types
- Domestic containers (53-foot)
- International containers (20-foot, 40-foot, 45-foot)
- Intermodal chassis for truck movement
- Double-stack rail cars for maximum efficiency

## Technology and Systems
CSX Intermodal Terminals has implemented Tideworks Terminal Operating System (TOS) across its full network to improve operational efficiency and customer service.

## Strategic Advantages
- Access to major East Coast ports
- Connections to inland markets
- Reduced highway congestion
- Lower carbon emissions compared to truck-only transport
- Scalable capacity for growing freight volumes
