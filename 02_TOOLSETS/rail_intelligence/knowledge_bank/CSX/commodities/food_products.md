# CSX — Food Products
**Source:** https://www.csx.com/index.cfm/customers/commodities/food/
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

## Overview
CSX transports food products across its 21,000-mile network, serving food processors, distributors, and manufacturers throughout the eastern United States and Canada.

## Service Capabilities
CSX provides comprehensive transportation solutions for the food industry, connecting agricultural production regions with processing facilities and consumption markets.

## Products Transported
Based on CSX's agricultural and food service portfolio:
- Processed food products
- Beverages (including soft drink production)
- Food ingredients
- Packaged goods
- Refrigerated products
- Shelf-stable products

## Equipment and Services
- Access to a fleet of more than 84,000 rail cars
- Specialized equipment for food products
- Temperature-controlled transportation options
- Food-grade rail cars meeting safety standards

## Network Advantages
- **21,000 miles** of track coverage
- Service to 2/3 of the U.S. population
- Access to major food production regions
- Connections to food processing centers
- Distribution to major consumption markets

## Related Services

### Agricultural Connections
- Direct service to grain elevators
- Feed mills
- Grain-processing facilities
- Bakeries
- Soft drink production facilities

### Distribution Support
- TRANSFLO services for transloading
- Warehousing through CSX network
- Just-in-time delivery capabilities
- Multi-modal distribution options

## Safety and Compliance
- Food safety standards compliance
- Proper equipment sanitation
- Temperature monitoring and control
- Regulatory compliance for food transportation

## Geographic Coverage
CSX serves:
- 23 states in the East, Midwest and South
- District of Columbia
- 2 Canadian provinces (Ontario and Quebec)
- Major food production and consumption centers

## Efficiency Benefits
- Rail provides lower cost per ton-mile compared to truck
- Reduced highway congestion
- Lower carbon emissions
- Scalable capacity for seasonal demand variations
