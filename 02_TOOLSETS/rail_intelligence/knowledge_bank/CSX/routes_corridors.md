## Major Traffic Corridors

### I-95 Corridor
The I-95 Corridor is one of CSX's most strategically important routes, paralleling Interstate 95 from the Northeast to the Southeast.

#### Geographic Coverage
- **Northern Terminus:** Maine/Massachusetts (via Pan Am Railways acquisition)
- **Southern Terminus:** Miami, Florida
- **Major Cities Served:**
  - Boston, MA area
  - New York, NY area
  - Philadelphia, PA
  - Baltimore, MD
  - Washington, DC
  - Richmond, VA
  - Raleigh, NC
  - Charlotte, NC (via Carolina Connector)
  - Columbia, SC
  - Savannah, GA
  - Jacksonville, FL
  - Miami, FL

#### Strategic Initiatives
**Mid-Atlantic Rail Operations (MAROps)** Study
- Initiative of the I-95 Corridor Coalition
- Partnership between:
  - Five Mid-Atlantic states (Delaware, Maryland, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Virginia)
  - Three railroads (Amtrak, CSX, Norfolk Southern)
- **Purpose:** Understand impact of rail choke points on freight transportation
- **Goal:** Address capacity constraints in the region
- **Result:** CSX incorporated projects into Gateway Corridor Strategic Plan

#### Traffic Types
- Intermodal (port-to-inland movements)
- Automotive (vehicle distribution)
- Merchandise freight (general freight)
- Coal (export traffic)
- Mixed manifest freight

### CSX Gateway Corridor Strategic Plan
CSX has developed strategic initiatives to improve capacity and fluidity on key corridors, resulting from MAROps collaboration and internal network analysis.

## Appalachian Coal Routes

### Overview
CSX's network runs through the heart of the **Appalachian Basin coal fields**, providing direct access to major coal-producing regions.

### Geographic Coverage
Primary coal routes traverse:
- **Eastern Kentucky** - Major coal-producing region
- **West Virginia** - Heart of Appalachian coal fields
- **Virginia** - Southwestern Virginia coal fields
- **Tennessee** - Eastern Tennessee coal regions
- **Pennsylvania** - Bituminous coal regions

### Key Coal Corridors

#### Big Sandy Subdivision (Former C&O Route)
- Through eastern Kentucky coal fields
- Access to loadouts in Pike, Floyd, Martin counties
- Routes to Russell, KY gateway

#### Former Clinchfield Railroad Route
- Through Virginia and Tennessee
- Historically known as "Coal Road"
- Steep grades, heavy tonnage operations
- Strategic Appalachian crossing

#### Cumberland Valley Routes
- Through Cumberland, MD area
- Historic B&O coal routes
- Access to northern West Virginia coal

#### New River Gorge Route (Former C&O)
- Through West Virginia's New River coal fields
- Heavy coal tonnage corridor
- Scenic route through rugged terrain

### Coal Service Points
- **75+ active coal load-outs** served directly by CSX
- Spread across **9 states**
- Access to both thermal and metallurgical coal
- Unit train operations throughout region

### Export Routes
Coal from Appalachian fields moves to export facilities via:
- **Curtis Bay, Baltimore, MD** - CSX-owned export pier
- **Norfolk, VA** - Via connections to Norfolk Southern
- **Charleston, SC** and other southeastern ports

## Gulf Coast Corridor

### Geographic Coverage
CSX operates along the Gulf Coast through:
- **Florida Panhandle** - Pensacola to Tallahassee
- **Alabama** - Mobile, Montgomery
- Connections to Gulf ports

### Strategic Connection: Myrtlewood, Alabama Gateway
**CPKC-CSX Connection** (Effective November 16, 2024)
- **Location:** Myrtlewood, Alabama
- **CSX Segment:** 93.7 miles (Burkville to Myrtlewood) - leased from M&B
- **CPKC Segment:** 50.4 miles (Myrtlewood to Meridian) - purchased
- **Traffic:** Initially 2 trains per day each direction (5-year agreement)
- **Strategic Importance:** Creates new direct Class I connection linking Mexico, Texas, and U.S. Southeast

### Port Access
- Mobile, AL (Alabama State Docks)
- Pensacola, FL
- Panama City, FL
- Tampa, FL

## Route Characteristics

### Double-Stack Clearances
CSX has invested in clearance projects on major intermodal corridors to accommodate double-stack containers:
- I-95 Corridor routes
- Chicago-Florida routes
- Major port access routes
- Selected transcontinental connecting routes

### Grades and Operating Characteristics
- **Appalachian Mountain crossings** - Significant grades, helper districts
- **Flat water-level routes** - Along rivers and coastal areas
- **Piedmont routes** - Rolling terrain through mid-Atlantic
- **Florida routes** - Flat, high-speed operations
