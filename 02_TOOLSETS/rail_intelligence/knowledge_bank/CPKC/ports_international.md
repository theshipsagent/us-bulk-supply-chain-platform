## Port Network Overview

CPKC serves ports on three coasts across three countries:
- Pacific Coast (Canada and Mexico)
- Atlantic Coast (Canada)
- Gulf Coast (Mexico)
- Great Lakes (Canada)

## Pacific Ports

### Vancouver, BC (Canada)
- Major container terminal (Centerm, others)
- Grain export terminals
- Potash export
- Coal export (Ridley Terminals via connections)
- Forest products
- **Destinations:** Asia (China, Japan, South Korea, Southeast Asia)
- **Recent:** Adding second daily train at Centerm (Gemini service)

### Lázaro Cárdenas, Mexico
- Automated container terminal
- Currently at 50% capacity with expansion planned
- **CPKC Investment:** $200 million opportunity
- Alternative to LA/Long Beach for Texas-bound imports
- Daily 10,000-foot stack trains planned (launches within 3 years)
- Service ramping up
- **Destinations:** Asia-Pacific, Latin America

## Atlantic Port

### Saint John, NB (Canada)
- Potash export
- General cargo
- Container capability
- Capacity expansion projects underway
- **Destinations:** Europe, Atlantic markets

## Gulf Port

### Veracruz, Mexico
- Container terminal
- Bulk commodities
- General cargo
- **Destinations:** Gulf of Mexico, Atlantic, Europe

## Great Lakes

### Thunder Bay, ON (Canada)
- Grain transshipment to lake vessels
- Lower Great Lakes and St. Lawrence Seaway access

## Single-Line Advantage

CPKC's unique single-line network enables:
- **Canada to Mexico:** No interchange, direct port-to-port service
- **Vancouver to Lázaro Cárdenas:** Pacific-to-Pacific corridor
- **Saint John to Veracruz:** Atlantic-to-Gulf corridor
- **Seamless international movements**

## Trade Lanes

**Trans-Pacific (via Vancouver):**
- Asia → Vancouver → North America

**Trans-Pacific (via Lázaro Cárdenas):**
- Asia → Lázaro Cárdenas → Mexico/Texas/U.S. interior

**Transatlantic (via Saint John):**
- Europe → Saint John → Canada/U.S.

**Intra-NAFTA:**
- Canada ↔ U.S. ↔ Mexico (single-line)

## Export Commodities

- Grain (Canadian wheat, canola)
- Potash (Saskatchewan to global markets)
- Coal (BC metallurgical coal to Asia)
- Forest products (lumber, pulp to Asia)
- Containers (manufactured goods)

## Import Commodities

- Containers (consumer goods from Asia)
- Automotive (parts from Mexico to U.S./Canada)
- Steel and metals
- Grain to Mexico (U.S. corn, soybeans)

## Port Strategy

CPKC emphasizes three strategic ports with capacity expansion:
1. **Vancouver, BC** — Established Pacific gateway
2. **Lázaro Cárdenas, Mexico** — Growing alternative to West Coast
3. **Saint John, NB** — Atlantic gateway with expansion potential
