**Source:** Web search results
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

## Overview

Intermodal is a major CPKC growth opportunity, with the single-line Mexico-U.S.-Canada network enabling seamless cross-border container movements. CPKC anticipates continued intermodal growth across its network.

## Major Terminals

**Canada:**
- Vancouver Intermodal Terminal, BC (Pacific gateway)
- Montreal Intermodal Terminal, QC
- Toronto
- Calgary
- Winnipeg (Symington Yard)

**United States:**
- Chicago (Clearing Yard, Bensenville)
- Kansas City (Knoche Yard)
- Minneapolis (Humboldt Yard)
- Houston (Englewood Yard)
- Dallas
- Detroit

**Mexico:**
- Lázaro Cárdenas Intermodal Terminal (Pacific port)
- Monterrey
- Mexico City
- Veracruz (Gulf port)
- San Luis Potosí

## Key Growth Areas

### Lázaro Cárdenas

- Automated terminal currently at half capacity
- $200 million opportunity
- Alternative to LA/Long Beach for Texas-bound imports
- Daily pair of 10,000-foot stack trains planned (launches within 3 years)
- Service ramping up

### Vancouver

- Centerm terminal adding second train per day
- Recovered from 2024 dockworker strike
- Gemini Cooperation service phasing in

### Kansas City

- Americold refrigerated warehouse under construction (opens 2026)
- Beef and pork exports to Mexico
- Broke ground recently

## Service Corridors

**North-South Backbone:**
- Vancouver/Calgary → Kansas City → Laredo/Mexico City → Lázaro Cárdenas

**Trans-Pacific Trade:**
- Asia → Lázaro Cárdenas → Texas/U.S. interior
- Asia → Vancouver → North America

**Intra-NAFTA:**
- Canada ↔ Mexico (single-line service, no interchange)
- U.S. Midwest ↔ Mexico
