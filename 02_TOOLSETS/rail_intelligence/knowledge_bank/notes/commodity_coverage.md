## Detailed Commodity Analysis

### Grain

**Top Carriers:** BNSF, UP, CN, CPKC
- **BNSF:** Northern Transcon (Midwest to PNW), Southern Transcon (to CA), Gulf routes
- **UP:** Overland Route, Sunset Route to Gulf, Mexico gateways
- **CN:** Canadian Prairies to Vancouver/Prince Rupert/Halifax/Thunder Bay, ex-IC to Gulf
- **CPKC:** Canadian Prairies to Vancouver/Thunder Bay, Kansas City gateway, single-line to Mexico
- **CSX/NS:** Eastern grain (limited production), interchange traffic to Gulf/Atlantic ports

**Export Routes:**
- Pacific Northwest: BNSF, UP, CN, CPKC (via Vancouver, Seattle, Tacoma, Prince Rupert)
- Gulf Coast: All carriers (via interchange to New Orleans, Houston, Corpus Christi)
- Atlantic: CN (Halifax), CSX/NS (Baltimore, Norfolk, Charleston, Savannah)

---

### Coal

**Top Carriers:** BNSF, UP (Powder River Basin), NS (Appalachian), CSX (Appalachian)

**BNSF & UP:**
- Powder River Basin Joint Line (shared operation)
- Low-sulfur sub-bituminous coal
- Destinations: Midwest/Great Plains/Southwest utilities

**Norfolk Southern:**
- Appalachian coal (WV, KY, VA, PA)
- Lamberts Point export terminal (Norfolk) — 48M tons capacity
- Highest-quality metallurgical coal
- Export to Asia, Europe for steel production

**CSX:**
- Appalachian coal (WV, KY, VA)
- Export via Hampton Roads (Norfolk/Newport News), Baltimore
- Domestic utilities in Southeast

**CN/CPKC:**
- British Columbia metallurgical coal
- Alberta thermal coal (declining)
- Export via Vancouver to Asia

---

### Intermodal

**All carriers are major intermodal players** with regional specializations:

**BNSF:**
- Northern & Southern Transcons
- LA/Long Beach to Chicago (fastest route)
- PNW ports (Seattle, Tacoma)

**Union Pacific:**
- West Coast to Midwest/East
- LA/Long Beach, Oakland
- Mexico gateways (6 locations)

**CSX:**
- Atlantic ports to interior
- 70+ ocean/river/lake ports
- I-95 corridor

**Norfolk Southern:**
- Largest eastern U.S. intermodal network (54 terminals)
- Crescent, Heartland, Meridian corridors
- All major Atlantic Coast ports

**Canadian National:**
- Tri-coastal network (Pacific, Atlantic, Gulf)
- Prince Rupert (fastest Asia route)
- Chicago gateway

**CPKC:**
- Single-line Canada-U.S.-Mexico advantage
- Lázaro Cárdenas (Mexico Pacific) growing
- Vancouver, Saint John

---

### Automotive

**Top Carrier:** Norfolk Southern (largest auto shipment originator in North America)
- 26 assembly plants, 35 distribution terminals, 3 JIT Rail Centers
- Southeast assembly plant cluster (AL, GA, SC, TN, KY)

**Other Major Players:**
- **CSX:** Southeast and Midwest assembly plants
- **UP:** Mexico assembly imports, Midwest plants
- **CPKC:** Single-line Mexico-U.S.-Canada (major advantage for Mexican production)
- **CN:** Ontario assembly plants (Toronto/Windsor area)
- **BNSF:** California plants, limited coverage

---

### Chemicals & Petroleum

**All carriers serve chemical markets,** but with regional concentrations:

**Gulf Coast Chemical Corridor:**
- All carriers access via interchange (BNSF, UP direct; CSX, NS via trackage; CN via ex-IC)
- Major petrochemical production (TX, LA)

**Regional Production:**
- **BNSF:** West Coast, Midwest chemicals; Bakken crude oil
- **UP:** Permian crude, West Coast refineries
- **CSX/NS:** Mid-Atlantic chemical plants, NJ chemical corridor
- **CN:** Alberta petrochemicals, Eastern Canada
- **CPKC:** Canadian and U.S. chemical movements

---

### Forest Products

**Top Carriers:** BNSF, CN, CPKC (Pacific Northwest & Canadian sources)

**BNSF:**
- Pacific Northwest lumber (WA, OR, ID, MT)
- Paper mills
- Export to Asia

**CN:**
- British Columbia, Alberta, Quebec
- Pulp and paper leadership
- Export via Vancouver, Halifax

**CPKC:**
- British Columbia lumber
- Canadian paper mills
- Single-line to U.S./Mexico markets

**CSX/NS:**
- Southeastern paper mills (GA, SC, NC, AL)
- Limited lumber (imports via ports)

**UP:**
- Limited PNW coverage
- Some Intermountain production

### Construction Materials (Cement, Aggregates, Steel)

See separate construction_materials.md for detailed analysis.

**Brief Summary:**
- **Cement:** All carriers serve cement facilities (BNSF 15+, NS 15, CSX, UP similar)
- **Aggregates:** NS leads with 67 quarries + 30 distribution yards; all carriers serve regional quarries
- **Steel:** NS leadership (50+ steel processors); CSX strong in Midwest (via interchange); UP serves western mills

### Fertilizer & Potash

**Top Carriers:** CN, CPKC (Canadian potash)

**CN & CPKC:**
- Saskatchewan potash (world's largest reserves)
- Export via Vancouver, Prince Rupert (CN), Thunder Bay
- 41.5% of Saskatchewan potash moves by rail to West Coast

**BNSF & UP:**
- Distribute imported potash (PNW, Gulf ports)
- Nitrogen fertilizers (Gulf Coast production)
- Phosphate (Idaho, Florida)

**CSX/NS:**
- Distribute imported potash
- Florida phosphate
- Nitrogen (Gulf Coast via interchange)

## Regional Specializations

### Western Carriers (BNSF, UP)
- Grain (Great Plains to export)
- Coal (Powder River Basin)
- Intermodal (Asia trade via West Coast)
- Forest products (Pacific Northwest)

### Eastern Carriers (CSX, NS)
- Appalachian coal (export quality)
- Automotive (Southeast assembly cluster)
- Atlantic port access
- Paper/pulp (Southeast mills)

### Canadian Carriers (CN, CPKC)
- Potash (world's largest reserves)
- Grain (Canadian Prairies)
- Forest products (Canadian timber)
- Tri-coastal/single-line advantage

## Competitive Overlaps

**Chicago Gateway:**
- All carriers compete for transcontinental traffic
- Grain, intermodal, manifest freight

**Gulf Coast:**
- All carriers access (direct or via interchange)
- Grain export, chemicals, intermodal

**Mexico Trade:**
- UP (6 gateways), CPKC (single-line advantage)
- Growing automotive, intermodal, grain imports

**Pacific Ports:**
- BNSF, UP dominate West Coast
- CN via Vancouver/Prince Rupert
- CPKC via Vancouver, Lázaro Cárdenas (Mexico)
