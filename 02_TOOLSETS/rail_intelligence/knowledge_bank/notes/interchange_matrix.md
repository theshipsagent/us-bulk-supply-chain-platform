# Interchange Matrix — Class I Railroad Connections

**Created:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

This document identifies major interchange points where Class I railroads connect to exchange freight traffic. Interchanges enable transcontinental movements, regional distribution, and access to markets beyond each carrier's direct network.

## Major Interchange Hubs

### Chicago, IL (All Class I Carriers)

**Participating Carriers:** BNSF, Union Pacific, CSX, Norfolk Southern, Canadian National, Canadian Pacific Kansas City

**Characteristics:**
- Largest rail hub in North America
- Belt Railway of Chicago (BRC) facilitates interchange
- Multiple interchange yards across Chicago terminal
- All commodity categories exchanged
- Critical transcontinental gateway

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Western grain to eastern markets (BNSF/UP to CSX/NS)
- PRB coal to eastern utilities (BNSF/UP to CSX/NS)
- Intermodal (transcontinental traffic via all carriers)
- Canadian traffic to/from U.S. (CN/CPKC to all)

---

### Kansas City, MO/KS

**Participating Carriers:** BNSF, Union Pacific, Norfolk Southern, Canadian Pacific Kansas City

**Characteristics:**
- Central U.S. hub
- Major north-south and east-west junction
- CPKC major classification yard (Knoche Yard)
- Agricultural products exchange

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Grain movements (all directions)
- Automotive (Southeast assembly plants via NS to western markets)
- Intermodal (transcontinental)
- Mexico traffic (via CPKC)

---

### Memphis, TN

**Participating Carriers:** BNSF, Union Pacific, CSX, Norfolk Southern, Canadian National, Canadian Pacific Kansas City

**Characteristics:**
- Mississippi River crossing
- North-south gateway
- Six Class I carriers interchange

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Coal to southeastern utilities
- Grain export to Gulf Coast
- Intermodal (Southeast to West)
- Automotive

---

### New Orleans, LA / Gulf Coast

**Participating Carriers:** BNSF, Union Pacific, CSX, Norfolk Southern, Canadian National, Canadian Pacific Kansas City

**Characteristics:**
- Gulf Coast port access

- Chemical corridor connections

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Grain export (Midwest to Gulf terminals)
- Chemicals (Gulf Coast production to markets)
- Intermodal (Gulf ports to interior)
- Steel and forest products

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Intermodal (primary)

- General freight

### Minneapolis/St. Paul, MN

### Pacific Northwest (Multiple Points)

**Characteristics:**

- Seattle/Tacoma, WA
- Spokane, WA
- Shared trackage agreements
- Puget Sound port access

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Grain (both carriers serve PNW export terminals)
- Forest products
- Intermodal
- Potash (from Canada via CN/CPKC interchange)

---

### Powder River Basin, WY/MT

**Participating Carriers:** BNSF, Union Pacific (Joint Line)

**Characteristics:**
- Joint ownership and operation
- Coal unit trains exclusively
- Reciprocal switching arrangements
- Mine assignments to either carrier

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Coal (Powder River Basin to utilities nationwide)

---

## Conrail Shared Assets Areas

**Participating Carriers:** CSX, Norfolk Southern (joint operation)

**Three Shared Assets Areas:**
1. **North Jersey** (access to NY/NJ ports)
2. **South Jersey/Philadelphia** (chemical corridor, Philadelphia ports)
3. **Detroit** (automotive corridor, international gateway)

**Key Traffic Flows:**
- Port containers (NY/NJ, Philadelphia)
- Chemicals (New Jersey corridor)
- Automotive (Detroit area)

## Operational Considerations

### Dwell Time
- Interchange delays can add 12-48 hours to transit time
- Modern agreements aim to reduce dwell (reciprocal switching, joint schedules)
- CPKC's single-line Canada-U.S.-Mexico eliminates interchange delays

### Equipment Exchange
- Foreign car usage (cars from other railroads on your lines)
- Per diem charges for car usage
- Interline settlement agreements

### Service Reliability
- Interchange is often the weakest link in transcontinental service
- Schedule coordination critical for intermodal
- Unit trains often have priority interchange handling
