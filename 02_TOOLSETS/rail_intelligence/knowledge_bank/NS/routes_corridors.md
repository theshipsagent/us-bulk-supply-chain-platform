## Overview

Norfolk Southern operates approximately 19,200 route miles across 22 states and the District of Columbia. The company has invested heavily in three strategic corridors: Crescent Corridor, Heartland Corridor, and Meridian Speedway, all featuring double-stack intermodal clearance and enhanced capacity.

## Strategic Corridors

### Crescent Corridor

**Investment:** $2.5 billion rail infrastructure project
**Distance:** 2,500 miles
**Coverage:** 11 states
**Route:** New Orleans → Memphis → Chattanooga → Atlanta → Charlotte → Greensboro → Raleigh → Richmond → Washington DC → Philadelphia → New York/New Jersey

**Key Features:**
- One of the fastest, most direct routes from Southeast to Northeast
- 30 new lanes now open
- Convenient connections to Mexico and Los Angeles
- High-capacity intermodal routes
- Truck competitive, fuel efficient, and dependable
- Double-stack clearance throughout

**Markets Served:**
- Southeast to Northeast intermodal
- Import/export via Atlantic ports
- Mexico gateway connections (via interchange)
- California gateway connections (via interchange)

**Commodities:**
- Intermodal containers (international and domestic)
- Automotive
- Consumer goods
- Food and beverage

---

### Heartland Corridor

**Investment:** Over $300 million (public-private partnership)
**Route:** Norfolk, VA → Portsmouth, OH → Columbus, OH → Chicago, IL (via connections)
**Construction:** Over 35 distinct projects

**Key Features:**
- Core project: 28 tunnels through West Virginia improved to ~22 feet vertical clearance
- Accommodates double-stack trains between Norfolk and Midwest
- Direct connection from Port of Virginia to Midwest markets
- Coal corridor (historically) now major intermodal route

**Tunnel Improvements:**
- Vertical clearance increased to approximately 22 feet
- Allows double-stack container trains
- West Virginia mountain crossings

**Markets Served:**
- Port of Virginia imports → Midwest
- Midwest exports → Port of Virginia
- Coal (Appalachian mines → Midwest utilities)
- Chemicals and general freight

**Commodities:**
- Intermodal containers
- Coal (declining but still significant)
- Automotive parts
- Consumer goods

---

### Meridian Speedway

**Investment:** $300 million ($260 million NS, $40 million KCS/CPKC)
**Distance:** 320 miles
**Route:** Meridian, MS → Shreveport, LA
**Partnership:** Joint venture with Kansas City Southern (now CPKC)

**Key Features:**
- Most direct route between California and Southeast
- Faster service between Dallas and Southeast
- Shortest, fastest rail route between Southeast and West Coast
- Connection to CPKC for Mexico and West Coast access

**Routing:**
- **Southeast to West Coast:** NS network → Meridian Speedway → CPKC → Southern California
- **Southeast to Mexico:** NS network → Meridian Speedway → CPKC → Laredo/Mexico City

**Markets Served:**
- Southeast to/from California (intermodal)
- Southeast to/from Dallas
- Southeast to/from Mexico
- Transcontinental freight movements

**Commodities:**
- Intermodal (primary)
- Automotive
- Consumer goods
- Manifest freight

## Regional Corridors

### Northeast Corridor (via Conrail Shared Assets)

**Route:** Washington DC → Baltimore → Philadelphia → New York/New Jersey
**Characteristics:**
- Shared trackage with CSX (Conrail Shared Assets Areas)
- Access to Northeast megalopolis
- Dense population and industrial centers
- Major port access (NY/NJ, Philadelphia, Baltimore)

**Key Features:**
- High traffic density
- Passenger rail interference (Amtrak, commuter rail)
- Tight clearances in some areas
- Critical market access (Northeast population)

---

### Appalachian Coal Corridors

**Primary Coal Route:**
**West Virginia/Kentucky Coal Fields → Roanoke, VA → Norfolk, VA (Lamberts Point)**

**Characteristics:**
- Mountain railroading (steep grades, curves)
- Unit coal train operations
- Historically the busiest coal corridor in U.S.
- Export coal to Lamberts Point terminal

**Secondary Coal Routes:**
- Appalachia → North Carolina utilities
- Appalachia → South Carolina utilities
- Appalachia → Georgia utilities

---

### Piedmont Corridor

**Route:** Washington DC → Richmond → Raleigh → Greensboro → Charlotte → Atlanta

**Key Features:**
- Major population centers (Piedmont urban crescent)
- Intermodal traffic (Crescent Corridor component)
- Automotive (Southeast assembly plants)
- Chemicals and industrial products

**Markets:**
- Automotive assembly plants (South Carolina, Georgia, Alabama)
- Distribution centers
- Chemical facilities
- Food processing

---

### Great Lakes Corridor

**Route:** Chicago → Fort Wayne → Lima → Columbus → Cleveland/Toledo

**Key Features:**
- Midwest gateway access
- Steel and automotive markets
- Great Lakes port connections
- Interchange with western carriers at Chicago

**Markets:**
- Steel mills and processors (declining)
- Automotive assembly and parts
- Great Lakes shipping connections
- Midwest agriculture (grain)

---

### Gulf Coast Corridor

**Route:** Atlanta/Birmingham → Meridian → New Orleans

**Key Features:**
- Gulf Coast port access (Mobile, New Orleans)
- Meridian Speedway connection
- North-south traffic
- Export grain corridor

**Markets:**
- Port of Mobile (coal, steel, containers)
- Port of New Orleans (containers, steel, grain)
- Chemical corridor access
- Forest products (paper mills)

---

### Pan Am Southern Route (Historical)

**Note:** Norfolk Southern acquired Pan Am Railways (now Pan Am Southern), providing access to:
- New England markets
- Upstate New York
- Additional Northeast coverage

## Interstate Highway Parallels

- **I-81 Corridor:** Virginia to Pennsylvania (Appalachian parallel)
- **I-85 Corridor:** Piedmont (Atlanta to Richmond)
- **I-95 Corridor:** Northeast (via Conrail Shared Assets)
- **I-75 Corridor:** Georgia/Tennessee
- **I-20 Corridor:** Atlanta to Dallas (Meridian Speedway connection)

## Key Junction Points

### Chicago, IL
- Interchange with all Class I carriers
- Belt Railway of Chicago (BRC)
- Gateway to western U.S.

### Kansas City, MO/KS
- Interchange with BNSF, UP, CPKC
- Midwest gateway

### Memphis, TN
- Major interchange hub (BNSF, UP, CSX, CN, CPKC)
- Mississippi River crossing
- Gateway to West and Southwest

### Meridian, MS
- Meridian Speedway junction with CPKC
- Critical transcontinental gateway

### Roanoke, VA
- Coal corridor junction
- Appalachian gateway

### Columbus, OH
- Heartland Corridor hub
- Midwest distribution

## Double-Stack Clearance

**Routes with Double-Stack Capability:**
- Crescent Corridor (entire route)
- Heartland Corridor (Norfolk to Columbus/Chicago)
- Meridian Speedway (entire route)
- Selected other main lines

**Benefits:**
- International container movements
- Competitive with West Coast ports
- Fuel efficiency
- Capacity optimization

---

## Positive Train Control (PTC)

PTC deployed across all main lines and required routes, enhancing:
- Safety
- Operational efficiency
- Regulatory compliance
