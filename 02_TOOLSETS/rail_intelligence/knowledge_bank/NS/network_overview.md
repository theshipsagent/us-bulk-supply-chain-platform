## Network Characteristics

### Route Structure

- **East-West Corridors:** Connecting Atlantic Coast to Midwest gateways
- **North-South Corridors:** Connecting Great Lakes to Gulf Coast
- **Dense Network:** Extensive coverage in Southeast and Mid-Atlantic
- **Strategic Corridors:** Crescent, Heartland, Meridian Speedway

### Infrastructure

- **Double-stack clearance:** Major corridors (Crescent, Heartland, Meridian)
- **Positive Train Control (PTC):** Deployed across main lines
- **Centralized Traffic Control (CTC):** Main corridors
- **Intermodal terminals:** 54 facilities
- **Classification yards:** Major hubs throughout network

## Major Market Centers

### Manufacturing & Industrial
- **Automotive:** Southeast assembly plant cluster
- **Steel:** Ohio, Indiana (via interchange), Alabama
- **Chemicals:** Mid-Atlantic, Southeast corridor
- **Paper & Forest Products:** Southeast mills

### Population Centers
- **Northeast Megalopolis:** New York, Philadelphia, Baltimore, Washington DC (via Conrail Shared Assets)
- **Piedmont Region:** Charlotte, Greensboro, Raleigh, Atlanta
- **Great Lakes:** Cleveland, Toledo, Detroit area
- **Gulf Access:** New Orleans, Mobile

### Port Access

- **24 seaports** (Atlantic Coast, Gulf Coast)
- **10 river ports** (Mississippi River system)
- **9 lake ports** (Great Lakes)

## Freight Categories

### Major Commodity Groups

1. **Intermodal** — Largest intermodal network in eastern U.S.
2. **Coal** — Appalachian coal, export via Lamberts Point
3. **Automotive** — Largest auto shipment originator in North America
4. **Agriculture & Forest** — Grain, lumber, paper, food products
5. **Chemicals & Petroleum** — Industrial chemicals, plastics, refined products
6. **Metals & Construction** — Steel, aggregates, cement, machinery

## Strategic Investments

### Recent Infrastructure Projects

- **Crescent Corridor:** $2.5 billion, 2,500 miles, 11 states
- **Heartland Corridor:** Double-stack clearance Norfolk to Columbus
- **Meridian Speedway:** $300 million joint venture with CPKC
- **Terminal expansions:** Louisville, other key facilities

### 2025 Industrial Development

- **60+ industrial development projects**
- **$7.7 billion in industry investment**
- Strong activity in metals, paper, aggregates, automotive sectors
- Focus on turnkey sites and high service standards

## Connections & Interchanges

### Class I Railroad Interchanges

- **Chicago:** All Class I carriers
- **Kansas City:** BNSF, UP, CPKC
- **Memphis:** BNSF, UP, CSX, CN, CPKC
- **New Orleans:** BNSF, UP, CSX, CN
- **Shreveport (Meridian Speedway):** CPKC
- **Other key gateways:** St. Louis, Louisville

### Conrail Shared Assets

- **Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Delaware:** Shared trackage with CSX
- **Access to New York/New Jersey ports**
- **Philadelphia, Wilmington, North Jersey terminals**

### Shortline Connections

- Extensive shortline and regional railroad network
- First-mile/last-mile connectivity
- Industrial switching and local service

## Operational Characteristics

### Service Priorities

- **Precision Scheduled Railroading (PSR)** principles
- **Safety leadership:** First responder training, hazmat programs
- **On-time performance:** Automotive and intermodal focus
- **Supply chain management:** Modal solutions for customers

### Technology

- Positive Train Control (PTC)
- Real-time tracking and visibility
- Digital tools for customers
- Automated equipment inspection

## Competitive Position

### Eastern U.S. Leadership

- Most extensive intermodal network in eastern U.S.
- Largest auto shipment originator in North America
- Leading Appalachian coal carrier
- Direct access to all major Atlantic Coast ports
- Dense Southeast coverage (automotive, chemicals, forest products)

### Strategic Advantages

- **Three major corridors:** Crescent, Heartland, Meridian
- **Truck-competitive service:** Fast, reliable transit times
- **Port diversity:** Atlantic, Gulf, Great Lakes access
- **Industrial development:** Turnkey sites, certified locations
- **Interline partnerships:** Connections to western carriers
