## Overview

Norfolk Southern's Agriculture, Fertilizer, & Consumer Products Group serves shippers and receivers of grain, soybeans, wheat, corn, animal and poultry feed, food oils, flour, beverages, canned goods, sweeteners, fertilizers, and consumer products. Across their network, NS connects with over 400 customer facilities that ship and receive grain and soybean products.

## Commodities

### Grain & Oilseeds
- **Corn:** Feed corn, seed corn
- **Soybeans:** Whole soybeans, soybean meal, soybean oil
- **Wheat:** Soft red winter wheat, hard red wheat
- **Oilseeds:** Sunflower, canola

### Processed Agricultural Products
- **Flour:** Wheat flour, specialty flours
- **Food oils:** Soybean oil, corn oil, vegetable oils
- **Sweeteners:** Corn syrup, HFCS
- **Beverages:** Beer, soft drinks, juices
- **Canned goods:** Vegetables, fruits, processed foods

### Animal Feed
- **Animal feed:** Livestock feed formulations
- **Poultry feed:** Commercial poultry operations

### Fertilizer
- See separate fertilizer commodity file (if applicable)

## Equipment

- **Covered Hoppers:** Grain, soybeans, flour, feed
- **Tank Cars:** Edible oils, corn syrup, liquid food ingredients
- **Boxcars:** Canned goods, beverages, packaged foods
- **Refrigerated Cars:** Temperature-sensitive products (limited)

## Service Types

- Unit trains for high-volume grain shippers
- Manifest freight for smaller shipments
- Shuttle train service to export terminals
- Transload facilities for truck-to-rail conversion

## Poultry Industry Support

Norfolk Southern serves a major role in the Southeast poultry industry:
- Feed corn delivery to poultry feed mills
- Soybean meal to feed mills
- Export of poultry products (containerized)
- Major chicken producers in NS territory (Georgia, North Carolina, Alabama, Arkansas)

- **Port of Savannah:** Grain export, containerized food
- **Port of Mobile:** Grain export
- **Port of New Orleans:** Grain export (via trackage/interchange)

## Competitive Position

Norfolk Southern's agricultural franchise benefits from:
- Direct access to Southeast poultry industry
- Connection to Ohio/Indiana grain belt
- Multiple Atlantic and Gulf port export options
- 400+ grain facility customer base
- Transload and transfer facility network

## Market Trends

- Poultry industry growth in Southeast (feed demand)
- Export grain demand variable (international market conditions)
- Ethanol production impacting corn markets (limited ethanol plants on NS)
- Food processing consolidation
- Local grain elevator closures (shift to larger facilities)
