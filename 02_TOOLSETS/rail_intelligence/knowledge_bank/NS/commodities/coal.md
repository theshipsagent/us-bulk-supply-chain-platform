## Overview

Norfolk Southern is a leader in sourcing, blending, and moving the highest quality steam and metallurgical coal in the world. The company works closely with producers, receivers, agents, and brokers to support the sourcing, blending, and transportation of high-quality utility, metallurgical, and industrial coals that power communities, advance economic development, and feed manufacturing in America and around the world.

## Coal Types

### Metallurgical (Coking) Coal
- Used in steel production
- Primary export commodity
- Appalachian sourcing
- High-quality specifications
- International demand (Europe, Asia, South America)

### Utility (Steam) Coal
- Power generation
- Domestic utilities
- Appalachian basins
- Lower sulfur content preferred

### Industrial Coal
- Manufacturing applications
- Specialty industrial users

## Source Regions

### Appalachian Coal Fields

**Primary Production Areas:**
- West Virginia (largest source)
- Kentucky (Eastern Kentucky)
- Virginia (Southwest Virginia)
- Pennsylvania
- Tennessee (limited)
- Maryland (limited)
- Ohio (limited)

**Key Features:**
- High-quality metallurgical coal
- Higher BTU content than western coals
- Direct NS rail access to mines
- 3-4 new mines recently developed in Appalachia
- New coal train loading points in development

## Export Terminal

### Lamberts Point Coal Terminal (Norfolk, VA)

**Facility Characteristics:**
- Largest, fastest, and most efficient transloading facility for coal in the Northern Hemisphere
- Opened around 1865 on the Elizabeth River
- Annual throughput capacity: 48 million tons
- Complete train flood loading capability
- Up to four coal blending and mixing services
- Handles both metallurgical and utility coal

**Operations:**
- Most business involves met coal export
- Loaded onto ocean-going vessels
- Shipped to Europe, Asia, South America, and beyond
- Used in steel production globally
- Recently ramped up coal dumping capability

## Service Characteristics

- Unit train operations (100+ car trains)
- Mine-to-port direct service
- Coal blending capabilities at Lamberts Point
- Transload facilities for smaller volumes
- Quality control and testing services

## Recent Developments (2024-2026)

- NS expects to handle more coking coal (met coal growth)
- 3-4 new mines developed in Appalachia
- New coal train loading points under development
- Lamberts Point ramps up coal dumping capability
- Met coal export demand stronger than utility coal

## Equipment

- Aluminum coal cars (lighter weight)
- Rotary coupler-equipped cars for flood loading
- Unit train dedicated fleet
- High-capacity gondolas

## Routes

**Coal Mine to Lamberts Point:**
- West Virginia coal fields → Roanoke, VA → Norfolk, VA
- Kentucky coal fields → Williamson, WV → Roanoke → Norfolk
- Virginia coal fields → Roanoke → Norfolk

**Coal Mine to Domestic Utilities:**
- Appalachia → North Carolina utilities
- Appalachia → Georgia utilities
- Appalachia → Other southeastern utilities

## Competitive Position

Norfolk Southern's Appalachian coal franchise provides:
- Direct access to high-quality met coal
- Shortest route to deep-water export terminal (Lamberts Point)
- Blending and transloading capabilities
- Established relationships with coal producers
- International shipping expertise

## Market Trends

- Domestic utility coal declining (natural gas competition, plant retirements)
- Metallurgical coal export demand more stable
- International steel production drives met coal demand
- Environmental regulations affecting utility coal
- New Appalachian mine development (met coal focus)
