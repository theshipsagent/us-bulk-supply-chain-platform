## Overview

Norfolk Southern operates the most extensive intermodal network in the eastern U.S., serving major population and manufacturing centers with connections to every major Atlantic Coast port, as well as key Gulf Coast and Great Lakes ports. The company operates 54 terminals with an extensive and reliable double-stack rail network, serving more major eastern US markets than any other rail carrier.

## Network Scale

- **54 intermodal terminals** across the eastern United States
- **24 seaports** served
- **10 river ports** served
- **9 lake ports** served
- Most extensive intermodal network in the eastern U.S.
- All major East Coast ports connected

## Major Corridors

### Crescent Corridor
- $2.5 billion rail infrastructure project
- 2,500 miles spanning 11 states
- One of the fastest, most direct routes from Southeast to Northeast
- 30 new lanes now open
- Connections to Mexico and Los Angeles
- High-capacity intermodal routes
- Truck competitive, fuel efficient, and dependable

### Heartland Corridor
- Over 35 distinct construction projects
- Route between Norfolk and Columbus
- Core project: 28 tunnels through West Virginia improved to ~22 feet vertical clearance
- Accommodates double-stack trains between Norfolk and Midwest
- Double-stack intermodal to/from Norfolk

### Meridian Speedway (Joint with CPKC)
- $300 million project ($260 million NS investment)
- Joint venture between NS and Kansas City Southern (now CPKC)
- 320-mile corridor: Meridian, MS to Shreveport, LA
- Most direct route between California and Southeast
- Faster service between Dallas and Southeast
- Shortest, fastest rail route between Southeast and West Coast
