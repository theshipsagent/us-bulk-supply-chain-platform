# Union Pacific Railroad — Ports & International Gateways

**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

Union Pacific provides direct rail service to **all major West Coast ports** and **all Gulf Coast ports**, plus exclusive access to **all six major Mexico gateways**. This comprehensive port and international gateway network positions UP as a critical link in North American and global supply chains.

---

## West Coast Ports

Union Pacific serves every significant West Coast port, providing connections between Asian trade lanes and North American markets.

## Los Angeles/Long Beach Port Complex

### Overview
The **largest container port complex** in the United States and Union Pacific's highest-volume intermodal gateway.

### Ports Served
- Port of Los Angeles
- Port of Long Beach

### Combined Statistics
- Leading U.S. container port complex
- Major gateway for trans-Pacific trade
- Approximately 40% of U.S. containerized imports

### UP Service
- **Multiple intermodal terminals:** Near-dock and off-dock facilities
- **On-dock rail:** Direct container loading from ships to trains
- **Double-stack trains:** High-capacity intermodal service
- **Automotive facilities:** Import vehicle processing centers

### Major Commodities
- **Import containers:** Consumer goods, electronics, furniture, apparel from Asia
- **Export containers:** Agricultural products, recycled materials, manufactured goods
- **Automobiles:** Asian vehicle imports
- **Project cargo:** Heavy equipment and machinery

### Destinations
- Chicago and eastern gateways (via Overland Route)
- Texas and Gulf Coast (via Sunset Route)
- Midwest distribution centers
- Mexico (via southern California gateways)

## Mexico Gateways

Union Pacific is the **only railroad serving all six major gateways to Mexico**, providing unparalleled cross-border rail access.

---

## Laredo, Texas Gateway

### Overview
**Largest U.S.-Mexico rail crossing** by volume.

### Location
Laredo, Texas / Nuevo Laredo, Tamaulipas, Mexico

### Mexican Railroad Connections
- **Kansas City Southern de México (KCSM):** Now part of CPKC network
- **Ferromex:** Mexican railroad serving central and western Mexico

### Major Commodities
- **Automotive:** Finished vehicles and parts from Mexican assembly plants
- **Intermodal containers:** Cross-border manufactured goods
- **Industrial products:** Consumer goods, electronics, appliances
- **Agricultural products:** Two-way agricultural trade
- **Chemicals:** Industrial products and plastics

### Traffic Volume
Laredo handles the highest volume of rail freight of any U.S.-Mexico border crossing, driven by:
- USMCA trade agreement (formerly NAFTA)
- Maquiladora manufacturing (automotive, electronics)
- Cross-border supply chains

## Mexico Gateway Strategic Advantages

### Exclusive Six-Gateway Coverage
Union Pacific is the **only railroad serving all six major gateways to Mexico**, providing:
- **Routing flexibility:** Multiple entry/exit points for cross-border traffic
- **Network redundancy:** Alternative gateways during congestion or service disruptions
- **Geographic coverage:** Access to eastern, central, and western Mexico destinations
- **Competitive advantage:** Comprehensive Mexico service portfolio

### USMCA Trade Agreement
The United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA, formerly NAFTA) drives significant cross-border rail traffic:
- **Automotive supply chains:** Integrated North American vehicle production
- **Manufacturing:** Maquiladora and assembly plant operations
- **Agricultural trade:** Two-way commodity flows
- **Consumer goods:** Cross-border retail supply chains

### Traffic Growth
Mexico gateways represent a growth opportunity for Union Pacific:
- Expanding Mexican manufacturing
- Nearshoring trends (companies moving production closer to U.S. markets)
- Automotive industry integration
- Consumer goods and e-commerce growth

## Canadian Gateways

While Union Pacific does not directly serve Canadian border crossings, the railroad provides Canadian market access through:

### Interchange with Canadian Railroads
- **Chicago gateway:** Interchange with Canadian National (CN)
- **Pacific Northwest:** Connections via BNSF/CN to western Canada

### Canadian Commodities
- **Grain and potash:** Canadian agricultural products exported via U.S. Gulf ports
- **Forest products:** Lumber and pulp from British Columbia and western provinces
- **Automotive:** Canadian vehicle production to U.S. markets
- **Intermodal:** Cross-border container movements
