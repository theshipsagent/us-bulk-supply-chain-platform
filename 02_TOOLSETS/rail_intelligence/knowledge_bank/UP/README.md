## Overview

This knowledge bank contains comprehensive information about Union Pacific Railroad's network, commodities, facilities, and operations. Union Pacific is the second-largest Class I railroad in the United States, operating 32,693 route miles across 23 states in the western two-thirds of the country.

## Key Statistics

### Network
- **Route Miles:** 32,693
- **States Served:** 23 (western two-thirds of U.S.)
- **Locomotives:** 8,300+
- **Employees:** Thousands across network
- **2026 Infrastructure Investment:** $3.3 billion

### Major Facilities
- **Bailey Yard (North Platte, NE):** World's largest classification yard, 2,850 acres, 10,000 cars/day
- **Davis Yard (Roseville, CA):** Major West Coast yard, handles 98% of Northern California traffic
- **Intermodal Terminals:** 40+ locations
- **Automotive Distribution Centers:** 40+ locations
- **Mexico Gateways:** All six major crossings (only railroad with complete coverage)

### Unique Capabilities
- **Largest automotive carrier** west of the Mississippi River (5+ million vehicles annually)
- **Only railroad** serving all six major Mexico gateways
- Serves **all major West Coast and Gulf Coast ports**
- Operates the **world's largest classification yard** (Bailey Yard)
- **Largest multi-level railcar fleet** in the industry (14,500+ autoracks)

## Major Corridors

### Transcontinental Routes
1. **Sunset Route** — New Orleans to Los Angeles (1,995 miles, 5 states, 20% of UP traffic)
2. **Overland Route** — Omaha/Council Bluffs to San Francisco Bay Area (historic first transcontinental)
3. **Central Corridor** — Heartland agricultural and industrial regions

### Specialized Routes
4. **Powder River Basin Coal Route** — Shawnee Junction, WY to O'Fallons, NE (103-mile Joint Line with BNSF)
5. **Mexico Gateway Corridors** — Six major border crossings serving cross-border trade

## Market Position

### Competitive Position
- **Second-largest U.S. railroad** (after BNSF by route miles)
- **Western two-thirds of U.S.** service territory
- **Coast-to-coast service** via partnerships with CSX and Norfolk Southern

### Strategic Advantages
- **Mexico gateway monopoly:** Only railroad serving all six major crossings
- **Port access:** All West Coast and Gulf Coast ports
- **Transcontinental corridors:** Sunset Route (southern) and Overland Route (northern)
- **Major facilities:** Bailey Yard, extensive intermodal network, automotive distribution
- **Diversified commodity mix:** Balanced portfolio across multiple industries
