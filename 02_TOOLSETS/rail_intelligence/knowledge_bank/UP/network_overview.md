## System Characteristics

### Transcontinental Reach
Union Pacific operates entirely west of Chicago and New Orleans but provides **coast-to-coast service** through partnerships with eastern railroads (CSX and Norfolk Southern).

### Port Access
- **All major West Coast ports:** Complete Pacific Rim connectivity
- **All Gulf Coast ports:** Gulf of Mexico and trans-Panama trade lanes
- **Mexico border:** Only railroad serving all six major gateways to Mexico

### Strategic Corridors
- **Overland Route:** Northern transcontinental corridor
- **Sunset Route:** Southern transcontinental corridor
- **Central Corridor:** Through the heartland
- **Powder River Basin coal route:** Wyoming to southern/midwestern utilities

## Major Facilities

### Classification Yards

#### Bailey Yard (North Platte, Nebraska)
- **World's largest railroad classification yard**
- 2,850 acres (8 miles long, 2 miles wide)
- 315 miles of track, 200 separate tracks
- 10,000 cars handled daily
- 3,000 cars sorted daily in hump yards
- 2,600+ employees

#### Davis Yard (Roseville, California)
- Major West Coast classification facility
- Opened 1906, major renovations 1952 and 1999
- 98% of Northern California traffic moves through Davis Yard
- One of the biggest operating yards on the West Coast

### Intermodal Terminals
- **40+ intermodal terminals** across the network
- Major hubs: Los Angeles, Chicago (Global IV), Dallas, Salt Lake City
- State-of-the-art technology for rapid truck processing

### Automotive Distribution Centers
- **40+ vehicle distribution centers**
- Directly serves 5 vehicle assembly plants
- 14,500+ multi-level railcar fleet

## Market Position

### Competitive Position
- **Second-largest U.S. railroad** (after BNSF by route miles)
- **Largest automotive carrier** west of the Mississippi River
- **Dominant carrier** for Wyoming Powder River Basin coal

### Service Territory
Serves the **western two-thirds of the United States**, providing critical links between:
- Pacific Rim trade and North American markets

## Recent Developments (2025-2026)

### Merger Proposal (Rejected)
In **January 2026**, federal regulators **rejected** Union Pacific and Norfolk Southern's proposed merger, which would have created the largest railroad merger in history. The decision maintained the existing competitive landscape.

### Service Improvements
Following service challenges in 2022-2023, Union Pacific has focused on:
- Operational efficiency improvements
- Crew availability and training
- Terminal velocity enhancements
- Customer service reliability

### Market Outlook
- **Strong growth** anticipated from construction and intermodal markets
- **Nonresidential construction** forecasted to drive demand in 2025-2026
- **Infrastructure investment** supporting long-term capacity needs
