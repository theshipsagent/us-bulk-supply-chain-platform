# Union Pacific Railroad — Interchanges

**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

Union Pacific interchanges freight traffic with all major North American Class I railroads, enabling seamless coast-to-coast and cross-border freight movements. Strategic interchange locations provide shippers with routing options and competitive service.

---

## Primary Eastern Railroad Interchanges

Union Pacific's western network connects with eastern railroads at multiple **mid-continent gateways**, creating transcontinental routing options.

---

## Chicago Interchanges

### Overview
**Chicago** is Union Pacific's primary eastern interchange point, connecting with both major eastern railroads.

### Railroads Interchanged
- **Norfolk Southern (NS)**
- **CSX Transportation (CSX)**

### Traffic Types
- **Intermodal:** High-volume container and trailer interchange
- **Manifest freight:** General merchandise and carload traffic
- **Automotive:** Finished vehicles and parts
- **Bulk commodities:** Coal, grain, chemicals (select movements)

### Volume (UP-NS 2023 Data)
Approximately **daily interchange** between UP and NS in Chicago and other gateways:
- 1,637 cars of manifest traffic
- 1,648 containers of intermodal traffic
- 122 cars of bulk shipments

### UP Facilities
- **UP Global IV (Joliet):** Major intermodal interchange facility
- Classification yards for manifest freight interchange
- Direct connections to NS and CSX systems

### Strategic Importance
Chicago is the largest rail gateway in North America, providing:
- **Transcontinental connections:** West Coast to eastern U.S.
- **Market access:** Midwest distribution and consumption
- **Multiple routing options:** Competitive service via NS or CSX

### Overview
**St. Louis** is a critical mid-continent gateway where UP and NS/CSX systems meet.

### Railroads Interchanged
- **Norfolk Southern (NS)**
- **CSX Transportation (CSX)**

### Geographic Advantage
- **Mississippi River crossing:** Strategic chokepoint
- **Central U.S. location:** Midwest, South, and Southwest connectivity
- **Gateway to Southeast:** NS access to Atlanta and southeastern markets

### Traffic Types
- Manifest freight (general merchandise)
- Intermodal containers
- Automotive
- Agricultural products
- Industrial commodities

### Strategic Importance
Proposed UP-NS merger included plans to tie together:
- Union Pacific's Sunset Route
- Former Southern Railway main line (NS)
- Creating single-line route linking Southern California, Gulf Coast, Mexico, Southeast, and Northeast

## BNSF Railway Interchanges

### Overview
Union Pacific interchanges with its primary western competitor, **BNSF Railway**, at multiple locations.

### Major Interchange Points
- **Chicago:** Eastern gateway
- **Kansas City:** Central gateway
- **Houston:** Gulf Coast petrochemical and port traffic
- **Memphis:** Mid-South gateway
- **Pacific Northwest:** Oregon and Washington regional traffic
- **California:** Regional interchange points

### Traffic Types
- **Overhead traffic:** Movements requiring both railroads for origin-destination coverage
- **Short-line connections:** Serving customers accessible to one railroad
- **Competitive routing:** Alternative routing options for shippers
- **Port access:** Shared port serving requirements

### Strategic Relationship
UP and BNSF compete in many markets but also cooperate for:
- **Powder River Basin coal:** Joint Line operations in Wyoming
- **Port access:** Shared terminal facilities in some locations
- **Through routes:** Enabling complete origin-destination service

## Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC) Interchanges

### Overview
Following the 2023 merger of Canadian Pacific and Kansas City Southern, **CPKC** provides Union Pacific with interchange opportunities for Canada and Mexico traffic.

### Major Interchange Points
- **Kansas City:** Primary gateway
- **Houston:** Port Terminal Railroad Association (PTRA) provides shared access to petrochemical plants and port facilities
- **Other Texas locations:** Regional interchange

### Traffic Types
- **Canada-U.S. traffic:** Canadian grain, potash, forest products
- **Mexico-U.S. traffic:** Cross-border manufactured goods, agricultural products
- **Industrial products:** Chemicals, automotive, consumer goods

### Houston PTRA Cooperation
In Houston, the **Port Terminal Railroad Association** provides Union Pacific, BNSF, and CPKC with shared access to:
- Major petrochemical plant cluster
- Port facilities
- Industrial distribution centers

## Short Line and Regional Railroad Interchanges

### Overview
Union Pacific connects with **dozens of short line and regional railroads** across its 23-state network, providing first-mile/last-mile service to customers not directly on UP's main lines.

### Functions
- **Origination:** Customers located on short lines
- **Termination:** Final delivery via short line connections
- **Switching:** Local industry service
- **Branch line access:** Former UP lines sold to short line operators

### Examples
- Regional railroads in agricultural states (grain elevators)
- Industrial switching railroads (port and plant switching)
- Tourist and heritage railroads (limited freight operations)

## International Gateways

### Mexico Interchanges

Union Pacific is the **only railroad serving all six major gateways to Mexico**, interchanging with Mexican railroads:

#### Ferromex (Ferrocarril Mexicano)
- Major Mexican railroad serving western and central Mexico
- Interchanges at all six UP Mexico gateways

#### Kansas City Southern de México (KCSM, now part of CPKC)
- Serves eastern Mexico and Mexico City
- Primary interchange at Laredo

### Interchange Gateways
1. **Laredo, Texas** (largest U.S.-Mexico rail crossing)
2. **El Paso, Texas/Ciudad Juárez**
3. **Eagle Pass, Texas**
4. **Brownsville, Texas**
5. **Nogales, Arizona**
6. **Calexico, California**

### Traffic Types
- **Automotive:** Finished vehicles and parts from maquiladora plants
- **Intermodal:** Cross-border container traffic
- **Industrial products:** Manufacturing components, consumer goods
- **Agricultural products:** Two-way agricultural trade
- **Chemicals:** Cross-border industrial products

## Committed Gateway Pricing

### Overview
In January 2026, Union Pacific announced **Committed Gateway Pricing** initiatives designed to share merger benefits through enhanced gateway service.

### Concept
- Improved pricing and service at major interchange gateways
- Enhanced reliability at gateway locations
- Competitive routing options for shippers
- Benefits without requiring railroad consolidation

### Strategic Importance
Following the rejection of the UP-NS merger proposal, Committed Gateway Pricing demonstrates UP's focus on:
- Competitive gateway operations
- Customer service improvements
- Interchange efficiency enhancements

## Future Interchange Considerations

### Merger Discussions (2025-2026)
The proposed **Union Pacific-Norfolk Southern merger** (rejected January 2026) would have fundamentally altered interchange patterns:

#### Proposed Changes
- **Reduced interchange:** UP-NS traffic would become single-line
- **Shifted patterns:** Remaining interchange concentrated with CSX and BNSF
- **Competitive concerns:** Reduced routing options for shippers

#### Rejection Impact
Maintaining the current competitive landscape preserves:
- **Multiple routing options:** Shippers can choose UP/NS or UP/CSX
- **Competitive pricing:** Railroad competition at gateways
- **Service alternatives:** Backup routing during service disruptions

## Traffic Flow Patterns

### Eastbound (UP Origin)
Eastbound shipments originating on Union Pacific can be interchanged with:
- **Norfolk Southern** at Chicago, St. Louis, Kansas City, Memphis, or New Orleans
- **CSX** at Chicago, St. Louis, Kansas City, Memphis, or New Orleans

### Westbound (Eastern Origin)
Westbound shipments originating on Norfolk Southern or CSX can be interchanged to:
- **Union Pacific** for western U.S. delivery
- **BNSF** for alternative western routing

### Competitive Routing
Shippers typically choose railroad routing based on:
- **Service reliability:** On-time performance
- **Transit time:** Fastest route to destination
- **Pricing:** Competitive rate offerings
- **Capacity:** Equipment and space availability
