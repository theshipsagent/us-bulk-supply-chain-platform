## Major Transcontinental Corridors

Union Pacific operates several historic and strategically important corridors connecting the West Coast with the Midwest and eastern gateways.

---

## Sunset Route

### Overview
The **Sunset Route** is a major east-west railroad mainline spanning approximately **1,995 miles** from New Orleans, Louisiana, to Los Angeles, California.

### Geography
Traverses **five states:**
1. Louisiana
2. Texas
3. New Mexico
4. Arizona
5. California

### Key Cities and Junctions
- **New Orleans, Louisiana** (eastern terminus)
- **Houston, Texas** (major intermediate point)
- **San Antonio, Texas**
- **El Paso, Texas** (Mexico gateway)
- **Tucson, Arizona**
- **Phoenix, Arizona** (branch line)
- **Yuma, Arizona**
- **Los Angeles, California** (western terminus)

### Traffic Volume and Characteristics
- Handles approximately **20% of Union Pacific's total traffic**
- **Major commodities:**
  - Intermodal containers from West Coast ports
  - Cross-border goods to/from Mexico
  - Automobiles and auto parts through Mexico gateways (Nogales, AZ and Calexico, CA)
  - Chemicals and petrochemicals from Gulf Coast
  - Agricultural products
  - Industrial products

### Strategic Importance
The Sunset Route is critical for:
- **West Coast-Gulf Coast connectivity**
- **Mexico cross-border trade** (Nogales, Arizona and Calexico, California gateways)
- **Southern transcontinental freight flows**
- **Port connectivity** (Los Angeles and Gulf Coast ports)

### 2005 Enhancement
Union Pacific highlighted the Sunset Route's importance in 2005 with capital investments as traffic boomed, underscoring its strategic value for transcontinental commerce.

## Overland Route

### Historical Context
The **Overland Route** was the first transcontinental railroad corridor, operated jointly by Union Pacific Railroad and Central Pacific Railroad/Southern Pacific Railroad.

### Geography
Connects:
- **Eastern termini:** Council Bluffs, Iowa, and Omaha, Nebraska (UP headquarters)
- **Western terminus:** San Francisco Bay Area, California

### Current Operations
- The Overland Route remains a common name for the line from **northern California to Chicago**
- Now owned entirely by Union Pacific
- **Primary use:** Freight service
- **Passenger service:** Portions used by Amtrak's California Zephyr

### Key Segments
- **Omaha to Cheyenne, Wyoming:** Central Plains segment
- **Cheyenne to Ogden, Utah:** Mountain crossing via Sherman Hill
- **Ogden to Sacramento/Oakland:** Sierra Nevada crossing
- **Chicago connections:** Via Iowa and Illinois

### Major Commodities
- **Intermodal containers:** Transcontinental double-stack trains
- **Manifest freight:** General merchandise
- **Agricultural products:** Grain from Nebraska and Iowa
- **Automotive:** Finished vehicles from California
- **Consumer goods:** E-commerce and retail distribution

### Strategic Importance
- **Northern transcontinental corridor**
- **Shortest route** from San Francisco Bay Area to Chicago
- **High-capacity main line:** Double and triple track in key segments
- **Technology corridor:** Silicon Valley to Midwest markets

## Powder River Basin Coal Route

### Overview
Dedicated coal corridor from Wyoming's Powder River Basin to power plants in the Midwest and Southwest.

### Route
- **Shawnee Junction, Wyoming** to **O'Fallons, Nebraska**
- Connects to UP's southern and midwestern network

### Joint Line Operations
BNSF Railway and Union Pacific jointly operate:
- **103-mile triple-track Joint Line** through the southern Powder River Basin
- **21 miles of quadruple track** over Logan Hill
- **50-mile stretch** serving eight mine complexes

### Production
The Joint Line serves coal mines producing nearly **40% of the coal used in U.S. electricity generation**.

### Traffic Characteristics
- **Unit coal trains:** 100+ car trains in continuous loop service
- **High-volume corridor:** Despite declining coal demand, infrastructure maintained
- **Destination markets:** Southwestern and Midwestern power plants

## Mexico Gateway Corridors

Union Pacific is the **only railroad serving all six major gateways to Mexico**.

### Laredo, Texas Gateway
- **Largest U.S.-Mexico rail crossing**
- Automotive, intermodal, industrial products
- Direct connection to Mexican interior

### El Paso, Texas/Ciudad Juárez Gateway
- **Sunset Route connection**
- Automotive (maquiladora plants)
- Intermodal and general merchandise

### Eagle Pass, Texas Gateway
- Automotive and industrial products
- Coal exports to Mexico

### Brownsville, Texas Gateway
- Agricultural products
- Industrial goods
- Intermodal

### Nogales, Arizona Gateway
- **Sunset Route connection**
- Automobiles and auto parts
- Fresh produce (northbound)
- Consumer goods

### Calexico, California Gateway
- **Sunset Route connection**
- Automobiles and auto parts
- Agricultural products
- Intermodal containers

### Strategic Importance
Union Pacific's comprehensive Mexico gateway coverage provides:
- **USMCA trade support:** U.S.-Mexico-Canada Agreement commerce
- **Automotive supply chain:** Maquiladora and assembly plant connectivity
- **Agricultural trade:** Two-way commodity flows
- **Intermodal growth:** Cross-border container movements

## Eastern Gateway Connections

### Chicago
- **Primary eastern interchange** with CSX and Norfolk Southern
- Major intermodal hub (Global IV)
- Manifest freight interchange

### St. Louis
- **Mid-continent gateway** for UP/NS and UP/CSX interchange
- Strategic Mississippi River crossing

### Kansas City
- **Central gateway** with BNSF, NS, and other carriers
- Agricultural and industrial interchange

### Memphis
- **Mid-South gateway** with NS and CSX
- Cotton, agricultural products, industrial goods

### New Orleans
- **Gulf Coast gateway** with NS (former Southern Railway main line)
- Sunset Route eastern terminus
- Port and industrial interchange

### Shreveport, Louisiana
- **Regional gateway** with Kansas City Southern (now CPKC)
- Timber products and regional freight

## Corridor Performance Metrics

### Traffic Density
- **Sunset Route:** 20% of total UP traffic
- **Overland Route:** High-density intermodal corridor
- **Powder River Basin:** Coal unit trains (declining volumes but maintained capacity)
- **Mexico corridors:** Growing cross-border trade

### Service Characteristics
- **Intermodal trains:** Premium scheduled service
- **Manifest trains:** General merchandise, multiple commodities
- **Unit trains:** Dedicated commodity movements (coal, grain, automotive)
- **Local service:** Switching and industry service
