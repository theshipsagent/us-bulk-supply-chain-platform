## Classification Yards

Classification yards are the nerve centers of Union Pacific's network, where freight cars are sorted and assembled into trains for their destinations.

---

## Bailey Yard — North Platte, Nebraska

### Overview
**The world's largest railroad classification yard**

### Location
North Platte, Nebraska (central Nebraska on the Overland Route)

### Size and Scale
- **Total area:** 2,850 acres (4.45 square miles / 11.5 km²)
- **Length:** Over 8 miles (13 km)
- **Width:** 2 miles (3.2 km) at widest point
- **Track infrastructure:**
  - 200 separate tracks
  - 315 miles (507 km) of total track
  - 985 switches
  - 766 turnouts
  - 17 receiving tracks
  - 16 departure tracks

### Operations
- **Daily volume:** 10,000 railroad cars handled every 24 hours
- **Daily sorting:** 3,000 cars sorted in eastward and westward classification yards
- **Hump yards:** Dual hump classification (eastward and westward)
- **24/7 operations:** Continuous train makeup and classification

### Workforce
More than **2,600 Union Pacific employees** in North Platte, most working at Bailey Yard operations.

### Facilities
- **Locomotive maintenance facilities:** Major repair and servicing capability
- **Car repair operations:** Supporting ongoing equipment reliability
- **Crew facilities:** Lodging, training, and support infrastructure
- **Fueling facilities:** Locomotive refueling operations

### Named After
**Edd H. Bailey**, former Union Pacific president

### Visitor Information
**Golden Spike Tower and Visitor Center** provides public viewing of Bailey Yard operations, offering an observation deck overlooking the massive classification complex.

### Strategic Importance
Bailey Yard is the critical hub for Union Pacific's transcontinental operations, sorting freight moving between:
- West Coast and Midwest/Eastern gateways
- Powder River Basin coal trains
- Manifest freight from multiple origins/destinations
- Intermodal trains requiring re-blocking

## Davis Yard — Roseville, California

### Overview
One of the largest operating railroad yards on the West Coast, serving as the primary gateway for **98% of all traffic in Northern California**.

### Location
Roseville, California (northeast of Sacramento)

### History
- **Opened:** 1906
- **Major renovations:** 1952 and 1999
- Continuous operations for over 115 years

### Operations
Handles manifest carload traffic to and from Northern California, including:
- Transcontinental freight
- Local/regional distribution
- Port traffic from Oakland and Bay Area
- Agricultural products from Central Valley
- Automotive and intermodal

### Strategic Importance
Davis Yard greatly improves the Union Pacific network to and from Northern California, serving as the primary sorting and distribution hub for:
- Silicon Valley technology shipments
- Northern California agriculture
- Port of Oakland container traffic
- Automotive distribution
- Consumer goods distribution

### Facilities
- Classification tracks
- Locomotive servicing
- Car repair facilities
- Crew facilities

## Grain Shuttle Facilities

Union Pacific serves numerous **shuttle train facilities** (loop-track elevators) for efficient grain loading.

---

## MFA Facility — Hamilton, Missouri

### Capacity
- **60,000 bushels per hour** loading rate
- Two 1,500-bushel dump pits (8-minute unload time)
- 30,000-bushel-per-hour conveyor belt

### Infrastructure
- Self-service weigh scale
- Circular railroad track (loop)
- Direct connection to UP main line

### Service
110-car shuttle trains for export and domestic markets

---

## Cargill Atchison — Atchison, Kansas

### Capacity Expansion
Expanded from 75-car to **110-car shuttle train capability**

### Partnership
Joint Union Pacific and Cargill investment

### Operations
High-speed grain loading for export via Gulf Coast and Pacific Northwest terminals
