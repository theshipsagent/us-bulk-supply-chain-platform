## Overview

Union Pacific is a major carrier of lumber and forest products, serving the western United States timber industry and connecting producers with markets nationwide. Lumber is part of UP's "Metals, Minerals & Forest" commodity group.

## Commodities Shipped

### Lumber Products
- Dimensional lumber
- Oriented Strand Board (OSB)
- Engineered Wood Products (EWP)
- Plywood/veneer
- Particleboard
- Mass timber
- Treated lumber

### Wood Products
- Railroad ties
- Utility poles
- Logs
- Bark
- Composite decking/lumber
- Wallboard

## Markets and Regions Served

Union Pacific serves forest products throughout its 23-state network, with particular strength in:

- **Pacific Northwest:** Major timber-producing regions in Oregon and Washington
- **Mountain West:** Idaho, Montana timber areas
- **Southern timber regions:** Texas, Louisiana
- **Distribution markets:** Major metropolitan areas across the western U.S.

The network connects sawmills, processing facilities, and distribution centers with construction markets nationwide.

## Routes and Corridors

Key forest products corridors include:
- Pacific Northwest to California markets
- Pacific Northwest to Texas and Gulf Coast
- Mountain states to Midwest distribution centers
- Connections to eastern railroads for transcontinental movements

## Facilities and Terminals

Union Pacific serves:
- Major sawmill complexes in timber-producing regions
- Wood products manufacturing facilities
- Building materials distribution centers
- Reload/transload facilities for truck distribution

## Equipment Types

Specialized equipment for forest products:
- Center-beam flatcars for dimensional lumber and panels
- Bulkhead flatcars for logs and specialty products
- Covered hoppers for wood chips and bark
- Standard flatcars for packaged products

## Volume and Service Information

Forest products represent a significant portion of Union Pacific's industrial products segment. Demand is driven by:
- Residential construction activity
- Commercial construction projects
- Repair and remodeling markets
- Export demand

## Market Outlook (2026)

Strong demand anticipated from construction sector growth, with nonresidential construction forecasted to drive increased lumber and forest products shipments in 2025-2026.
