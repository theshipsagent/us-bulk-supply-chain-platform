## Overview

Union Pacific provides comprehensive agricultural transportation services throughout America's heartland, connecting grain elevators, fertilizer plants, and agricultural processors with domestic and export markets. Agriculture represents a significant portion of UP's commodity mix.

### Fertilizer

### Other Agricultural Products

- Processed agricultural commodities

## Grain Elevator Facilities

Union Pacific serves a comprehensive network of grain elevators and shuttle facilities throughout its agricultural territory.

### Shuttle Train Facilities

**Loop-Track/Shuttle Facilities:** These modern facilities feature circular railroad tracks connected to Union Pacific's main line, enabling efficient unit train loading.

#### Example: MFA Facility, Hamilton, Missouri
- **Capacity:** 60,000 bushels per hour loading rate
- **Infrastructure:**
  - Two 1,500-bushel dump pits (8-minute unload time)
  - 30,000-bushel-per-hour conveyor belt
  - Self-service weigh scale
- **Speed:** Rapid turnaround for 100+ car shuttle trains

#### Cargill Atchison, Kansas (Expanded)
- **Capacity expansion:** From 75-car to **110-car shuttle trains**
- Partnership with Union Pacific and Cargill
- Enhanced efficiency and throughput

### Shuttle Service Benefits
- **Faster loading:** 110-car trains loaded in hours vs. days
- **Lower costs:** Economies of scale
- **Improved efficiency:** Continuous loop operations
- **Market access:** Direct connection to export terminals and processors

## Fertilizer Facilities

Union Pacific serves fertilizer production and distribution facilities throughout its agricultural service territory.

### Liquid Fertilizer Infrastructure
**Example: Republic County, Kansas Facility**
- **3.5-million-gallon liquid fertilizer plant**
- Rail and truck receiving capability
- Integrated with grain shuttle facility
- Seasonal service patterns aligned with planting seasons

### Service Patterns
Fertilizer movements follow agricultural calendars:
- **Spring application:** Peak movements before planting
- **Fall application:** Secondary peak after harvest
- **Storage:** Winter positioning for spring demand

## Markets and Regions Served

Union Pacific's agricultural network spans major grain-producing states:

### Primary Grain Regions
- **Kansas:** Major wheat and grain sorghum production
- **Nebraska:** Corn and soybean belt
- **Iowa:** Corn production (via connections)
- **Missouri:** Diverse grain production
- **Texas:** Sorghum and cotton regions
- **Other states:** Throughout UP's 23-state network

### Destination Markets
- **Export terminals:** Pacific Northwest and Gulf Coast ports
- **Processors:** Ethanol plants, flour mills, feed manufacturers
- **Domestic consumers:** Food manufacturers, livestock operations

## Routes and Corridors

Key agricultural corridors:

### Grain Export Routes
- **Heartland to Pacific Northwest:** Grain to export terminals
- **Central Plains to Gulf Coast:** Via Sunset Route to New Orleans/Texas ports
- **Cross-border to Mexico:** Agricultural exports to Mexican markets

### Fertilizer Routes
- **Import movements:** Gulf Coast ports to interior distribution
- **Domestic production:** Manufacturing plants to agricultural regions
- **Seasonal flows:** Aligned with planting calendars

## Equipment Types

- **Covered hoppers:** Standard grain cars (100-ton capacity)
- **Jumbo covered hoppers:** Higher-capacity cars
- **Unit trains:** 100+ car shuttle trains

### Fertilizer Equipment
- **Covered hoppers:** Dry fertilizer products
- **Tank cars:** Liquid fertilizers
- **Mixed manifest:** Smaller lot sizes

## Service Challenges (2022-2023)

Union Pacific faced service challenges that impacted agricultural customers:

### Traffic Limitations
- UP placed limits on rail traffic affecting major fertilizer companies
- **CF Industries** forced to cut shipments nearly 20%
- Federal regulators held hearings on service problems

### Impacts
- Grain mills and ethanol plants curtailed production waiting on trains
- Farmers faced limited selling options due to grain elevator shipping difficulties
- Supply chain disruptions during critical agricultural seasons

### Response
Federal oversight and railroad operational improvements to address service reliability issues.

## Volume and Service Information

Agriculture represents a seasonal, high-volume commodity group:

- **Harvest season peaks:** Fall grain movements
- **Spring fertilizer:** Pre-planting season surge
- **Export demand:** Ongoing international market flows
- **Ethanol production:** Year-round corn movements to processing plants

## Market Outlook

Agricultural commodities remain core to Union Pacific's business:
- Strong export demand to Asia and Mexico
- Domestic ethanol production supporting corn movements
- Growing fertilizer demand with agricultural intensification
- Infrastructure improvements enhancing shuttle train efficiency
