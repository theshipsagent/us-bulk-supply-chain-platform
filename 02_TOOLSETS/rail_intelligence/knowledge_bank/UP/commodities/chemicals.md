# UP — Chemicals & Plastics

**Source:** Multiple (see sources below)
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

Union Pacific provides specialized transportation services for chemicals, plastics, and petroleum products across its 23-state network. Chemical commodities represent a diverse and significant portion of UP's freight mix, requiring specialized equipment and safety protocols.

## Commodities Shipped

### Industrial Chemicals
- Basic industrial chemicals
- Specialty chemicals
- Chemical intermediates

### Plastics
- Plastic resins
- Plastic pellets
- Finished plastic products

### Petroleum Products
- Petroleum
- Liquid Petroleum Gases (LPG)
- Crude oil
- Refined petroleum products

### Other Chemical Products
- Fertilizer (see separate agriculture section)
- Soda ash (see minerals section)

## Markets and Regions Served

Union Pacific's chemical network connects:

### Key Chemical Producing Regions
- **Gulf Coast:** Houston-area petrochemical complex (largest in North America)
- **Texas:** Major chemical manufacturing corridor
- **Louisiana:** Petrochemical facilities
- **California:** Refineries and chemical plants

### Consumption Markets
- Manufacturing facilities throughout 23-state network
- Agricultural regions (fertilizer)
- Industrial customers nationwide

## Routes and Corridors

Key chemical corridors:
- **Gulf Coast to West Coast:** Refineries to California markets
- **Gulf Coast to Midwest:** Petrochemicals to industrial consumers
- **Cross-border to Mexico:** Six major gateways serving Mexican chemical industry
- **Sunset Route:** Major chemical corridor from Houston/New Orleans to Los Angeles

## Facilities and Terminals

Union Pacific serves:
- Petrochemical plant complexes
- Refineries
- Chemical transload facilities
- Tank terminal facilities
- Port facilities on Gulf Coast and West Coast
- Plastics manufacturing plants

### Houston Area Access
In Houston, the **Port Terminal Railroad Association** provides Union Pacific (along with BNSF and Canadian Pacific Kansas City) access to a cluster of major petrochemical plants and port facilities.

## Equipment Types

Specialized tank car fleet for chemicals:
- **Tank cars:** Various configurations for different chemical products
- **Pressure tank cars:** LPG and pressurized gases
- **Insulated tank cars:** Temperature-sensitive products
- **DOT-specification tank cars:** Meeting federal safety standards
- **Unit trains:** High-volume chemical movements

## Safety and Compliance

Union Pacific maintains comprehensive hazardous materials (Hazmat) safety programs:

### Hazmat Response Teams
- Regionally located across UP's 23-state network
- Four-prong mission: **Prevention, Preparedness, Response, Recovery**
- Specialized training and equipment for chemical incidents
- Coordination with local emergency responders

### Safety Recognition
Union Pacific regularly honors chemical shippers for safe transportation practices, recognizing companies that maintain excellent safety records in chemical transportation.

## Volume and Service Information

Chemicals represent one of Union Pacific's most diversified commodity groups, with stable demand from:
- Manufacturing sector
- Agricultural industry (fertilizers)
- Energy sector (crude oil, petroleum products)
- Consumer products industries

## Industry Importance

According to the Association of American Railroads (AAR), the freight rail industry is critical to the chemical industry's supply chain, with railroads safely moving millions of carloads of chemicals annually.

## Market Outlook

Chemical shipments remain a stable, core business for Union Pacific, with:
- Ongoing demand from petrochemical sector
- Agricultural fertilizer movements (seasonal peaks)
- Energy sector crude oil and refined products
- Plastics demand tied to manufacturing activity
