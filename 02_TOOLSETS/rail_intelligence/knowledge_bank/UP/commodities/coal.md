## Overview

Union Pacific is one of the two major railroads (along with BNSF) serving the Powder River Basin (PRB) in Wyoming, the largest coal-producing region in the United States. Coal has historically been a major commodity for UP, though volumes have declined with changes in energy markets.

## Commodities Shipped

- **Steam coal:** For electric power generation
- **Thermal coal:** Power plant fuel
- **Powder River Basin sub-bituminous coal:** Low-sulfur coal for environmental compliance

## Primary Source Region: Powder River Basin

### Joint Line Operations
BNSF Railway and Union Pacific Railroad operate a **103-mile triple-track Joint Line** in the southern Powder River Basin:

- **21 miles of quadruple track** over Logan Hill
- **50-mile stretch** serving eight mine complexes
- Produces nearly **40% of coal used in U.S. electricity generation**

### UP Coal Route
Union Pacific's dedicated coal route runs between:
- **Shawnee Junction, Wyoming** (PRB)
- **O'Fallons, Nebraska** (connection to southern UP network)

## Markets and Regions Served

Union Pacific hauls coal from Wyoming's Powder River Basin to power plants located primarily in:
- **Southwest United States**
- **Midwest United States**
- Texas and surrounding states
- Electric utilities throughout UP's service territory

## Equipment and Operations

### Unit Trains
Coal moves in dedicated unit trains:
- **100+ car trains:** Continuous loop service between mine and power plant
- **Rapid loading:** Advanced mine loading facilities
- **Aluminum coal gondolas:** Lightweight cars for maximum payload

### Infrastructure
- High-capacity main lines capable of handling heavy coal traffic
- Advanced signaling and dispatching systems
- Maintenance facilities dedicated to coal car fleet

## Volume Trends and Market Outlook

### Historical Significance
Coal has been one of Union Pacific's largest commodities by tonnage and a significant revenue generator for decades.

### Declining Volumes
Coal volumes have declined due to:
- Natural gas competition in electric power generation
- Renewable energy growth (wind, solar)
- Coal plant retirements
- Environmental regulations

### 2026 Outlook
Despite declining volumes, Union Pacific and BNSF maintain PRB infrastructure:
- **No plans to remove capacity** from Powder River Basin (as of 2025-2026)
- UP CEO Jim Vena stated: "It would cost more to remove track than to continue to maintain it"
- No plans to remove third main lines or related infrastructure
- Maintaining operational capability for remaining coal traffic

## Recent Developments

### UP-NS Merger Impact (2026)
In January 2026, federal regulators **rejected** Union Pacific and Norfolk Southern's proposed merger. This decision:
- Gave breathing room to competitors
- Addressed concerns that the merger would raise costs for Wyoming coal producers
- Maintained competitive dynamics in PRB coal transportation

## Facilities and Terminals

### Coal Mines Served
Union Pacific serves multiple coal mine complexes in the Powder River Basin, connecting to:
- Mine loading facilities
- Rail loadout silos
- Unit train staging areas

### Destination Facilities
- Coal-fired power plants throughout service territory
- Utility-owned rail sidings
- Coal storage yards at power stations

## Service Characteristics

- **Unit train service:** Dedicated trainsets cycling between mine and power plant
- **Long-term contracts:** Typically multi-year agreements with utilities
- **Scheduled service:** Regular delivery patterns to maintain power plant stockpiles
- **Weather resilience:** Operations continue year-round in challenging Wyoming conditions
