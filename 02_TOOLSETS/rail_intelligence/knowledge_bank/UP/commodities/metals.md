## Overview

Union Pacific provides comprehensive transportation services for ferrous and non-ferrous metals throughout its network. Metals are a key component of UP's industrial products segment.

## Commodities Shipped

### Ferrous Metals
- Sheet steel
- Steel plate
- Rebar
- Steel beams
- Wire rod
- Pipe
- Ferrous scrap

### Non-Ferrous Metals
- Aluminum
- Copper
- Other non-ferrous metals and ores

## Markets and Regions Served

Union Pacific serves metal producers, processors, and consumers across its 23-state network in the western two-thirds of the United States. The network connects:

- Manufacturing facilities
- Steel mills and processing plants
- Scrap yards
- Construction sites (via distribution centers)
- Port facilities on the West Coast and Gulf Coast

## Service Characteristics

Metal products are a significant driver of UP's industrial products business, with demand closely tied to:
- Commercial construction activity
- Highway construction projects
- Manufacturing sector performance
- Infrastructure investment

## Equipment Types

Union Pacific uses specialized equipment for metals transportation:
- Gondola cars for scrap and coiled products
- Flatcars for structural steel and pipe
- Covered hoppers for certain processed metals

## Volume and Market Outlook

**2026 Forecast:** Strong demand expected from construction and intermodal markets, with forecasted sales growth from nonresidential construction expected to fuel demand in 2025-2026.

Commercial and highway construction projects drive shipments of steel and construction products, positioning metals as a growth commodity for Union Pacific's industrial products segment.

## Infrastructure Investment

Union Pacific has committed $3.3 billion for infrastructure investment in 2026, supporting enhanced service for metals and other industrial products.
