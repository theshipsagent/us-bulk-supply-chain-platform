## File Types & Content

### Commodity Files (commodities/*.md)
Each commodity file contains:
- Overview of carrier's role in that commodity market
- Commodities and product types
- Source regions (origins)
- Destination markets
- Equipment used (car types)
- Routes and corridors
- Facilities (terminals, transload, etc.)
- Service characteristics (unit trains, manifest, etc.)
- Market trends
- Sources cited

**Example:** `BNSF/commodities/grain.md` covers BNSF's grain transportation including shuttle trains, export gateways (PNW, Gulf), routes, and equipment.

### Network Overview Files (network_overview.md)
- Headquarters, route miles, service territory
- Geographic coverage (states/provinces)
- Commodity revenue mix (where available)
- Key network features
- Competitive position
- Recent developments

### Routes & Corridors Files (routes_corridors.md)
- Major transcontinental routes
- Regional corridors
- Key junction points
- Double-stack clearances
- Infrastructure highlights
- Interchange locations

### Facilities Files (facilities.md)
- Intermodal terminals
- Classification yards
- Specialized facilities (auto ramps, coal terminals, etc.)
- Transload facilities
- Maintenance facilities

### Interchanges Files (interchanges.md)
- Class I railroad interchange points
- Border gateways (Canada, Mexico)
- Belt lines and terminal railroads
- Reciprocal switching arrangements
- Trackage rights

### Ports & International Files (ports_international.md)
- Pacific, Atlantic, Gulf, Great Lakes port access
- Export/import commodities
- International trade routes
- Single-line advantages (CPKC)

## Cross-Carrier Reference Notes

### interchange_matrix.md
Comprehensive analysis of where Class I carriers connect:
- Chicago (all carriers)
- Kansas City (BNSF, UP, NS, CPKC)
- Memphis (all except BNSF)
- Pacific Northwest (BNSF/UP shared trackage)
- Powder River Basin (BNSF/UP Joint Line)
- Border crossings and Mexico gateways

### commodity_coverage.md
Matrix showing which carriers handle which commodities:
- Grain (all carriers, BNSF/UP/CN/CPKC strongest)
- Coal (BNSF/UP PRB, NS/CSX Appalachia)
- Intermodal (all carriers major players)
- Automotive (NS largest, CSX/UP/CPKC significant)
- Chemicals (all carriers serve Gulf Coast)
- Forest products (BNSF/CN/CPKC Canadian/PNW sources)

### construction_materials.md
**Priority research area** covering:
- **Cement:** NS serves 15 facilities, BNSF 15+, all carriers present
- **Aggregates:** NS market leader (67 quarries + 30 distribution yards), frac sand specialty for BNSF/UP
- **Steel:** NS transportation leader (50+ processors), CSX Midwest access
- **Lumber:** BNSF PNW leader, CN/CPKC Canadian sources
- Unit train vs. manifest service
- Equipment types (covered hoppers, gondolas, coil cars, centerbeam flatcars)
- Market drivers and trends

### data_gaps.md
Documentation of:
- Website access issues (UP blocked, CSX 403 errors, NS redirect)
- Information not publicly available (pricing, exact addresses, volumes)
- Dynamic content not captured (interactive maps)
- Login-required customer portals
- Recommended follow-up actions

## Use Cases

### Market Analysis
- Identify which carriers serve specific commodity markets
- Compare carrier strengths across geographic regions
- Understand commodity flow patterns (origins to destinations)

### Route Planning
- Plan transcontinental movements using interchange points
- Identify single-line service options (CPKC Canada-US-Mexico advantage)
- Understand double-stack clearance corridors (NS Crescent/Heartland, BNSF Transcons)

### Competitive Intelligence
- Compare carrier facility counts (NS 67 quarries vs. others)
- Analyze revenue mix (CN 22% intermodal, 20% petroleum, 20% grain)
- Understand strategic investments (NS Crescent Corridor $2.5B, CPKC Lazaro Cardenas $200M)

### Construction Materials Research
- Find cement terminals (NS 15, BNSF 15+)
- Identify aggregate sources (quarries, distribution yards)
- Understand steel distribution networks (NS 50+ processors)
- Locate lumber mills and reload centers (BNSF PNW, CN Canada)

### Logistics Planning
- Understand port access (Atlantic, Pacific, Gulf, Great Lakes)
- Plan intermodal movements (terminal locations, equipment)
- Identify transload facilities (truck-to-rail conversion)

## Carrier Highlights

### BNSF Railway
- **Strength:** Western U.S., Powder River Basin coal, Pacific Northwest grain/lumber, intermodal (Northern/Southern Transcons)
- **Files:** 19 (14 commodities + 5 supporting)
- **Key Route:** Southern Transcon (fastest intermodal LA-Chicago)

### Union Pacific
- **Strength:** Western U.S., Mexico gateways (6 locations), Powder River Basin coal, Overland/Sunset routes
- **Files:** 16 (9 commodities + 6 supporting + data_gaps.md)
- **Key Facility:** Bailey Yard (world's largest classification yard)

### CSX Transportation
- **Strength:** Eastern U.S., 70+ ports, Atlantic Coast, Appalachian coal, automotive
- **Files:** 16 (11 commodities + 5 supporting)
- **Key Access:** I-95 Corridor, Northeast megalopolis

### Norfolk Southern
- **Strength:** Southeast U.S., automotive (largest in North America), Appalachian coal export, intermodal (54 terminals)
- **Files:** 12 (7 commodities + 5 supporting)
- **Key Assets:** Lamberts Point (48M tons coal), Crescent/Heartland/Meridian corridors, 67 quarries

### Canadian National
- **Strength:** Tri-coastal network (Pacific, Atlantic, Gulf), Canadian grain/potash, Prince Rupert port
- **Files:** 12 (7 commodities + 5 supporting)
- **Key Feature:** Only tri-coastal railroad in North America

### CPKC (Canadian Pacific Kansas City)
- **Strength:** Only single-line Canada-U.S.-Mexico, Saskatchewan potash, Lazaro Cardenas port (Mexico Pacific)
- **Files:** 13 (8 commodities + 5 supporting)
- **Key Advantage:** No interchange delays for tri-national traffic
