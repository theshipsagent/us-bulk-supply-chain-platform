## Network Overview

BNSF operates 32,500 miles of rail track across North America, serving 28 U.S. states and 2 Canadian provinces. The company describes itself as "a critical link that connects consumers with the global marketplace."

## Major Transcontinental Routes

### Northern Transcon (Seattle–Chicago)

**Route:** Seattle/Tacoma, WA → Spokane, WA → Havre, MT → Minot, ND → Fargo, ND → Minneapolis/St. Paul, MN → La Crosse, WI → Chicago, IL

**Key Features:**
- Premier intermodal corridor
- Grain export route (Midwest to Pacific Northwest)
- Double-stack clearance throughout
- Highest priority freight corridor
- Connects Puget Sound ports to Chicago

**Commodities:**
- Intermodal containers (international trade)
- Grain (export via Seattle/Tacoma)
- Forest products (eastbound from PNW)
- Consumer goods (both directions)

### Southern Transcon (Los Angeles–Chicago)

**Route:** Los Angeles/Long Beach, CA → San Bernardino, CA → Barstow, CA → Needles, CA → Flagstaff, AZ → Gallup, NM → Belen, NM → Amarillo, TX → Wichita, KS → Kansas City, MO → Fort Madison, IA → Galesburg, IL → Chicago, IL

**Key Features:**
- Fastest intermodal route in North America
- Primary corridor for international containers from LA/Long Beach
- Double-stack clearance
- Serves Southwest markets (Phoenix, Albuquerque)
- High-velocity merchandise traffic

**Commodities:**
- Intermodal containers (Asia imports)
- Grain (to California)
- Petroleum products
- Automotive
- Consumer goods

## Regional Corridors

### Powder River Basin Joint Line (with Union Pacific)

**Route:** Powder River Basin mines (WY/MT) → Joint Line → Alliance, NE (divergence point)

**Key Features:**
- Shared trackage with Union Pacific
- Heaviest tonnage corridor in North America
- Coal unit trains (100-135 cars)
- Multiple mine loading facilities
- Continuous shuttle operations

**Commodities:**
- Coal (sub-bituminous, low-sulfur)

### Gulf Coast Routes

**Texas/Oklahoma Corridor:**
- Amarillo, TX → Fort Worth, TX → Dallas, TX
- Fort Worth → Houston/Galveston (grain export)
- Kansas City → Oklahoma City → Fort Worth

**Louisiana Corridor:**
- Dallas/Fort Worth → Shreveport, LA → Alexandria, LA

**Key Features:**
- Grain export to Gulf Coast terminals
- Petroleum/chemical movements
- Access to Houston Ship Channel

**Commodities:**
- Grain (export)
- Chemicals
- Petroleum products
- Intermodal

### Pacific Northwest Corridors

**Cascade Route:**
- Seattle → Everett → Bellingham, WA (Canadian border)
- Seattle → Spokane → Sandpoint, ID → Montana

**Columbia River Route:**
- Portland, OR → Pasco, WA → Spokane, WA

**Key Features:**
- Grain export to Puget Sound ports
- Forest products from mills to markets
- Access to deep-water ports

### California Corridors

**Central Valley Route:**
- Stockton → Fresno → Bakersfield → Barstow
- Connection to Southern Transcon

**Coastal Route (Shared with UP):**
- Limited California coastal service

**Key Features:**
- Agricultural products (produce, wine, food)
- Petroleum from Central Valley fields
- Consumer goods distribution

### Midwest/Great Plains Network

**Kansas City Hub:**
- Connections to all major BNSF routes
- Interchange with UP, CPKC, NS

**Chicago Gateway:**
- Multiple routes into Chicago
- Connections to eastern carriers (NS, CSX, CN, CPKC)
- Major intermodal terminals

**Minneapolis/St. Paul:**
- Grain origins
- Northern Transcon junction

## Coal Distribution Routes

- PRB → Midwest utilities (Iowa, Wisconsin, Minnesota)
- PRB → Great Plains utilities (Kansas, Nebraska, Oklahoma)
- PRB → Texas (ERCOT grid)
- PRB → Southwest (Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona)

## International Gateways

### Canadian Border Crossings

- **Pacific Northwest:** New Westminster, BC; Sumas, WA
- **Northern Tier:** Portal, ND; Sweet Grass, MT
- **Manitoba:** Gretna, MB (via running rights)

### Mexico Connections

- BNSF reaches Mexican gateways via trackage rights and interchange with UP and CPKC
- Primary connections through El Paso, TX region

## Interchange Points with Other Class I Carriers

- **Chicago:** All Class I carriers
- **Kansas City:** UP, CPKC, NS (via trackage)
- **Memphis:** UP, CSX, NS, CN
- **St. Louis:** UP, CSX, NS
- **New Orleans area:** UP, CSX, NS, CN (via trackage)
- **Minneapolis/St. Paul:** UP, CN, CPKC
- **Pacific Northwest:** UP (extensive shared trackage)

## Shortline Connections

BNSF connects with approximately 200 shortline, regional, and switch carriers to provide comprehensive door-to-door service across North America.

## Infrastructure Highlights

- **Positive Train Control (PTC):** Deployed across main lines
- **Double-stack clearance:** Northern and Southern Transcons
- **Centralized Traffic Control (CTC):** Main corridors
- **Directional running:** Shared with UP in certain territories
