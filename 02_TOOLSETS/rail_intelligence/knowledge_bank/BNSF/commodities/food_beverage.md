## Overview

BNSF transports food and beverages by railroad, stating: "Moving food and beverages by railroad may seem complicated, but with the right equipment and decades of experience, we are a great partner to get your product where it needs to go." The company emphasizes "consistent and cost-effective service" for products of any size.

## Equipment

- **Refrigerated Boxcars:**
  - Mechanical refrigeration
  - Temperature control systems
  - Food-grade interiors

- **Insulated Boxcars:**
  - For products requiring temperature stability
  - No mechanical refrigeration needed

- **Standard Boxcars:**
  - Shelf-stable packaged foods
  - Canned and boxed products

- **Tank Cars:**
  - Edible oils
  - Corn syrup
  - Liquid food ingredients
  - Food-grade segregated fleet

- **Covered Hoppers:**
  - Sugar
  - Flour
  - Grain products
  - Food-grade compartments

## Temperature-Controlled Service

BNSF offers temperature-controlled shipping options for fresh and frozen foods, maintaining cold chain integrity from origin to destination.

## Routes

- California (produce, beverages) to nationwide markets
- Midwest (processed foods) to West Coast
- Import terminals to inland distribution centers
- Bidirectional food product movements across network

## Service Types

- Unit trains for high-volume food manufacturers
- Manifest freight for smaller shipments
- Temperature-controlled dedicated service
- Transload facilities for local delivery

## Network Access

BNSF's extensive network across:
- United States (28 states)
- Canada (2 provinces)
- Mexico (international shipping solutions)
- Overseas (port connections for international trade)
