# BNSF — Coal

**Source:** https://www.bnsf.com/ship-with-bnsf/energy/index.page
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Overview

BNSF is a major coal carrier, serving power companies with decades of experience in safely and efficiently moving coal. The company operates unit trains from western coal basins to power plants across the United States.

## Coal Basins Served

- **Powder River Basin (PRB)** — Wyoming/Montana (primary source)
  - Largest coal-producing region in North America
  - Low-sulfur sub-bituminous coal
  - BNSF operates the Powder River Basin Joint Line (with UP)

- **Other basins:**
  - Colorado (limited production)
  - New Mexico
  - Montana (non-PRB mines)

## Destination Markets

- **Electric Utilities:**
  - Midwest power plants (Iowa, Illinois, Wisconsin, Minnesota)
  - Great Plains utilities (Kansas, Nebraska, Oklahoma)
  - Texas power plants (ERCOT grid)
  - Western utilities (Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona, Nevada)
  - Pacific Northwest (limited, declining)

- **Export Terminals:**
  - West Coast terminals (when active)
  - Gulf Coast export facilities (limited)

## Unit Train Operations

- Standard consist: 100-135 cars per train
- Dedicated power plant loops (continuous shuttle service)
- Rapid loading at mine (2-4 hours)
- Rapid unloading at power plant (rotary dumpers)
- Continuous cycle operation

## Routes

- **Powder River Basin Joint Line** (shared with Union Pacific)
  - PRB mines to interchange points
  - Southern Wyoming to Nebraska/Colorado/Kansas

- **Southern Transcon** (PRB to Texas/Southwest)
  - Wyoming → Kansas → Texas

- **Northern routes** (PRB to Upper Midwest)
  - Wyoming → Montana → North Dakota → Minnesota

## Equipment

- Aluminum coal cars (lighter weight, higher capacity)
- Rotary-coupler equipped cars for rotary dumping
- Unit train dedicated fleet
- 120-143 ton capacity cars

## Market Trends

- Declining coal demand due to natural gas competition and renewables
- Power plant retirements reducing long-term coal volumes
- Shift from central Appalachian coal to PRB coal (lower sulfur)
- Export coal market volatility

## Facilities

- Mine-mouth loading facilities in Powder River Basin
- Power plant unloading loops across service territory
- Transload facilities for smaller industrial users

## Interchange Points

BNSF interchanges coal with other Class I carriers at:
- Chicago (to eastern utilities)
- Kansas City (to southeastern utilities via other carriers)
- Memphis (to southeastern utilities)
