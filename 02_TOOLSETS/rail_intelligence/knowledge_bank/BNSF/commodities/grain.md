# BNSF — Grain & Field Crops

## Overview

BNSF has partnered with farmers since 1854 to transport wholesome grain from America's heartland to dinner tables around the globe. The company handles field crops and field crop products as core agricultural commodities.

## Service Description

BNSF emphasizes a long-term partnership model with American producers, stating: "For 170 years, we've partnered with American producers" to develop "one of the most efficient and productive" farming supply chains globally.

## Commodities

- Field Crops (wheat, corn, soybeans, etc.)
- Field Crop Products (processed grains)

## Markets Served

- Domestic processing facilities
- Export terminals (Pacific Northwest, Gulf Coast)
- International markets globally

## Service Types

- Unit trains (shuttle trains for high-volume grain)
- Manifest freight for smaller shipments
- Direct-to-port service for export grain

## Network Access

BNSF's 32,500-mile network spans 28 states and 2 Canadian provinces, connecting grain origins in the Great Plains, Midwest, and Pacific Northwest to processing centers and export terminals.

## Key Routes

- Great Plains to Pacific Northwest (export via Seattle/Tacoma)
- Midwest to Gulf Coast (export via Houston/Galveston)
- Northern Transcon (grain movement corridor)
- Southern Transcon (grain distribution)

## Equipment

- Covered hopper cars for grain
- Unit train configurations for high-volume shuttle service
- Temperature-controlled options for specialty grains

## Export Gateways

- **Pacific Northwest**: Seattle, Tacoma ports
- **Gulf Coast**: Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi
- **California**: Oakland, Stockton (for domestic and international distribution)

## Customer Support

BNSF provides dedicated agricultural contacts and tools for rates, tracking, and account management through their customer portal.
