## Overview

BNSF moves dry and liquid fertilizer materials to support agricultural production across North America. The company transports various fertilizer types from production facilities and import terminals to agricultural distribution centers.

## Fertilizer Types

- **Dry Fertilizers:**
  - Potash (potassium chloride, potassium sulfate)
  - Phosphate (DAP, MAP)
  - Nitrogen (urea, ammonium nitrate)
  - Blended fertilizers

- **Liquid Fertilizers:**
  - Anhydrous ammonia
  - Liquid nitrogen solutions (UAN)
  - Liquid phosphate

## Source Regions

- **Potash**: Saskatchewan, Canada (via interchange)
- **Phosphate**: Florida, Idaho
- **Nitrogen**: Gulf Coast (ammonia plants), Midwest (corn belt)
- **Import Terminals**: Pacific Northwest, Gulf Coast

## Destination Markets

- Agricultural distribution centers in:
  - Great Plains (wheat, corn belt)
  - Midwest (row crops)
  - Pacific Northwest (wheat country)
  - California (specialty crops)

## Equipment

- Covered hoppers for dry fertilizers (potash, phosphate, urea)
- Pressure tank cars for anhydrous ammonia
- General service tank cars for liquid fertilizers
- Unit trains for high-volume movements

## Routes

- Canadian border (potash import) to Midwest/Great Plains
- Gulf Coast (nitrogen, phosphate) to agricultural regions
- Pacific Northwest import terminals to inland distribution
- Mine-to-market direct service

## Service Types

- Unit train service for large volumes
- Manifest freight for distribution centers
- Seasonal surge capacity for spring planting season

## Facilities

- Transload facilities for fertilizer blending
- Team tracks for local distribution
- Direct rail service to large agricultural cooperatives

## Seasonal Considerations

Fertilizer shipping peaks in late winter and early spring (pre-planting season), requiring capacity planning and equipment availability.
