# BNSF — Lumber and Forest Products

**Source:** https://www.bnsf.com/ship-with-bnsf/industrial-products/lumber.html
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23

---

## Service Coverage

BNSF is **"the only North American railroad with direct access to the lumber producing areas of the Pacific Northwest and Western Canada."**

This unique geographic advantage enables BNSF to connect Pacific Northwest timber regions directly to major metropolitan areas across North America, including Northeastern and Southeastern markets through interchange connections.

## Product Types

BNSF transports a comprehensive range of lumber and wood products:

- **Dry lumber** (kiln-dried dimensional lumber)
- **Green lumber** (freshly cut, undried)
- **Cedar lumber** (premium species)
- **Treated lumber** (pressure-treated for outdoor use)
- **Laminated beams** (engineered wood products)
- **Cedar fencing**
- **Plastic lumber** (composite/recycled materials)

## Equipment Options

**Centerbeam Flatcars** (primary choice for lumber transport):

| Length | Capacity (lbs) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **60 ft** | 198,000-225,000 | Standard centerbeam for lumber bundles |
| **73 ft** | 198,000-225,000 | Extended centerbeam for longer products |

**Design Features:** Centerbeams feature a vertical structural spine down the center of the car, allowing lumber bundles to be loaded on both sides while preventing shifting during transit.

**Alternative Equipment:**
- Bulkhead flatcars (for specialized loads)
- Boxcars (for weather-sensitive or high-value products)

## Pricing & Scheduling

**Rate Adjustments:**
Lumber pricing is updated **twice yearly** (February and August) to reflect market conditions.

**Rate Access:**
Customers obtain pricing via the "Get a Freight Rate" tool at bnsf.com.

**Transit Times:**
Available through BNSF's carload schedule lookup tool for origin-destination pairs.

## Service Features

**Volume Flexibility:**
- Single car shipments for smaller orders
- Unit train services for high-volume customers
- Consistent, reliable delivery schedules

**Dedicated Support:**
- Logistics team with lumber industry expertise
- Well-maintained fleet of specialized centerbeam cars

## Key Markets

**Origin Regions:**
- Pacific Northwest (Washington, Oregon)
- Western Canada (British Columbia, Alberta)
- Northern California timber regions

**Destination Markets:**
- Northeastern US (via Eastern partner roads)
- Southeastern US (via Southern interchanges)
- Midwest distribution centers
- California/Southwest construction markets

## BNSF Lumber Routes (Key Corridors)

**Pacific Northwest to Midwest:**
- Seattle/Portland → Chicago/Minneapolis (via Northern Transcon)
- Serving Midwest lumber distribution centers

**Pacific Northwest to California:**
- Washington/Oregon → Southern California construction markets
- High-volume corridor for housing/construction materials

**Pacific Northwest to Texas/South:**
- Via BNSF Southern routes to Dallas, Houston, Gulf markets
- Growing market due to Southern construction boom

**Cross-Country (Interline):**
- BNSF to Eastern carriers (CSX, NS) at Chicago, Kansas City, Memphis
- Enables service to Atlantic Coast markets

## Notes

The lumber commodity page emphasizes BNSF's unique positioning as the sole Class I railroad with direct access to the Pacific Northwest's timber-producing regions. This geographic monopoly on origin access makes BNSF the dominant carrier for West Coast lumber movements, with most lumber heading East or South moving on BNSF for at least the Western portion of the journey.
