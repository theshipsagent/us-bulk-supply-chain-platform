## Service Overview

BNSF offers cement shipping with "superior service and competitive rates" across North America. The carrier emphasizes efficiency through rail consolidation: **"We can ship four truckloads worth of cement in one railcar,"** reducing highway congestion and emissions.

## Geographic Coverage

**Western United States:** BNSF provides direct access to most major cement production and consumption areas in the Western United States.

**Eastern Markets:** Joint-line access to Eastern Markets extends BNSF's cement distribution reach beyond its primary Western network.

**Route Restrictions:** Cement service is **"provided...on designated routes only."** Customers should verify route availability through BNSF switching guides.

## Equipment Options

BNSF offers three primary railcar types for cement transport:

| Equipment Type | Length | Capacity (lbs) |
|---|---|---|
| **Boxcars** | 50-60 ft | 185,000-215,000 |
| **Covered Hoppers** | Standard | 263,000-286,000 |
| **Insulated Boxcars** | Standard | 164,000-175,000 |

## Service Types

**Single Car Services:**
- Transit times available via BNSF carload schedules
- Standard manifest service for smaller volumes

**Unit Train Service:**
- Available for high-volume shippers
- Requires shipper to own or lease equipment
- Must comply with BNSF Unit Train Operating Guidelines

## Pricing and Tools

- **Rate Quotes:** Accessible through "Get a Freight Rate" tool at bnsf.com
- **Price Authorities:** Cement moves under various published price authorities
- **Service Verification:** Use BNSF switching guides to confirm destination service
- **Transload Options:** Available for locations without direct rail service

## Key Advantages

✓ **Environmental:** 4:1 truck-to-rail consolidation reduces emissions and highway congestion
✓ **Network:** Access to major Western cement production centers and consumption markets
✓ **Flexibility:** Single car or unit train service options
✓ **Equipment:** Multiple car types accommodate different cement products and packaging
