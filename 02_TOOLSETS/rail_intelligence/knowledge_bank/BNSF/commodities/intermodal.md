## Service Overview

BNSF operates **"the largest, fastest intermodal network in the United States"** with strategically positioned hubs for consumer goods distribution. The company positions itself as a supply chain partner providing personalized service and damage-free deliveries.

## Core Services

**Intermodal Container/Trailer Transport:**
BNSF addresses complex supply chain challenges through its "innovation, optionality and capacity." Intermodal services represent a core offering for consumer product movement, competing directly with highway trucking.

**Automotive Products:**
- Finished vehicles (import and domestic)
- Vehicle parts and components
- Transport from manufacturers/ports to dealer networks nationwide
- Emphasis on on-time performance and logistical support

## Network Characteristics

**Size and Speed:**
BNSF claims the **largest** and **fastest** intermodal network in the US, indicating:
- Most extensive facility network
- Fastest transit times between major markets
- Greatest capacity for container/trailer movements

**Strategic Hub Positioning:**
Intermodal terminals located to optimize consumer goods distribution to major metropolitan areas.

## Service Philosophy

BNSF emphasizes three customer-focused elements:
- **Innovation:** Developing new service offerings and operational improvements
- **Optionality:** Multiple routing and service options for customers
- **Capacity:** Ability to scale volumes up/down based on demand

## New Customer Resources

BNSF guides prospects through three fundamental questions:

**What to ship:**
Products of any size via cost-effective rail service, particularly suited for containerized consumer goods.

**Where to ship:**
Network coverage spans BNSF's service territory (28 states + 2 Canadian provinces) with connections to all North American markets.

**How to ship:**
Multiple service options for freight delivery:
- Single container/trailer movements
- Regular dedicated services
- Premium expedited intermodal

## Notes

BNSF's intermodal network is built around their two transcontinental "Transcons":
- **Southern Transcon** (LA–Chicago): Fastest intermodal route, ~40 hours
- **Northern Transcon** (Seattle–Chicago): Pacific NW port alternative

These routes compete directly with Union Pacific's intermodal network and serve as the backbone of BNSF's consumer products business, moving import containers from West Coast ports to inland markets.
