## Overview

BNSF operates an extensive network of facilities including intermodal terminals, logistics parks, certified industrial sites, transload facilities, and team tracks. The company offers an interactive Strategic Programs Map showing Logistics Parks, Logistics Centers, and Certified Sites.

## Intermodal Terminals

BNSF operates major intermodal facilities for container and trailer handling:

### Major Intermodal Terminals

**California:**
- Los Angeles area (multiple terminals)
- Long Beach
- San Bernardino (San Bernardino International Gateway)
- Stockton
- Richmond (Bay Area)

**Pacific Northwest:**
- Seattle
- Tacoma

**Texas:**
- Dallas/Fort Worth (Alliance Texas)
- Houston

**Midwest:**
- Chicago (Cicero, Corwith)
- Kansas City

**Other Key Terminals:**
- Minneapolis/St. Paul
- Memphis
- Denver

## Logistics Parks & Centers

BNSF develops and markets Logistics Parks and Centers for rail-served industrial development. These facilities offer:

- Direct rail access
- Truck-to-rail transload capability
- Warehousing and distribution
- Multimodal connectivity
- Economic development partnerships

**Notable Logistics Parks:**

- **Alliance Texas (Fort Worth):** Premier logistics park, one of the largest inland port facilities in North America
- Additional parks across the network (specific locations available via interactive map)

## Certified Sites

BNSF offers a Certified Sites program for industrial development, providing:
- Pre-qualified rail-served sites
- Infrastructure readiness assessment
- Environmental clearance
- Utility availability
- Development support

## Transload Facilities

BNSF provides transload services for truck-to-rail conversion and rail-to-truck distribution:

### Facility Types:

**Bulk Transload:**
- Grain transload for shuttle train loading
- Coal transload for smaller industrial users
- Aggregate and sand transload
- Cement transload

**Liquid Transload:**
- Chemical transload (tank truck to tank car)
- Petroleum products
- Edible oils

**Packaged Goods Transload:**
- Lumber and building materials
- Palletized goods
- Containerized freight

## Team Tracks

Team tracks provide rail access for locations without direct rail service. BNSF recognizes these facilities as solutions for customers needing occasional or small-volume rail service.

## Specialized Facilities

### Auto Ramps
- Vehicle loading/unloading facilities
- Distribution centers for finished vehicles

### Coal Loading Facilities
- Powder River Basin mine-mouth loading
- Power plant unloading loops (customer-owned)

### Grain Elevators

- Export terminal connections

- Tank car loading/unloading racks

- Tank car cleaning stations

### Forest Products Facilities
- Lumber reload centers
- Panel product distribution centers
- Paper mill rail sidings

### Cement Terminals
- Bulk cement unloading
- Cement distribution centers
- Covered storage facilities

### Aggregate/Sand Facilities
- Unit train loading (frac sand)
- Construction aggregate distribution
- Ballast loading for railroad use

## Yard & Classification Facilities

Major classification yards for freight car sorting and train assembly:

**Major Yards:**
- **Barstow, CA:** Southern Transcon hub
- **Kansas City, MO:** Central network hub
- **Galesburg, IL:** Midwest classification
- **Minneapolis/St. Paul, MN:** Northern hub
- **Alliance, NE:** Coal and manifest yard
- **Fort Worth, TX:** Texas region hub
- **Northtown (Minneapolis), MN:** Large hump yard

## Maintenance Facilities
