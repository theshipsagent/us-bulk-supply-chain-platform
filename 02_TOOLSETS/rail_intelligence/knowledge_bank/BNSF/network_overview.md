# BNSF Railway — Network Overview
**Source:** https://www.bnsf.com
**Fetched:** 2026-02-23
---

## System Summary

- **Headquarters:** Fort Worth, TX
- **Route Miles:** ~32,500 miles
- **Geographic Coverage:** 28 U.S. states + 2 Canadian provinces
- **Parent Company:** Berkshire Hathaway subsidiary
- **Network Type:** Western U.S. Class I railroad

## Service Territory

BNSF operates across North America with coverage in:
- **United States:** 28 states, primarily west of the Mississippi River
- **Canada:** 2 provinces (British Columbia, Manitoba)
- **Mexico:** Cross-border gateway connections
- **Overseas:** West Coast port access for international shipments

## Network Characteristics

- 32,500 miles of rail track
- Largest intermodal railroad (handling over 1 million additional loads annually vs. competitors)
- Approximately 200 short line, regional carrier, and switch carrier connections
- Multi-modal connectivity through ports, team tracks, and transload facilities

**Port Connectivity:**
- West Coast ports for international shipping
- "Best-in-class connectivity" for land-water transportation integration
- Seamless domestic and international freight movement

## Major Commodity Groups Served

1. **Industrial Products:** Chemicals, fertilizers, plastics, construction materials, cement, steel, lumber, aggregates, machinery
2. **Agricultural Products:** Field crops, grain (since 1854), bulk foods, fertilizers
3. **Energy:** Coal, crude oil, LPG, refined products, renewable fuels
4. **Intermodal:** Containers, trailers, LTL, domestic/international
5. **Automotive:** Finished vehicles, vehicle parts
6. **Food & Beverages:** Bulk foods, beverages, canned/boxed, fresh/frozen (temperature-controlled)

## Historical Note

BNSF has been "partnered with American producers for over 170 years" — grain transportation since 1854.
