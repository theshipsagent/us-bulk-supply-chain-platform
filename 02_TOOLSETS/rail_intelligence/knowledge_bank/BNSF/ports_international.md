# BNSF — Ports & International Connections

**Source:** https://www.bnsf.com/ship-with-bnsf/maps-and-shipping-locations/index.page

## Overview

BNSF connects to major ports on the West Coast, Gulf Coast, and California coast, enabling access to international markets. The company states that its port connections allow shippers to reach "100 percent of U.S. markets" via West Coast ports and facilitate overseas shipments of commodities and finished goods.

## Pacific Northwest Ports (Primary Export Gateway)

### Puget Sound Ports (Washington)

**Port of Seattle:**
- Container terminals (Asia trade)
- Grain export terminals
- Forest products export
- Intermodal rail access via BNSF

**Port of Tacoma:**
- Container terminals (major Asia gateway)
- Automobile import/export
- Bulk commodities
- Intermodal rail access via BNSF

**Grain Terminals:**
- Louis Dreyfus (Seattle)
- United Grain (Vancouver, WA)
- Temco (Kalama, WA)
- Columbia Grain (Portland, OR — via trackage)
- Export destinations: Asia (China, Japan, South Korea), Middle East

**Key Commodities:**
- Grain (wheat, corn, soybeans)
- Forest products (lumber, pulp)
- Containers (Asia imports/exports)
- Potash (import from Canada for distribution)
- Automobiles

**Service Features:**
- Northern Transcon direct connection
- Unit train service (grain shuttles)
- Double-stack intermodal
- On-dock rail facilities

## International Gateways

### Canada

BNSF reaches Canadian ports via interchange with Canadian National and Canadian Pacific Kansas City:

**Port of Vancouver, BC:**
- Major Asia gateway (import/export)
- Grain export (Canadian wheat, U.S. grain via interchange)
- Potash export
- Forest products

**Port of Prince Rupert, BC:**
- Container gateway (via CN — fastest North American port to Asia)
- Grain export

**Thunder Bay, ON (Great Lakes):**
- Grain transshipment to Great Lakes vessels
- Via interchange with CN/CPKC

---

### Mexico

BNSF reaches Mexican ports via interchange with Union Pacific and CPKC:

**Pacific Coast:**
- Lazaro Cardenas (via CPKC)
- Manzanillo (via UP/Ferromex)

**Gulf Coast:**
- Veracruz (via UP/Ferromex)
- Altamira (via UP/Ferromex)

## Service Features for Port Connections

- **Unit Train Service:** Grain shuttles to export terminals
- **Double-Stack Intermodal:** Container imports/exports
- **On-Dock Rail:** Direct rail access at major container ports
- **Transload Services:** Conversion between rail and ocean container
- **Port Storage:** Demurrage and container storage

---

**Note:** Specific port terminal names, berth numbers, and facility contact information were not provided on public marketing pages. Port authorities and terminal operators manage individual facilities, while BNSF provides rail transportation services connecting inland origins to port destinations.
