## Overview

BNSF connects with all other Class I railroads and approximately 200 shortline, regional, and switch carriers to provide comprehensive door-to-door service across North America.

## Class I Railroad Interchanges

### Chicago, IL (All Class I Carriers)

**Interchange Partners:** Union Pacific, CSX, Norfolk Southern, Canadian National, Canadian Pacific Kansas City

**Key Features:**
- Largest interchange hub in North America
- Multiple interchange points across Chicago terminal
- Belt Railway of Chicago (BRC) facilitates interchange
- Intermodal connections
- Manifest freight exchange

**BNSF Facilities in Chicago:**
- Cicero Yard
- Corwith Intermodal Facility
- Multiple interchange tracks with each carrier

**Commodities Exchanged:**
- Grain (westbound to BNSF for export, eastbound from BNSF to eastern markets)
- Coal (BNSF PRB coal to eastern utilities via NS/CSX)
- Intermodal (transcontinental traffic)
- Chemicals, automotive, manifest freight

## Shortline & Regional Railroad Connections

BNSF connects with approximately 200 shortline, regional, and switch carriers across its network. These connections provide:

- First-mile/last-mile service to industries
- Access to branch lines sold or leased to shortlines
- Local switching and industrial service
- Feed traffic to BNSF main lines

**Major Shortline Territories:**
- Pacific Northwest (lumber, grain, forest products)
- Great Plains (grain, agriculture)
- Texas/Oklahoma (energy, chemicals, grain)
- California (agriculture, industrial)

---

## Belt Lines & Terminal Railroads

BNSF connects with terminal and belt line railroads that facilitate interchange in major cities:

- **Belt Railway of Chicago (BRC):** Chicago interchange
- **Terminal Railroad Association of St. Louis (TRRA):** St. Louis interchange
- **Dallas, Garland & Northeastern (DGNO):** Dallas area
- **Galveston Railroad (GVSR):** Galveston port access
- **Port Terminal Railroad Association (PTRA):** Houston port access

---

## Reciprocal Switching & Trackage Rights

BNSF has trackage rights and reciprocal switching arrangements with other carriers in certain markets:

- Powder River Basin Joint Line (with UP)
- Pacific Northwest shared trackage (with UP)
- Houston Ship Channel area (various arrangements)
- Kansas City terminal (various arrangements)

---

## International Connections

BNSF reaches international markets through:

- **Pacific Ports:** Seattle, Tacoma, Oakland, Los Angeles, Long Beach (Asia trade)
- **Gulf Ports:** Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi (Gulf/Atlantic trade)
- **Canadian Interchange:** Grain, potash, forest products from Canada
- **Mexico Gateway:** Via UP and CPKC connections (El Paso, Laredo regions)
